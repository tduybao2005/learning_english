## BÀI TẬP 2: Mô tả thói quen với tần suất — 20 phút

Viết 8 câu mô tả thói quen THẬT SỰ của bạn, dùng trạng từ tần suất và các cụm từ đã học:

**Gợi ý:**
- always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never
- every day, once a week, three times a week
- I make it a point to..., I try to..., I've gotten into the habit of...

**Ví dụ:**
1. I always wake up at 6 AM on weekdays.
2. I usually have bread and coffee for breakfast.
3. I make it a point to study English for 2 hours every evening.
4. I rarely skip a study session — maybe once a month.
5. I sometimes pull an all-nighter before important exams.
6. I try to go for a run at least three times a week.
7. I've gotten into the habit of reviewing vocabulary before bed.
8. I never eat junk food late at night.

*(Viết câu của chính bạn, không copy mẫu)*

## BÀI TẬP 3: Đọc hiểu — 20 phút

> "Maintaining a consistent daily routine is one of the most effective strategies for achieving long-term goals. Research suggests that habits — whether positive or negative — are deeply ingrained in our brain's neural pathways and can be very difficult to change once established.
>
> For students, particularly those preparing for competitive exams like IELTS, consistency is key. Setting aside a fixed time each day for study — and sticking to it without fail — has been shown to be far more effective than cramming sessions.
>
> Bao, a second-year engineering student, has developed an impressive routine. He wakes up at 5:45 AM, meditates for 10 minutes, and then spends 45 minutes on English before his morning classes. In the evenings, he reviews the day's vocabulary and does a dictation exercise. He never misses a session, even on weekends. 'Discipline is more important than motivation,' he says. 'Motivation comes and goes, but discipline keeps you going.'"

**Câu hỏi:**
1. According to research, where are habits stored in the body?
2. What strategy is said to be more effective than cramming?
3. What time does Bao wake up?
4. What does he do for the first 10 minutes after waking?
5. How long does he spend on English in the morning?
6. What does he do in the evening?
7. Does he take days off? Quote the text.
8. What is the difference between discipline and motivation, according to Bao?

**Đáp án:**
1. In the brain's neural pathways.
2. Setting aside a fixed time each day and sticking to it.
3. 5:45 AM.
4. He meditates.
5. 45 minutes.
6. He reviews vocabulary and does a dictation exercise.
7. No. "He never misses a session, even on weekends."
8. Motivation comes and goes, but discipline keeps you going.
