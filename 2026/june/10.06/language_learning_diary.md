### 2. Gia đình hiện đại

| Từ | Nghĩa |

| **nuclear family** | gia đình hạt nhân (bố mẹ + con) |

| **single parent** | cha/mẹ đơn thân |
| **stepfather/stepmother** | cha dượng / mẹ kế |
| **stepbrother/stepsister** | anh/em trai cùng cha khác mẹ (hoặc ngược lại) |
| **in-laws** | họ hàng nhà vợ/chồng |
| **mother-in-law** | mẹ vợ / mẹ chồng |
| **only child** | con một |
| **twins** | sinh đôi |

### 3. Cách mô tả gia đình trong IELTS Speaking

**Part 1 — Mô tả gia đình:**
- "I come from a **nuclear family**. There are four of us — my parents, my older brother, and me."
- "My family is **close-knit** — we spend a lot of time together."
- "My father is an engineer and my mother is a teacher."
- "I'm the youngest child in my family."
- "My older brother is five years older than me."

**Tính từ mô tả gia đình:**
- **close-knit**: gắn bó, khăng khít
- **supportive**: hay giúp đỡ, hỗ trợ
- **independent**: tự lập
- **traditional**: truyền thống
- **modern**: hiện đại

### 4. Hội thoại thực tế
> **IELTS Part 1 sample:**
> **Q:** Can you tell me about your family?
> **A:** Sure. I come from a close-knit family of four. My father is a retired military officer and my mother is a nurse. I have one older brother who is currently working as a software engineer in Ho Chi Minh City. My parents live in Da Nang. Although we don't live in the same city, we stay in touch through video calls and visit each other during holidays.
>
> **Q:** Do you have a large or small family?
> **A:** I would say it's a moderately sized family. Beyond my immediate family, I have several uncles, aunts, and cousins on both sides. We often get together during Tet, which I really look forward to every year.
