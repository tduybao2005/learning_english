## SECTION 2: DISCOURSE MARKERS CHO SPEAKING (20 PHRASES)

### Mở đầu câu trả lời:
1. "Well, to be honest..." — Mở đầu tự nhiên, thể hiện tính chân thực
2. "That's an interesting question, actually..." — Dùng để mua thêm thời gian suy nghĩ
3. "Off the top of my head, I'd say..." — Thể hiện sự tự nhiên, không chuẩn bị trước
4. "Now, let me think about that..." — Cho thấy bạn đang suy nghĩ nghiêm túc

### Thêm ý:
5. "Not only that, but..." — Thêm điểm bổ sung mạnh mẽ hơn
6. "What's more..." — Thêm thông tin quan trọng hơn
7. "And on top of that..." — Thêm điểm cộng thêm
8. "Beyond that..." — Mở rộng phạm vi bình luận

### Đối lập / Thừa nhận:
9. "Having said that..." — Thừa nhận quan điểm đối lập sau khi đã nói ý chính
10. "On the flip side..." — Đưa ra mặt đối lập
11. "That said..." — Ngắn gọn hơn của "having said that"
12. "Even so..." — Dù vậy, nhưng...

### Đưa ra ví dụ:
13. "To give you a concrete example..." — Dẫn ví dụ cụ thể
14. "A good example of this would be..." — Ví dụ minh họa
15. "For instance, in my own experience..." — Cá nhân hóa ví dụ

### Kết luận / Tổng kết:
16. "So, all in all..." — Tóm lại, nhìn chung
17. "And that's why..." — Kết luận có lý do
18. "When I think about it overall..." — Xem xét tổng thể
19. "At the end of the day..." — Cuối cùng mà nói
20. "I suppose what I'm trying to say is..." — Tóm gọn lại điều mình muốn nói

## EXERCISE 1: MỞ RỘNG CÂU TRẢ LỜI NGẮN

*Chuyển đổi mỗi câu trả lời 1 câu thành câu trả lời mở rộng 4–5 câu. Áp dụng công thức: Direct answer + Reason + Example + Follow-up thought.*

**1. "Yes, I use technology a lot."**

> **Model extended answer:** "Yes, technology is pretty central to my daily life, I'd say. Practically speaking, I use it constantly for my studies — researching, submitting work, attending online lectures. Beyond academics, I stream music throughout the day and use messaging apps to stay connected with friends and family. What's more, I've been deliberately using technology to improve my English by listening to podcasts during my commute. So it plays both a practical and a developmental role for me."

**2. "I prefer staying at home in my free time."**

> **Model extended answer:** "Well, to be honest, I do tend to prefer staying at home in my free time, especially after a long week of lectures and group projects. There's something genuinely restorative about having my own space — I can read, listen to music, or just do nothing at all without any social obligations. That said, I make a real effort to get out at least a couple of times a week, because I know that social interaction is important for my wellbeing. It's really about balance — I need that home time to recharge, but I'd feel stagnant without going out occasionally."

**3. "My favourite food is phở."**

> **Model extended answer:** "My favourite food is definitely phở, though I should be more specific — I particularly love phở bò from a small family-run shop near my university. What I find so satisfying about it is the complexity of the broth, which takes hours to prepare and develops this incredibly deep, aromatic flavour. I eat it at least twice a week, which probably sounds excessive, but each bowl feels genuinely comforting. What's more, phở is one of those rare dishes that's both delicious and reasonably nutritious — it's a complete meal in itself."

**4. "I went to Ha Long Bay last year."**

> **Model extended answer:** "I visited Ha Long Bay last summer with my university friends, and it was genuinely one of the most memorable travel experiences I've had. The landscape is extraordinary — these dramatic limestone karsts rising out of misty green water — and photographs really don't do it justice. We stayed on a cruise boat overnight, which allowed us to kayak through the smaller caves at dawn when the tourist crowds hadn't arrived yet. That moment — paddling in near silence through an ancient cave with light filtering in from the entrance — was quite extraordinary. And that's why Ha Long Bay will probably stay with me for a very long time."

**5. "I study English at university."**

> **Model extended answer:** "English is part of my university curriculum, yes, though I've actually invested a lot of additional time in self-directed study beyond what the course requires. The university classes focus mainly on grammar and academic writing, which is valuable, but I've found that real fluency comes from immersing yourself in the language — reading novels, watching films, listening to native speakers talk about topics I genuinely find interesting. I've been following several English-language podcasts on business and psychology, which has expanded my vocabulary in ways that a textbook simply couldn't. My goal is to reach a level where I'm genuinely comfortable in professional international contexts."

## EXERCISE 3: THÊM DISCOURSE MARKERS

*Thêm discourse markers phù hợp để các câu trả lời sau nghe tự nhiên và liên kết hơn.*

1. "I like spending time with my friends. I also enjoy reading alone."
   → *Model:* "I like spending time with my friends. **That said**, I also genuinely enjoy reading alone — they satisfy completely different needs."

2. "Vietnam has beautiful nature. There are economic problems."
   → *Model:* "Vietnam has extraordinarily beautiful nature. **On the flip side**, there are real economic challenges that affect many people's daily lives."

3. "Technology is useful. It has negative effects."
   → *Model:* "Technology is undeniably useful in many aspects of life. **Having said that**, it clearly has some negative effects that we're only beginning to understand."

4. "I prefer home cooking. Restaurant food is also good sometimes."
   → *Model:* "I much prefer home cooking for everyday meals. **Even so**, eating at a good restaurant has its own particular pleasures that home cooking can't quite replicate."

5. "I don't watch much television. I watch documentaries."
   → *Model:* "I don't watch much television in the traditional sense. **However**, I do make time for documentaries — **in fact**, they've become one of my primary sources of information."

6. "My hometown is small. It has good food."
   → *Model:* "My hometown is relatively small, which some people find limiting. **What's more**, though, it has an exceptional food culture that puts much larger cities to shame."

7. "Learning English is difficult. It is rewarding."
   → *Model:* "Learning English is undeniably challenging at times. **All in all**, though, I find it deeply rewarding — **and that's why** I continue to invest so much time in it."

8. "I don't have many hobbies. I enjoy running and reading."
   → *Model:* "I wouldn't say I have a huge range of hobbies. **That said**, the ones I do have — running and reading — are activities I genuinely look forward to each week. **Beyond that**, they complement each other well: running clears my head, while reading fills it again."
