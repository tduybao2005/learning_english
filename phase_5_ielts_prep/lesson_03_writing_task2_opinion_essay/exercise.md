## SECTION A: Identify the question type and approach (5 items)

For each IELTS prompt below, identify:
- **(1) Essay type** — Opinion / Discussion / Advantages-Disadvantages / Problem-Solution / Two-part
- **(2) What structure to use** — outline the paragraph plan

---

**Question 1.**
"Technology is making people more isolated rather than more connected. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

- Essay type: ___
- Structure: ___
- Key signal words: "To what extent do you agree or disagree?" → you MUST state a clear position (agree / disagree / partially agree)
- Recommended structure: Intro (position) → Body 1 (main argument for position) → Body 2 (opposing view + rebuttal OR second supporting argument) → Conclusion

---

**Question 2.**
"Some people believe young people should focus on earning money, while others say they should spend time volunteering. Discuss both views and give your opinion."

- Essay type: ___
- Structure: ___
- Key signal words: "Discuss both views AND give your opinion" → you MUST develop BOTH sides AND state which you personally prefer
- Recommended structure: Intro (summary + your position) → Body 1 (View A fully developed) → Body 2 (View B fully developed + your preference) → Conclusion

---

**Question 3.**
"In many countries, obesity rates are rising. What are the causes? What measures could address this?"

- Essay type: ___
- Structure: ___
- Key signal words: "What are the causes? What measures?" → two-part question focusing on causes AND solutions
- Recommended structure: Intro → Body 1 (Causes only) → Body 2 (Solutions only, matching each cause) → Conclusion
- Critical rule: Solutions must directly correspond to causes mentioned

---

**Question 4.**
"Does the internet have more advantages than disadvantages for society? Discuss."

- Essay type: ___
- Structure: ___
- Key signal words: "more advantages than disadvantages?" → you MUST take a position on which side outweighs the other
- Recommended structure: Intro (state your position: advantages outweigh OR disadvantages outweigh) → Body 1 (advantages, argue they are stronger) → Body 2 (disadvantages, but acknowledge they are less significant) → Conclusion

---

**Question 5.**
"Why are more young people choosing to study abroad? Is this a positive or negative development?"

- Essay type: ___
- Structure: ___
- Key signal words: Two separate questions → "Why?" (reasons) AND "Positive or negative?" (evaluation)
- Recommended structure: Intro (paraphrase both questions) → Body 1 (Answer Q1: reasons WHY) → Body 2 (Answer Q2: your evaluation) → Conclusion
- Warning: Do NOT mix the two questions in one paragraph

### Model Answer — Band 7.5

The debate over whether university education should be publicly funded or partially paid for by students has become increasingly contentious in recent decades. While I acknowledge that tuition fees can impose a significant financial burden on many families, I firmly believe that some level of student contribution is both fair and economically necessary.

One compelling argument in favour of tuition fees is that higher education represents a personal investment that yields substantial financial returns for the individual. Research consistently shows that university graduates earn, on average, 60–70% more over their lifetime than those without degrees. Given these considerable personal benefits, it seems reasonable that students should contribute financially to a system from which they stand to gain so much. For instance, countries such as Australia and the United Kingdom operate income-contingent loan systems that allow graduates to repay fees only once they are earning above a certain threshold, ensuring that financial hardship does not prevent access to education.

Admittedly, critics might argue that entirely free university education — as practised in Germany and Norway — promotes greater social equality by removing financial barriers to entry. However, this model requires substantial tax revenue, which can result in reduced funding for other critical public services such as healthcare or primary education. Moreover, when higher education carries no personal cost, some students may fail to take it seriously, potentially leading to higher dropout rates and a misallocation of public resources.

In conclusion, while the ideal of free education is admirable, the most sustainable model is one that combines meaningful government subsidies with a modest student contribution, balancing accessibility with fiscal responsibility.

After writing your essay, check each item:

- [ ] Does each body paragraph begin with a **clear topic sentence** that makes a claim?
- [ ] Did I include a **specific example or evidence** in each body paragraph?
- [ ] Did I use at least **3 different cohesive devices** (e.g., Furthermore / However / As a result / Admittedly)?

## PHẦN H: SPEAKING / DISCUSSION PROMPT

*Trả lời miệng hoặc viết câu trả lời, dùng cấu trúc/từ vựng của bài.*

1. How can a candidate distinguish between a 'partially agree' response and a standard opinion essay in IELTS Task 2?
2. What is the most effective way to incorporate specific evidence into a body paragraph without it feeling forced?
3. Why is it important to maintain a consistent position throughout the entire opinion essay?
4. What are the key differences between a Band 5 and a Band 7 body paragraph in terms of content, language, and structure?

**Model answer (Band 7–8):** One of the most important skills in IELTS opinion essays is maintaining a clear, consistent position from introduction to conclusion. A Band 7+ writer states their thesis explicitly in the introduction — for example, 'I firmly believe that...' or 'I strongly agree that...' — and then develops this position through specific evidence in each body paragraph. A common weakness at Band 5 is presenting two sides equally without making it clear which the writer supports. Even when acknowledging an opposing view, the writer should immediately reaffirm their own position using phrases such as 'Nevertheless' or 'Despite this, it remains the case that'.
