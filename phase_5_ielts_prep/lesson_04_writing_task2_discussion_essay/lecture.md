# BÀI 4: IELTS TASK 2 — CÁC LOẠI BÀI LUẬN KHÁC

**Mục tiêu bài học:**
- Phân biệt chính xác 4 loại bài luận Task 2 còn lại: Discussion, Problem-Solution, Advantages-Disadvantages, Two-part Question
- Nắm vững cấu trúc và ngôn ngữ đặc trưng của từng loại
- Tránh những lỗi phổ biến khiến điểm số giảm xuống Band 5-6

---

## 1. DISCUSSION ESSAY — "Discuss both views and give your opinion"

### 1.1 Nhận diện dạng bài

Signal phrases:
- "Discuss both views and give your opinion."
- "Consider both sides of this argument and reach your own conclusion."
- "Some people believe X, while others think Y. Discuss."

**Keyword:** "BOTH views" — you MUST develop BOTH sides, not just the one you agree with.

---

### 1.2 Những lỗi phổ biến nhất — Band 5-6

**Lỗi 1: Viết như Opinion Essay — chỉ bàn một phía**
Sai: Writing only about the view you agree with and ignoring the other view.
→ Kết quả: Task Achievement drops to Band 5 (fails to address the task).

**Lỗi 2: Không đưa ra quan điểm cá nhân**
Sai: Writing balanced paragraphs about both views but never stating which you find more convincing.
→ Kết quả: Task Achievement Band 6 maximum (incomplete task).

**Lỗi 3: Quan điểm cá nhân xuất hiện không nhất quán**
Sai: Saying "I agree with View A" in the introduction but then writing a conclusion supporting View B.
→ Kết quả: Coherence & Cohesion penalty.

**Lỗi 4: Hai body paragraphs không cân bằng**
Một body paragraph 30 words, một body paragraph 120 words — thiếu sự cân bằng.

**Quy tắc vàng:** Both body paragraphs should be roughly equal in length and depth. Your personal opinion should appear clearly in BOTH the introduction (briefly) AND the conclusion (more fully).

---

### 1.3 Cấu trúc chuẩn — Discussion Essay

| Phần | Nội dung | Độ dài |
|---|---|---|
| Introduction | Background + Thesis (briefly state your position) | 50-60 words |
| Body 1 | Develop View A fully using PEEL | 100-110 words |
| Body 2 | Develop View B fully using PEEL + state your preference briefly | 100-110 words |
| Conclusion | Restate both views → state your final clear position | 40-50 words |

**PEEL Structure:**
- **P**oint — Topic sentence (the claim for this paragraph)
- **E**vidence — Example, data, or illustration
- **E**xplanation — Why/how this evidence supports the point
- **L**ink — Connect back to the overall question or signal transition

---

### 1.4 Introduction Template — Discussion Essay

"The question of whether [topic paraphrase] has generated considerable debate. While proponents of [View A] argue that [brief reason], those who favour [View B] contend that [brief reason]. This essay will examine both perspectives before concluding that [your position stated briefly]."

**Example:**
"The question of whether zoos serve a meaningful purpose in modern society has generated considerable debate. While proponents of zoological parks argue that they play a vital role in the conservation of endangered species, opponents contend that keeping animals in captivity is fundamentally cruel and that wildlife belongs in its natural habitat. This essay will examine both perspectives before concluding that well-regulated zoos provide irreplaceable conservation benefits."

---

### 1.5 Model Essay — Discussion (280 words)

**Topic:** "Some people believe that zoos serve an important conservation role, while others argue they are cruel and animals should live in the wild. Discuss both views and give your opinion."

---

The question of whether zoos serve a meaningful conservation purpose or represent an unacceptable form of animal captivity has generated considerable debate. While supporters of zoological parks argue that they are essential for protecting endangered species, critics contend that confining animals is inherently cruel and counterproductive. This essay will examine both perspectives before concluding that responsible, well-regulated zoos are a necessary tool for conservation.

**[VIEW A — Supports zoos]**
Those who advocate for zoos point to their significant contribution to the preservation of species facing extinction in the wild. *(Topic sentence)* Institutions such as the San Diego Zoo and the Durrell Wildlife Conservation Trust have successfully bred critically endangered animals — including the Arabian oryx and the black-footed ferret — and reintroduced them to their natural habitats. *(Evidence)* Without such controlled breeding programmes, these species might well have disappeared entirely, given the ongoing destruction of natural habitats caused by deforestation and urban expansion. *(Explanation)* Zoos, therefore, serve as a vital safety net for biodiversity.*(Link)*

**[VIEW B + Personal position]**
On the other hand, critics argue that confining wild animals to artificial enclosures causes lasting psychological harm, with many captive animals exhibiting stereotypic behaviours — such as repetitive pacing — that indicate chronic stress. *(Topic sentence)* Documentaries and scientific studies have repeatedly demonstrated that even large enclosures fail to replicate the complexity and freedom of a natural environment. *(Evidence)* While I acknowledge this concern, I would argue that the conservation benefits of modern accredited zoos outweigh the welfare drawbacks, provided that welfare standards are strictly enforced and that captivity serves a clear conservation purpose. *(Personal view + rebuttal)*

In conclusion, although valid concerns exist regarding animal welfare, well-managed zoos remain indispensable partners in the global effort to prevent extinction and educate the public about the natural world. *(Conclusion — restates position)*

---

**Annotation:**
- **TS** = Topic sentence | **EV** = Evidence | **EX** = Explanation | **PV** = Personal view | **RB** = Rebuttal

## 2. PROBLEM-SOLUTION ESSAY — "What are the causes? What solutions can you suggest?"

### 2.1 Nhận diện dạng bài

Signal phrases:
- "What are the main causes of this problem? What solutions can you suggest?"
- "Why has X become a problem? How can it be addressed?"
- "Examine the reasons for X and propose measures to tackle it."

---

### 2.2 Quy tắc vàng — KHÔNG được quên

**Solutions must DIRECTLY address the causes you mentioned.**

Nếu bạn viết: "Cause 1 = sedentary lifestyle / Cause 2 = poor diet"
→ Solution 1 MUST relate to sedentary lifestyle (e.g., promote exercise programmes)
→ Solution 2 MUST relate to poor diet (e.g., tax unhealthy foods)

**Sai lầm phổ biến:** Writing causes about unemployment in Body 1, then in Body 2 writing solutions about pollution. Zero coherence.

---

### 2.3 Cấu trúc chuẩn — Problem-Solution Essay

| Phần | Nội dung | Độ dài |
|---|---|---|
| Introduction | Paraphrase + state essay will examine causes and propose solutions | 40-50 words |
| Body 1 (Causes) | Topic sentence + 2-3 causes + brief explanation of each | 100-110 words |
| Body 2 (Solutions) | Topic sentence + matched solutions (one per cause) | 100-110 words |
| Conclusion | Restate main causes and solutions; forward-looking statement | 40-50 words |

---

### 2.4 Ngôn ngữ đặc trưng

**Cause language:**
- "This problem stems from / arises as a result of..."
- "A key contributing factor is..."
- "The situation can be attributed to..."
- "The problem is largely due to..."
- "The crisis is further exacerbated by / compounded by..."
- "This trend is driven by..."

**Solution language:**
- "This could be addressed by..."
- "The problem can be tackled through..."
- "The situation would be alleviated if..."
- "A viable approach would be to..."
- "Governments should implement / invest in..."
- "One effective measure would be..."
- "This could be mitigated by..."
- "Steps should be taken to..."

---

### 2.5 Introduction Template — Problem-Solution

"[Paraphrase of problem statement]. This essay will examine the primary causes of this issue and propose a number of practical measures that governments and individuals could take to address it."

---

### 2.6 Model Essay — Problem-Solution (280 words)

**Topic:** "In many cities, traffic congestion has become a severe problem. What are the main causes? What solutions can you suggest?"

---

Traffic congestion has reached critical proportions in many of the world's major cities, with knock-on effects on air quality, productivity, and quality of life. This essay will examine the primary causes of this problem and propose several practical measures to reduce it.

**[BODY 1 — Causes]**
Traffic congestion in urban areas stems from several interconnected factors. *(TS)* First and foremost, rapid urbanisation has led to a dramatic increase in the number of private vehicles on city roads, as more people relocate in search of employment, while infrastructure has failed to keep pace with this growth. *(Cause 1)* Compounding this, the inadequacy of public transport networks in many cities forces residents to rely on private cars, since buses and trains are either unreliable, overcrowded, or unavailable in outlying districts. *(Cause 2)* In some cases, outdated road designs featuring bottlenecks at key intersections further impede the flow of traffic. *(Cause 3)*

**[BODY 2 — Solutions]**
Addressing these root causes requires a multi-pronged approach. *(TS)* To reduce private vehicle use, governments could implement congestion charging schemes in city centres, a measure that has demonstrably reduced traffic levels in cities such as London and Stockholm. *(Solution for Cause 1)* Simultaneously, substantial public investment in efficient and affordable transport infrastructure — including metro systems, bus rapid transit corridors, and dedicated cycling lanes — would provide genuine alternatives to driving. *(Solution for Cause 2)* Road design issues, meanwhile, could be tackled through smart traffic management systems that dynamically adjust signal timing based on real-time data. *(Solution for Cause 3)*

In conclusion, congestion is a complex problem that demands coordinated solutions targeting vehicle dependency and public transport provision in equal measure.

## 3. ADVANTAGES-DISADVANTAGES ESSAY

### 3.1 Hai kiểu đề — Phân biệt ngay từ đầu

**Type 1: "Discuss the advantages AND disadvantages"**
→ Balanced essay. You do NOT need to take a clear personal position on which side is stronger.
→ Structure: Intro (no position needed) → Body 1 (Advantages) → Body 2 (Disadvantages) → Conclusion (balanced summary)

**Type 2: "Do the advantages OUTWEIGH the disadvantages?" (hoặc ngược lại)**
→ You MUST take a clear position: YES, advantages outweigh / NO, disadvantages outweigh.
→ Failing to take a position is a Band 5 error.

**Trap — Lỗi phổ biến nhất:**
Thấy "advantages and disadvantages" liền viết balanced essay — nhưng đề hỏi "outweigh" → BAND 5 for Task Achievement.

---

### 3.2 Cấu trúc — Type 2 (Outweigh Question)

| Phần | Nội dung |
|---|---|
| Introduction | Paraphrase + YOUR POSITION stated clearly ("I believe the advantages clearly outweigh...") |
| Body 1 | Advantages (argue they are strong and significant) |
| Body 2 | Disadvantages (acknowledge them, but minimise/rebut them) |
| Conclusion | Restate that advantages outweigh — OR reverse if you believe disadvantages outweigh |

**If you believe disadvantages outweigh:**
- Body 1 = Disadvantages (stronger, more significant)
- Body 2 = Advantages (acknowledge but minimise)

---

### 3.3 Weighing language

- "The benefits far outweigh the drawbacks..."
- "On balance, the advantages are more significant..."
- "While the disadvantages are real, they are ultimately outweighed by..."
- "Although some disadvantages exist, these are relatively minor compared to..."
- "The positive outcomes are more far-reaching and long-lasting than the negative ones."

---

### 3.4 Model Essay — Advantages-Disadvantages Type 2 (280 words)

**Topic:** "Working from home has become increasingly common. Do the advantages of this trend outweigh the disadvantages?"

---

Remote working has become a defining feature of the modern workplace, accelerated by advances in digital technology and the global pandemic. While there are certain drawbacks to this arrangement, I would argue that its advantages clearly outweigh the disadvantages for both employees and employers.

**[BODY 1 — Advantages (stronger side)]**
The most significant benefit of working from home is the substantial improvement in work-life balance it affords employees. *(TS)* Without the need for a daily commute — which, in major cities, can consume two to three hours per day — workers are able to reclaim time for family, exercise, or personal development. Research by Stanford University found that remote workers reported higher levels of job satisfaction and were 13% more productive than their office-based counterparts. *(Evidence)* Furthermore, companies that offer remote working options report lower staff turnover and reduced overhead costs, as smaller office spaces are required. *(Explanation)*

**[BODY 2 — Disadvantages (minimised/rebutted)]**
Critics of remote working argue that it erodes team cohesion and makes collaborative work more difficult, as spontaneous communication and face-to-face interaction are reduced. *(TS — concession)* This concern is understandable; however, modern digital tools such as video conferencing platforms and collaborative project management software have largely mitigated these challenges. *(Rebuttal)* Moreover, companies can adopt hybrid models — combining office attendance with home working — to preserve the social benefits of the workplace while retaining the flexibility that remote arrangements offer. *(Further point)*

In conclusion, while remote working does present some challenges to team dynamics, the gains in productivity, well-being, and efficiency make it a development whose advantages substantially outweigh its drawbacks.

## 4. TWO-PART QUESTION ESSAY — "Why is X happening? Is this positive or negative?"

### 4.1 Nhận diện dạng bài

Signal phrases (two separate, distinct questions):
- "Why is X happening? Is this a positive or negative development?"
- "What are the reasons for X? What are the effects?"
- "Why has X increased? Do you think this is beneficial or harmful?"

**Critical rule: Each body paragraph answers EXACTLY ONE question. Never mix the two.**

---

### 4.2 Cấu trúc chuẩn

| Phần | Nội dung |
|---|---|
| Introduction | Paraphrase the situation + signal that essay will address BOTH questions |
| Body 1 | Answer Question 1 ONLY (reasons / causes / explanations) |
| Body 2 | Answer Question 2 ONLY (your evaluation: positive / negative / mixed) |
| Conclusion | Brief summary of both answers |

---

### 4.3 Lỗi phổ biến nhất

**Lỗi 1:** Discussing "both sides" of the positive/negative question in Body 2 → loses focus; becomes weak
**Lỗi 2:** Answering only one of the two questions and getting a full Band 5 for Task Achievement
**Lỗi 3:** Writing causes AND evaluation in the same paragraph

---

### 4.4 Introduction Template — Two-Part Question

"[Paraphrase of trend]. There are several reasons why this trend has emerged, and whether it constitutes a positive or negative development warrants careful consideration."

---

### 4.5 Model Essay — Two-Part Question (280 words)

**Topic:** "In many countries, people are choosing to have fewer children. Why is this happening? Is this a positive or negative development?"

---

Declining birth rates have become a widespread demographic phenomenon across the developed world and increasingly in emerging economies as well. This essay will explore the primary reasons behind this trend and argue that, on balance, it represents a negative development with significant long-term consequences.

**[BODY 1 — Q1: WHY it is happening]**
Several interconnected factors explain why couples in modern societies are choosing to have fewer children or none at all. *(TS — Q1)* Economically, the rising cost of raising a child — encompassing education, childcare, and housing — has made large families financially prohibitive for many households. *(Reason 1)* Socially, the expansion of educational and professional opportunities for women has led many to prioritise careers over parenthood, or to delay having children until later in life. *(Reason 2)* Additionally, shifting cultural values in urban, post-industrial societies increasingly emphasise individual fulfilment over traditional family structures. *(Reason 3)*

**[BODY 2 — Q2: Is this positive or negative?]**
In my view, this trend is ultimately negative, primarily because of its long-term consequences for economic sustainability. *(TS — Q2)* An ageing population with a shrinking workforce places enormous strain on pension systems and healthcare services, which depend on a large base of working-age taxpayers to remain viable. Japan and several Southern European nations already face this demographic challenge acutely, with governments implementing costly pro-natalist policies in an attempt to reverse the trend. *(Evidence)* While some environmental benefits arise from reduced population pressure, these are insufficient to offset the structural economic difficulties that declining birth rates create. *(Rebuttal of counter-argument)*

In conclusion, falling birth rates result primarily from economic pressures and changing social values, and they represent a concerning trend due to their destabilising effect on national economies.

## 5. PLANNING STRATEGIES — Timing Guide for All Essay Types

### 5.1 The 40-minute breakdown

| Time | Activity |
|---|---|
| 0-5 min | Read question carefully; identify essay TYPE; plan outline |
| 5-35 min | Write essay following the structure |
| 35-40 min | Check word count; proofread for errors |

**If you haven't finished the essay in 35 minutes:** Write a short conclusion rather than leaving it incomplete. An incomplete essay has a hard cap at Band 5 for Task Achievement.

---

### 5.2 Minimum planning checklist (5 minutes)

Before writing a single sentence, confirm:
- [ ] I know which of the 5 essay types this is
- [ ] I know what my position is (for types that require one)
- [ ] I have a main argument for Body 1
- [ ] I have a main argument for Body 2
- [ ] I know what my conclusion will say

---

### 5.3 Common errors by essay type — Quick Reference

| Essay Type | Most common Band 5-6 error |
|---|---|
| Discussion | Both views developed but NO personal opinion stated |
| Discussion | Only ONE view discussed (written like an Opinion essay) |
| Problem-Solution | Solutions don't match the causes mentioned in Body 1 |
| Problem-Solution | Causes and solutions mixed in the same paragraph |
| Adv-Disadv (outweigh) | No clear position taken on which side outweighs |
| Adv-Disadv (outweigh) | New points introduced in the conclusion |
| Two-part | Q1 and Q2 mixed in one paragraph |
| Two-part | Only one of the two questions answered |

---

### 5.4 Essay type identification cheat sheet

| Signal in question | Essay type |
|---|---|
| "To what extent do you agree or disagree?" | Opinion |
| "Discuss both views and give your opinion." | Discussion |
| "What are the causes? What solutions...?" | Problem-Solution |
| "Discuss the advantages and disadvantages." | Adv-Disadv (Type 1 — balanced) |
| "Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?" | Adv-Disadv (Type 2 — position required) |
| Two distinct questions: "Why...? Is this...?" | Two-part Question |

## TÓM TẮT NHANH

- **Nhận diện đúng loại bài là bước quan trọng nhất:** "Discuss both views" ≠ Opinion essay; "Do the advantages outweigh" ≠ "Discuss advantages and disadvantages" — nhầm loại bài là lỗi nghiêm trọng nhất có thể giới hạn điểm ở Band 5
- **Discussion essay — hai quy tắc bắt buộc:** (1) Phát triển cả hai phía cân bằng về độ dài và chiều sâu; (2) Nêu ý kiến cá nhân ngắn gọn trong Introduction và đầy đủ trong Conclusion
- **Problem-Solution — quy tắc vàng tuyệt đối:** Mỗi solution trong Body 2 phải trực tiếp và rõ ràng giải quyết đúng cause tương ứng trong Body 1 — không được viết solution không liên quan
- **Advantages-Disadvantages (outweigh) — lập trường không thể thiếu:** Câu "I believe the advantages clearly outweigh the disadvantages" phải có ngay trong Introduction — không có = Band 5
- **Two-part question — kỷ luật tuyệt đối:** Một body paragraph = đúng một câu hỏi; không bao giờ trộn Q1 và Q2 vào cùng đoạn dù chúng có vẻ liên quan
- **Kế hoạch 5 phút trước khi viết:** Xác định loại bài → xác định lập trường (nếu cần) → phác thảo 2 body points → bắt đầu viết — bỏ qua bước này dẫn đến mất cấu trúc
