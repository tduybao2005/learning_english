**ANSWER KEY — Section A:**

| # | Essay Type | Key Signal Phrase | What You Must Do |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Discussion | "Discuss both views and give your opinion" | Develop BOTH views fully + state your own opinion |
| 2 | Problem-Solution | "What are the main causes? What solutions?" | Body 1 = causes only; Body 2 = matching solutions |
| 3 | Adv-Disadv (outweigh) | "Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?" | MUST take a clear position on which side is stronger |
| 4 | Two-part Question | Two separate questions: "Why?" + "Is this positive or negative?" | Body 1 = reasons only; Body 2 = your evaluation only |
| 5 | Opinion | "To what extent do you agree or disagree?" | State a clear position and argue it consistently |
| 6 | Adv-Disadv (balanced) | "Discuss the advantages AND disadvantages" | Balanced treatment; no need to take a "winner" position |
| 7 | Opinion | "Do you agree or disagree?" | State a clear position; argue for it throughout |
| 8 | Problem-Solution | "What are the causes? What measures?" | Body 1 = causes; Body 2 = matching solutions |

## SECTION D: Full Essay Writing

**Topic:**
"Traffic congestion in urban areas has become one of the most pressing problems of modern life. What are the main causes of this problem? What measures could be taken to reduce it?"

**Essay type:** Problem-Solution

---

### Planning Template — Complete before writing

**Causes (at least 2, ideally 3):**
- Cause 1: ___

**Solutions (must match the causes above, in the same order):**
- Solution for Cause 1: ___
- Solution for Cause 2: ___
- Solution for Cause 3: ___

---

### Write the essay (250-280 words)

Use the four-paragraph structure:
- **Introduction** (40-50 words): Paraphrase problem + signal essay direction
- **Body 1 — Causes** (100-110 words): Topic sentence + 2-3 causes with brief explanations
- **Body 2 — Solutions** (100-110 words): Topic sentence + matched solutions (one per cause)
- **Conclusion** (40-50 words): Restate main points; forward-looking statement

### Model Answer — Band 7.5

The problem of traffic congestion has reached critical proportions in many of the world's major cities, affecting both the economy and citizens' quality of life on a daily basis. This essay will examine the primary causes of this issue and propose several practical measures to address it.

Traffic congestion in urban areas stems from several interconnected factors. First and foremost, rapid urbanisation has led to a dramatic increase in the number of private vehicles on city roads, as more people relocate in search of employment while infrastructure has failed to keep pace with this growth. Furthermore, the inadequacy of public transport networks in many cities discourages residents from using alternatives to private cars, since buses and trains are often unreliable, overcrowded, or simply unavailable in outlying areas. In some regions, poorly designed road layouts featuring bottlenecks at key intersections further exacerbate the problem.

Addressing these root causes requires a coordinated, multi-pronged approach. To reduce private vehicle dependency, governments could implement congestion charging schemes in city centres — a measure that demonstrably reduced traffic levels by 30% in London following its introduction in 2003. Simultaneously, substantial investment in efficient, affordable public transport — including metro systems, bus rapid transit corridors, and dedicated cycling infrastructure — would provide genuinely attractive alternatives to driving. The issue of poor road design, meanwhile, could be tackled through smart traffic management technology that dynamically adjusts signal timings in response to real-time traffic data.

In conclusion, while traffic congestion is a complex challenge rooted in urbanisation and infrastructure deficits, it can be meaningfully reduced through targeted investment and intelligent policy design.

### Self-Assessment Checklist

After writing your essay, verify:

- [ ] Is this clearly a Problem-Solution structure (not causes + random solutions)?
- [ ] Does Body 1 discuss ONLY causes (no solutions leaked in)?
- [ ] Does Body 2 discuss ONLY solutions (one per cause mentioned)?
- [ ] Does each solution DIRECTLY address its corresponding cause?
- [ ] Did I use at least 3 cause language phrases (stems from / attributed to / exacerbated by)?
- [ ] Did I use at least 3 solution language phrases (could be addressed by / one effective measure / can be tackled through)?
- [ ] Is my introduction 40-50 words?
- [ ] Is my essay between 250 and 280 words?
- [ ] Did I avoid copying the question wording into my introduction?
- [ ] Does my conclusion briefly restate causes and solutions without introducing new points?
