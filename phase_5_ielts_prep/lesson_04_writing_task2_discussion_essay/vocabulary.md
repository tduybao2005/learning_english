### PHẦN 1 — FOR DISCUSSION ESSAYS (20 phrases)

#### Presenting View A (one side of the debate)

| Phrase | Essay Type | Usage Notes | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Proponents of this view argue that... | Discussion | Formal; introduces View A | Proponents of this view argue that stricter immigration controls are essential for national security. |
| Those who favour X contend that... | Discussion | Use when naming a specific position | Those who favour free university tuition contend that it promotes equal opportunity. |
| Advocates of X claim that... | Discussion | "Advocates" = supporters, defenders | Advocates of renewable energy claim that the transition away from fossil fuels is both possible and necessary. |
| One school of thought holds that... | Discussion | Formal academic phrasing | One school of thought holds that children learn best through unstructured play. |
| Supporters of this position maintain that... | Discussion | "maintain" = continue to believe firmly | Supporters of this position maintain that capital punishment serves as an effective deterrent. |

#### Presenting View B (the opposing side)

| Phrase | Essay Type | Usage Notes | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| On the other hand,... | Discussion | Classic transition to View B | On the other hand, critics argue that stricter controls restrict cultural exchange. |
| Conversely, those who oppose this view believe... | Discussion | "Conversely" = signals direct contrast | Conversely, those who oppose free tuition believe that students who pay value their education more. |
| An alternative perspective suggests that... | Discussion | Neutral, academic tone | An alternative perspective suggests that technology enhances rather than replaces human connection. |
| Those on the opposing side of the debate argue... | Discussion | Clearly signals shift to View B | Those on the opposing side of the debate argue that zoos cause irreparable psychological harm. |
| Critics of this viewpoint contend that... | Discussion | "critics" = those who challenge the idea | Critics of this viewpoint contend that social media can build communities as well as isolate individuals. |

#### Transitioning and comparing views

| Phrase | Essay Type | Usage Notes | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| While X has its merits, Y presents a compelling counter-argument. | Discussion | Balanced, concessive transition | While the argument for tuition fees has its merits, the case for free education presents a compelling counter-argument. |
| Both perspectives have considerable merit; however,... | Discussion | Acknowledges complexity | Both perspectives have considerable merit; however, I find the evidence for stricter regulation more persuasive. |
| It would be an oversimplification to dismiss either view entirely. | Discussion | Shows nuanced thinking | It would be an oversimplification to dismiss either view entirely, as the debate reflects genuine tensions in modern society. |

#### Stating personal opinion in Discussion essays

| Phrase | Essay Type | Usage Notes | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Having considered both perspectives, I am inclined to believe that... | Discussion | Appears after both views are developed — often in Body 2 or Conclusion | Having considered both perspectives, I am inclined to believe that conservation zoos do more good than harm. |
| On balance, I would argue that... | Discussion/Opinion | "On balance" = after weighing everything | On balance, I would argue that the social benefits of free education outweigh the fiscal drawbacks. |
| While both views have merit, I find the arguments for X more persuasive because... | Discussion | Strong, reasoned personal opinion | While both views have merit, I find the arguments for stricter regulation more persuasive because the evidence of harm is well-documented. |
| My own view is that... though I recognise that... | Discussion | States position while acknowledging complexity | My own view is that remote working benefits employees significantly, though I recognise that it is not without its challenges. |
| This essay will examine both perspectives before concluding that... | Discussion | Introduction template — signals essay direction | This essay will examine both perspectives before concluding that renewable energy investment is a moral and economic necessity. |
| Ultimately, I am of the view that... | Discussion/Opinion | "Ultimately" = at the end of reflection | Ultimately, I am of the view that individual freedom must be balanced with collective responsibility. |
| The weight of evidence, in my view, supports the position that... | Discussion | Suggests evidence-based opinion | The weight of evidence, in my view, supports the position that early childhood education yields significant long-term benefits. |

### PHẦN 2 — FOR PROBLEM-SOLUTION ESSAYS (20 phrases)

#### Describing causes

| Phrase | Essay Type | Usage Notes | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| This problem stems from... | Problem-Solution | Direct causal link | This problem stems from a lack of affordable housing in urban centres. |
| The issue can be attributed to... | Problem-Solution | Formal attribution of cause | The rise in obesity can be attributed to increasingly sedentary lifestyles and poor dietary habits. |
| This trend arises as a result of... | Problem-Solution | Shows consequence relationship | This trend arises as a result of globalisation and the breakdown of traditional communities. |
| The problem is largely due to... | Problem-Solution | "Largely" = emphasises this as the main cause | The problem is largely due to inadequate public investment in infrastructure. |
| The situation is exacerbated by... | Problem-Solution | Means "made worse by" | The situation is exacerbated by a lack of enforcement of environmental regulations. |
| The crisis is further compounded by... | Problem-Solution | Similar to "exacerbated" — adds another worsening factor | The mental health crisis is further compounded by the stigma that prevents many from seeking help. |
| A contributing factor is... | Problem-Solution | Use for a secondary or additional cause | A contributing factor is the widespread availability of cheap processed food. |
| This phenomenon is driven by... | Problem-Solution | "Driven by" = powered or motivated by | This phenomenon is driven by the rapid expansion of social media use among adolescents. |
| The root cause of this problem lies in... | Problem-Solution | Points to the fundamental underlying cause | The root cause of this problem lies in systemic inequality in access to quality education. |
| It is precipitated by... | Problem-Solution | Formal; means "triggered/caused by" | The housing crisis is precipitated by speculative investment in real estate markets. |

#### Proposing solutions

| Phrase | Essay Type | Usage Notes | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| This could be addressed by... | Problem-Solution | General solution phrase | This could be addressed by introducing mandatory nutrition education in schools. |
| The problem can be tackled through... | Problem-Solution | "Tackled" = dealt with directly | The problem can be tackled through a combination of investment and regulatory reform. |
| The situation would be alleviated if... | Problem-Solution | Conditional structure — suggests improvement | The situation would be alleviated if governments invested more heavily in public transport networks. |
| A viable approach would be to... | Problem-Solution | Suggests a practical, workable solution | A viable approach would be to implement progressive taxation on carbon emissions. |
| Governments should implement... | Problem-Solution | Direct recommendation | Governments should implement stricter controls on the advertising of unhealthy foods to children. |
| One effective measure would be... | Problem-Solution | Introduces a specific action | One effective measure would be the introduction of subsidised childcare to reduce the financial burden on families. |
| This could be mitigated by... | Problem-Solution | "Mitigated" = reduced in severity | The impact of unemployment could be mitigated by targeted retraining programmes for displaced workers. |
| Steps should be taken to... | Problem-Solution | Somewhat formal; calls for action | Steps should be taken to increase mental health resources available in schools and workplaces. |
| A comprehensive strategy must include... | Problem-Solution | Emphasises multi-faceted approach | A comprehensive strategy must include both short-term relief measures and long-term systemic reform. |
| Policymakers would be well advised to... | Problem-Solution | Formal recommendation to those in power | Policymakers would be well advised to consult communities directly affected before designing solutions. |

### PHẦN 3 — FOR ADVANTAGES-DISADVANTAGES ESSAYS (20 phrases)

#### Describing advantages

| Phrase | Essay Type | Usage Notes | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| A key benefit is... | Adv-Disadv | Straightforward introduction of advantage | A key benefit of working remotely is the significant reduction in commuting time. |
| A notable advantage is... | Adv-Disadv | "Notable" = worth remarking on | A notable advantage of online learning is its flexibility for working adults. |
| One significant merit of X is... | Adv-Disadv | "Merit" = positive quality | One significant merit of urban living is the proximity to cultural and employment opportunities. |
| This has the potential to... | Adv-Disadv | Introduces a future benefit | Investment in renewable energy has the potential to create millions of new jobs globally. |
| A positive aspect of X is... | Adv-Disadv | Simple, clear phrase | A positive aspect of social media is its ability to connect people across vast distances. |
| One beneficial outcome is... | Adv-Disadv | "Outcome" = result | One beneficial outcome of increased automation is greater efficiency in manufacturing. |
| Among the most compelling advantages is... | Adv-Disadv | Strong opener for the main advantage | Among the most compelling advantages is the environmental benefit of reduced private car use. |

#### Describing disadvantages

| Phrase | Essay Type | Usage Notes | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| A major drawback is... | Adv-Disadv | Introduces a significant disadvantage | A major drawback of social media is its role in spreading misinformation. |
| A significant concern is... | Adv-Disadv | Slightly softer than "drawback" | A significant concern is the potential impact of automation on low-skilled employment. |
| A potential downside is... | Adv-Disadv | "Downside" = informal for disadvantage; still acceptable in IELTS | A potential downside is that students may become over-reliant on technology. |
| This risks... | Adv-Disadv | Compact; introduces a negative consequence | Unlimited screen time risks damaging children's ability to concentrate. |
| A notable disadvantage is... | Adv-Disadv | Mirrors "A notable advantage" — good structural balance | A notable disadvantage is the loss of face-to-face interaction that remote work entails. |
| One concern that critics raise is... | Adv-Disadv | Attributes the concern to critics | One concern that critics raise is the environmental cost of producing electric vehicle batteries. |

#### Weighing and concluding

| Phrase | Essay Type | Usage Notes | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| The benefits far outweigh the drawbacks. | Adv-Disadv (Type 2) | Strong position: advantages win | On the whole, the benefits of globalisation far outweigh the drawbacks for developing economies. |
| The disadvantages ultimately outweigh the advantages. | Adv-Disadv (Type 2) | Strong position: disadvantages win | In my view, the disadvantages of nuclear energy ultimately outweigh the advantages. |
| On balance, the positive effects predominate. | Adv-Disadv (Type 2) | "Predominate" = are greater/stronger | On balance, the positive effects of internet access predominate, particularly in terms of education. |
| While the drawbacks are real, they are outweighed by... | Adv-Disadv (Type 2) | Concessive weighing statement | While the drawbacks are real, they are outweighed by the long-term economic and social gains. |
| The two sides must be carefully balanced before concluding that... | Adv-Disadv (Type 1) | For balanced, Type 1 conclusions | The two sides must be carefully balanced before concluding that the policy is net-beneficial. |
| The scale of the advantages is simply greater than... | Adv-Disadv (Type 2) | Emphatic; use with confidence | The scale of the advantages offered by e-commerce is simply greater than the disruption it causes to traditional retail. |
| It is clear that the merits considerably outstrip the drawbacks. | Adv-Disadv (Type 2) | "Outstrip" = exceed, surpass | It is clear that the merits of urban green spaces considerably outstrip the modest cost of maintaining them. |

### PHẦN 4 — FOR TWO-PART QUESTION ESSAYS (20 phrases)

#### Answering Question 1 (Reasons / Causes / Explanations)

| Phrase | Essay Type | Usage Notes | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| There are several reasons why this trend is emerging. | Two-part | Topic sentence for Q1 body paragraph | There are several reasons why this trend of choosing gap years is emerging among young people. |
| This phenomenon can be explained by a number of factors. | Two-part | "Phenomenon" = observable trend | This phenomenon can be explained by a number of economic, social, and technological factors. |
| A primary reason for this development is... | Two-part | Signals the most important reason | A primary reason for this development is the increased cost of raising children in modern cities. |
| This is partly a consequence of... | Two-part | "Partly" = acknowledges complexity | This is partly a consequence of globalisation and the widespread adoption of urban lifestyles. |
| Contributing to this trend is the fact that... | Two-part | Introduces a supporting reason | Contributing to this trend is the fact that digital nomadism has made location-independent work viable. |
| X can be attributed to a combination of... | Two-part | Suggests multiple factors working together | The decline in birth rates can be attributed to a combination of economic uncertainty and shifting social norms. |
| This is driven in part by... | Two-part | "In part" = one of several causes | This is driven in part by the growing awareness of climate change among younger generations. |

#### Answering Question 2 (Evaluation: Positive or Negative?)

| Phrase | Essay Type | Usage Notes | Example Sentence |
|---|---|---|---|
| Whether this development is positive or negative depends largely on... | Two-part | Nuanced opener for Q2 | Whether this development is positive or negative depends largely on the age at which children begin using digital devices. |
| In my view, this is primarily a positive development because... | Two-part | Clear, direct position | In my view, this is primarily a positive development because it encourages self-reliance and global awareness. |
| I would argue that this trend is largely detrimental because... | Two-part | Clear negative position | I would argue that this trend is largely detrimental because it accelerates income inequality. |
| On reflection, I believe the net effect is [positive/negative] due to... | Two-part | "Net effect" = overall outcome | On reflection, I believe the net effect is negative due to the long-term pressure it places on pension systems. |
| While there are some positive aspects, the overall impact is... | Two-part | Balanced but clear conclusion | While there are some positive aspects, the overall impact of mass tourism is predominantly negative. |
| The implications of this trend are mixed, but on balance... | Two-part | Shows complexity; then takes a stance | The implications of this trend are mixed, but on balance they point towards a more positive outcome. |
| This development is, on the whole, [beneficial/concerning] for... | Two-part | Clean evaluative statement | This development is, on the whole, concerning for countries already facing aging population challenges. |

### Exercise 3: Complete the partial introductions for each essay type (4 items)

Complete each introduction using appropriate vocabulary and structure. Aim for 50-60 words per introduction.

**1. Discussion Essay**
Topic: "Some people think that professional athletes are paid too much. Others believe they deserve their high salaries. Discuss both views and give your opinion."

"The question of whether elite sportspeople deserve their substantial earnings has ___. While ___ argue that ___, those on the opposing side contend that ___. This essay will ___ before concluding that ___."

**Model answer:**
"The question of whether elite sportspeople deserve their substantial earnings has become a topic of considerable debate in recent years. While advocates of high athlete salaries argue that these figures reflect the immense commercial value these individuals generate for their sport, critics contend that such sums are disproportionate and morally indefensible when compared to salaries in socially essential professions. This essay will examine both perspectives before concluding that the market, rather than moral judgement, should determine athlete compensation."

---

**2. Problem-Solution Essay**
Topic: "In many countries, homelessness is a growing social problem. What are the causes of this? What solutions would you propose?"

"Homelessness has emerged as one of the most ___ of contemporary urban life. This essay will ___."

**Model answer:**
"Homelessness has emerged as one of the most pressing and visible social challenges of contemporary urban life, with numbers rising in cities across the developed and developing world alike. This essay will examine the primary factors driving this trend and propose a series of targeted policy measures that governments and communities could adopt to address it effectively."

---

**3. Advantages-Disadvantages (outweigh) Essay**
Topic: "The growth of online shopping has both positive and negative effects on society. Do the advantages outweigh the disadvantages?"

"The rapid expansion of e-commerce has fundamentally ___ the way people shop. While ___. I firmly believe that ___."

**Model answer:**
"The rapid expansion of e-commerce has fundamentally transformed the way people shop for goods and services, with significant implications for businesses, consumers, and communities alike. While concerns about the decline of traditional high-street retail and the environmental cost of delivery logistics are legitimate, I firmly believe that the advantages of online shopping — in terms of convenience, price competition, and accessibility — outweigh its disadvantages."

---

**4. Two-Part Question Essay**
Topic: "Why are more young people choosing gap years before university? Is this a positive or negative trend?"

"An increasing number of young people are ___ before commencing their university studies. This essay will explore ___ and evaluate ___."

**Model answer:**
"An increasing number of young people are choosing to take a year away from formal education before commencing their university studies, a phenomenon that has grown considerably over the past two decades. This essay will explore the primary motivations behind this trend — including a desire for personal development and practical experience — and evaluate whether this represents a broadly positive or negative development for both individuals and society."
