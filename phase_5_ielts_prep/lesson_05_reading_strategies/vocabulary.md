**Hướng dẫn:** Read each sentence and deduce the meaning of the underlined word from context. Do NOT use a dictionary — this simulates the IELTS Reading exam.

**6.** "The building's *deterioration* was painfully evident from the crumbling facade, broken windows, and water-damaged interior."

Meaning from context: _____________________________________________

---

**7.** "The *longitudinal* study tracked the same group of 500 children from the moment of their birth to the age of twenty-five, measuring health, academic, and social outcomes at regular intervals."

Meaning from context: _____________________________________________

---

**8.** "High levels of urban *cohesion* — characterised by strong trust networks, active community organisations, and civic participation — are consistently associated with lower crime rates."

Meaning from context: _____________________________________________

---

**9.** "The new *legislation* made it mandatory for all companies employing more than fifty people to publish annual environmental impact reports."

Meaning from context: _____________________________________________

---

**Answers — Exercise 1:**

1. **indigenous** → native / local / original to that place
2. **replicate** → repeat / reproduce / produce the same result again
3. **deficit** → shortage / negative balance / spending more than received
4. **susceptible** → vulnerable / prone / likely to be affected
5. **catalyst** → trigger / cause / something that speeds up a process or change
6. **deterioration** → worsening / decline / falling into a state of disrepair
7. **longitudinal** → long-term / extended over a long period of time
8. **cohesion** → unity / togetherness / the quality of being bound together as a community
9. **legislation** → law / legal regulation / formal rules passed by government

## EXERCISE 2: True / False / Not Given Practice with Mini-Passage (8 items)

**Đọc đoạn văn sau rồi trả lời các câu hỏi:**

---

**Passage (approx. 180 words):**

"The concept of 'food sovereignty' — the right of peoples to define their own food and agriculture systems — was first articulated by the international peasant movement La Via Campesina in 1996. Distinct from the better-known concept of 'food security', which focuses primarily on the availability and affordability of food, food sovereignty emphasises the cultural and political dimensions of food production and distribution. Proponents argue that the growing corporate control of global agriculture threatens traditional farming practices and the *biodiversity* of crops cultivated over centuries. By 2020, the movement had gained the active support of over 182 organisations operating in 81 countries across six continents. However, critics contend that small-scale farming, however culturally significant, cannot realistically feed an urbanising global population projected to reach ten billion by 2050."

---

**Statements:**

1. Food sovereignty and food security are synonymous terms referring to the same concept.
2. La Via Campesina first introduced the concept of food sovereignty in 1996.
3. The text states that corporate agriculture is definitively responsible for reducing crop biodiversity.
4. The food sovereignty movement has active supporters in more than 80 countries.
5. All academic economists support the food sovereignty movement's goals.
6. The movement was initiated by an organisation representing farming communities.
7. The author of the passage personally endorses the food sovereignty movement.
8. Critics of food sovereignty believe small-scale farming has limitations in feeding the global population.

---

**Answers — Exercise 2:**

1. **FALSE** — The passage clearly distinguishes between the two concepts: food sovereignty concerns the *right to define* food systems; food security concerns *availability and affordability*.
2. **TRUE** — "first articulated by...La Via Campesina in 1996"
3. **FALSE** — The passage says corporate control "threatens" biodiversity, not that it *is responsible for* reducing it. This is a subtle but important distinction.
4. **TRUE** — "over 182 organisations operating in 81 countries" = more than 80 countries
5. **NOT GIVEN** — The passage mentions critics but makes no claim about *all* economists.
6. **TRUE** — "international peasant movement" = organisation representing farming communities
7. **NOT GIVEN** — The passage presents both views but the author's personal opinion is not expressed.
8. **TRUE** — "critics contend that small-scale farming...cannot realistically feed an urbanising global population"

## GHI NHỚ NHANH — Chiến lược học từ vựng cho IELTS Reading

**Nguyên tắc 1 — Đừng học từ đơn lẻ:** Học từ trong cụm (collocations)
- *conduct* research (NOT *do* research in academic context)
- *significant* correlation (NOT *big* correlation)
- *address* a problem (NOT *solve* a problem — different connotation)

**Nguyên tắc 2 — Nhận diện từ loại từ đuôi (suffixes):**
- *-tion / -sion* → noun (correlation, legislation, perception)
- *-ity* → noun (biodiversity, validity, inequality)
- *-al / -ic* → adjective (empirical, chronic, demographic)
- *-ate / -ify* → verb (facilitate, diversify, contaminate)

**Nguyên tắc 3 — Trong IELTS T/F/NG:** Từ trong câu hỏi thường KHÔNG match trực tiếp với từ trong passage — passage dùng từ đồng nghĩa. Ví dụ:
- Câu hỏi: "The policy was *successful*" → Passage: "The initiative *yielded significant results*"
- Câu hỏi: "experts *disagreed*" → Passage: "researchers *contested* the findings"
