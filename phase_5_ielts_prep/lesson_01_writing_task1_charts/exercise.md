## PHẦN 1: VOCABULARY IN CONTEXT (Từ vựng trong ngữ cảnh) — 10 câu

**Hướng dẫn:** Chọn từ đúng trong ngoặc đơn.

1. The line graph (illustrates / narrates) changes in global temperature over 50 years.

2. Coal (accounts / makes) for approximately 35% of total energy production.

3. The rate of unemployment (peaked / maximized) in 2009 at nearly 10%.

4. Tourist numbers (fell / collapsed) (sharply / highly) during the 2020 pandemic.

5. France and Germany had (respectively / separately) 65% and 72% internet penetration.

6. The proportion of female graduates has grown (gradually / rapidly), rising from 40% to 58% (over / during) the past two decades.

7. Oil consumption (remained / stayed) (stable / stably) at around 30% throughout the period.

8. The figure for renewable energy (surged / soared) from just 5% in 2000 to (almost / most) 30% by 2020.

9. (Whereas / Although) car ownership increased in urban areas, it (declined / reduced) in rural regions.

10. The data (reveals / uncovers) a clear correlation between income and internet access, with higher-income households (consistently / constantly) recording higher usage rates.

## PHẦN 2: SENTENCE TRANSFORMATION (Biến đổi câu) — 8 câu

**Hướng dẫn:** Viết lại câu sử dụng từ gợi ý trong ngoặc. Không thay đổi nghĩa.

1. The number went from 20 to 80 — that's four times more.
   → The number **quadrupled** from 20 to 80.

2. In 2005, sales were £40m. In 2010, sales were £20m.
   → By 2010, sales ________ by half compared to 2005 levels. *(halved / fell)*

3. The USA had the highest spending; Japan had the lowest.
   → The USA had **by far** the ________ spending, while Japan recorded the ________. *(highest / lowest)*

4. 30% of energy came from coal. 25% came from gas.
   → Coal ________ the largest share at 30%, ________ by gas at 25%. *(accounted for / followed)*

5. The rise was very quick and happened in a short time.
   → There was a **sharp** rise / The figure ________ rapidly. *(surged / shot up)*

6. Both the UK and Australia saw increases of about 15%.
   → The UK saw a 15% increase; ________, Australia recorded a similar rise. *(likewise / similarly)*

7. In 1990, the figure was 10%. By 2020, the figure was 10%.
   → The figure ________ stable at 10% throughout the 30-year period. *(remained)*

8. The percentage of car owners in city A was higher than in city B.
   → City A had a higher percentage of car owners ________ city B. *(than / compared with)*

## PHẦN 5: ERROR CORRECTION (Sửa lỗi) — 10 câu

**Hướng dẫn:** Tìm và sửa lỗi trong các câu sau (mỗi câu có 1-2 lỗi).

1. The chart shows the percentage of people who uses the internet in three countries.
   → _______________________________________________

2. Overall, there was a dramatically increase in the number of online shoppers.
   → _______________________________________________

3. The USA accounted the largest proportion at 45%.
   → _______________________________________________

4. In 2010, the figures was approximately 30% for both countries.
   → _______________________________________________

5. Coal and natural gas comprised of 60% of total energy production.
   → _______________________________________________

6. The rate risen sharply from 20% in 2000 to 65% in 2020.
   → _______________________________________________

7. France had a higher rate of unemployment compare to Germany.
   → _______________________________________________

8. The proportion of households owning smartphones grow gradually before surged after 2015.
   → _______________________________________________

9. Overall, it is clearly that consumption increased over the period.
   → _______________________________________________

10. The graph illustrates how many people are visiting the museum between 2010 to 2020.
    → _______________________________________________

**ĐÁP ÁN PHẦN 5:**
1. "who use" (present simple, not present perfect)
2. "a dramatic increase" (dramatic là tính từ, không phải trạng từ)
3. "accounted for the largest proportion" (account for = chiếm)
4. "figures were" (subject = figures, plural verb)
5. "comprised 60%" hoặc "made up 60%" (không dùng "comprised of")
6. "rose sharply" (rose là quá khứ đơn của rise, không dùng risen với past simple)
7. "compared with/to Germany" (so sánh với = compared with)
8. "grew gradually before surging" (parallel structure)
9. "it is clear that" (not "clearly")
10. "visited" (past tense) / "between 2010 and 2020" (not "to")

### BÀI TẬP 6A: LINE CHART — Mức độ: Trung bình

**IELTS TASK 1 PROMPT:**

*The line graph below shows the percentage of people aged 15-64 who were in employment in three countries (Brazil, South Korea, and Canada) between 2000 and 2020.*

**Dữ liệu (đọc và mô tả từ bảng này):**

| Năm | Brazil | South Korea | Canada |
|-----|--------|-------------|--------|
| 2000 | 58% | 62% | 72% |
| 2005 | 61% | 63% | 73% |
| 2010 | 65% | 64% | 70% |
| 2015 | 68% | 66% | 74% |
| 2020 | 62% | 69% | 71% |

**Yêu cầu:** Viết ít nhất 150 từ. Thời gian: 20 phút.

**Gợi ý dàn ý:**
- Introduction: Paraphrase đề bài
- Overview: Canada highest overall; South Korea most consistent growth; Brazil fluctuated
- Body 1: Compare starting points in 2000 + changes to 2010
- Body 2: Focus on 2015-2020 changes + end points + notable feature (Brazil's dip in 2020)

**BAND 7 MODEL ANSWER:**

The line graph illustrates employment rates among people aged 15 to 64 in three nations — Brazil, South Korea, and Canada — over a twenty-year period from 2000 to 2020.

Overall, Canada maintained the highest employment rate throughout the period, while South Korea showed the most consistent upward trend. Brazil, by contrast, experienced some fluctuation, particularly in the latter part of the period.

In 2000, Canada had by far the highest employment rate at 72%, with South Korea at 62% and Brazil at the lowest level of 58%. Over the following decade, Brazil's employment grew steadily, rising from 58% to 65% by 2010. South Korea saw more modest growth, edging up from 62% to 64%, while Canada's rate dipped slightly to 70% in 2010 before recovering.

Between 2010 and 2020, South Korea showed the strongest growth among the three countries, climbing from 64% to 69%, while Brazil's figures peaked at 68% in 2015 before falling back to 62% in 2020 — close to its 2000 level. Canada remained relatively stable, ending the period at 71%.

*(Word count: ~175 words | Band: 7)*

**ASSESSMENT NOTES:**
- Task Achievement: ✓ All key trends covered, overview present
- CC: ✓ Clear progression, logical paragraph structure
- LR: ✓ Good variety (maintained, consistent, fluctuated, edging up, recovering)
- GRA: ✓ Mix of simple and complex sentences; past tense used correctly

### BÀI TẬP 6B: PIE CHARTS — Mức độ: Trung bình-Cao

**IELTS TASK 1 PROMPT:**

*The two pie charts below show household expenditure in a typical UK family in 1985 and 2015.*

**Dữ liệu:**

| Hạng mục | 1985 | 2015 |
|----------|------|------|
| Housing | 30% | 35% |
| Food | 25% | 18% |
| Transport | 15% | 20% |
| Entertainment | 10% | 15% |
| Clothing | 12% | 5% |
| Other | 8% | 7% |

**Yêu cầu:** Viết ít nhất 150 từ. Thời gian: 20 phút.

**Gợi ý dàn ý:**
- Introduction: Two pie charts + what they compare + time frame
- Overview: Housing remained largest category and grew; food declined most; entertainment grew
- Body 1: Categories that INCREASED (housing, transport, entertainment)
- Body 2: Categories that DECREASED (food, clothing) + stability of "other"

**BAND 7-8 MODEL ANSWER:**

The two pie charts compare how a typical British household allocated its spending across six categories in 1985 and in 2015.

Overall, housing represented the largest expense in both years and saw further growth over the period, while food and clothing experienced the most notable declines. Entertainment, by contrast, grew considerably in importance over the thirty years.

In 1985, housing was the dominant expenditure at 30%, followed by food at 25%. Transport and clothing accounted for 15% and 12% respectively, with entertainment at 10% and other expenses making up the remaining 8%.

By 2015, housing had increased to 35% of household spending, cementing its position as the single largest category. Transport also grew, rising to 20%, and entertainment more than doubled in relative terms, from 10% to 15%. In contrast, food's share dropped significantly from 25% to just 18%, and clothing saw the most dramatic decline, falling from 12% to only 5% — less than half its 1985 level. The proportion spent on other items showed little change at 7%.

*(Word count: ~180 words | Band: 7-8)*

### BÀI TẬP 6C: BAR CHART — Mức độ: Cao

**IELTS TASK 1 PROMPT:**

*The bar chart below shows the percentage of adults in four age groups who used different types of media (television, internet, newspapers) in a given country in 2019.*

**Dữ liệu:**

| Nhóm tuổi | Television | Internet | Newspapers |
|-----------|-----------|---------|------------|
| 18-24 | 45% | 95% | 10% |
| 25-44 | 65% | 88% | 20% |
| 45-64 | 80% | 70% | 35% |
| 65+ | 92% | 38% | 55% |

**Yêu cầu:** Viết ít nhất 150 từ. Thời gian: 20 phút.

**Gợi ý dàn ý:**
- Introduction: Bar chart + what it shows (media types vs age groups) + when
- Overview: Internet dominant for young; TV dominant for older; newspapers consistently lowest; clear age divide
- Body 1: Internet and TV patterns across age groups
- Body 2: Newspaper patterns + summary of overall age trends

**BAND 7-8 MODEL ANSWER:**

The bar chart presents data on the proportion of adults across four age groups who consumed three forms of media — television, the internet, and newspapers — in a single country in 2019.

Overall, a clear divide is evident between younger and older generations in their media consumption habits. Internet usage was highest among the youngest group and declined with age, while the opposite trend was true for television and, to a lesser extent, newspapers.

Among adults aged 18-24, the internet was by far the most popular medium, used by 95% of this group, compared to only 45% who watched television and a mere 10% who read newspapers. In the 25-44 age bracket, internet use remained high at 88%, while television viewership grew to 65% and newspaper readership doubled to 20%.

A striking reversal in media preferences becomes apparent in older age groups. Among 45-64-year-olds, television overtook the internet (80% vs 70%), while newspaper use rose to 35%. This gap widened considerably for those aged 65 and over, with television watched by 92% of this group and internet use falling sharply to just 38%. Newspaper readership reached its peak at 55% among the oldest age group — more than five times higher than in the 18-24 bracket.
