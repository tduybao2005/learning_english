# BÀI 7: IELTS SPEAKING PHẦN 3 — THẢO LUẬN CHUYÊN SÂU
**Phase 5 IELTS Prep | Mục tiêu: IELTS 6.5–8.0**

---

## 1. TỔNG QUAN PART 3

### Part 3 là gì?

IELTS Speaking Part 3 kéo dài **4–5 phút** và là phần khó nhất, phân biệt rõ nhất giữa Band 6 và Band 7+. Sau khi bạn hoàn thành bài nói Part 2 về một chủ đề cụ thể (ví dụ: "Describe a person who influenced you"), giám khảo sẽ đặt các câu hỏi thảo luận trừu tượng hơn, liên quan đến xã hội và thế giới rộng lớn hơn.

### Thời lượng và số lượng câu hỏi:
- Thời gian: 4–5 phút
- Số câu hỏi: thường 5–8 câu
- Dạng câu hỏi: trừu tượng, xã hội, giả định, so sánh
- Không có chuẩn bị trước — phải trả lời ngay

### Các loại câu hỏi Part 3 thường gặp:

| Dạng câu hỏi | Ví dụ |
|---|---|
| Nguyên nhân / Why | "Why do you think social media has become so popular?" |
| So sánh / Compare | "How has the role of technology changed compared to the past?" |
| Giả định / Hypothetical | "What might happen if governments invested more in renewable energy?" |
| Quan điểm / Opinion | "Do you think governments should regulate social media?" |
| Đánh giá / Evaluate | "Who bears more responsibility — individuals or governments?" |

### Tại sao Part 3 phân biệt Band 6 và Band 7+?

Giám khảo không chỉ nghe *nội dung* — họ đánh giá *cách bạn tư duy và trình bày* trong tiếng Anh:

- **Band 6:** Trả lời ngắn, ít lý do, từ vựng đơn giản, không có hedging (thể hiện sự khiêm tốn trong ý kiến)
- **Band 7:** Có lý do, ví dụ, từ vựng đa dạng, biết thừa nhận ý kiến đối lập
- **Band 8:** Chiều sâu phân tích, liên kết ý tưởng với các khái niệm rộng hơn, hedging tự nhiên, câu trả lời 70–90 giây mà không bị gượng

## 2. SO SÁNH CÁC MỨC ĐỘ (Band 5 vs Band 7 vs Band 8)

**Câu hỏi:** "Why do you think social media has become so important in modern society?"

---

### Band 5 Example (~30 words):

*"Because people use it to communicate. They can see news and talk with friends. It is popular now because everyone has a smartphone."*

**Vấn đề:**
- Câu trả lời quá ngắn (dưới 30 giây)
- Không có lý do sâu — chỉ mô tả, không phân tích
- Từ vựng cơ bản: *use it, popular, has a smartphone*
- Không có hedging, không có liên kết câu (no discourse markers)
- Không thể hiện bất kỳ ý kiến cá nhân có chiều sâu nào

---

### Band 7 Example (~90 words):

*"Well, I think there are several reasons for this. Firstly, social media gives people a platform to connect regardless of physical distance, which is increasingly valuable in our globalised world. Moreover, it democratises information — anyone can share news or opinions without needing traditional media gatekeepers. However, I'd also say that the addictive design of these platforms has played a significant role. Tech companies have deliberately engineered features like infinite scrolling and notification systems that keep users engaged for longer periods than they might otherwise intend."*

**Điểm mạnh:**
- Có cấu trúc rõ (Firstly / Moreover / However)
- Lý do cụ thể với ví dụ (gatekeepers, infinite scrolling)
- Từ vựng đa dạng: *democratises, engineered, deliberately*
- Có một luận điểm đối lập (addictive design)
- Thể hiện hedging: *I think, I'd also say*

---

### Band 8 Example (~120 words):

*"That's a fascinating question. I think social media's rise reflects something quite fundamental about human psychology — our deep-seated need for connection, social validation, and a sense of belonging. What's particularly interesting is the way platforms have been architecturally designed to exploit cognitive biases: the variable reward system of 'likes', for instance, functions in a way that is remarkably similar to the mechanics of a slot machine. Beyond the psychological dimension, there's a structural argument worth making: in societies where traditional community bonds have weakened — through urbanisation, career mobility, and the fragmentation of family units — social media fills a genuine social vacuum. Whether this digital substitution is an adequate replacement for face-to-face community is, of course, a deeply contested question."*

**Điểm mạnh:**
- Mở đầu tự nhiên: *"That's a fascinating question"*
- Liên kết với khái niệm rộng hơn: tâm lý học (cognitive biases), xã hội học (urbanisation)
- Phép so sánh bất ngờ: slot machine mechanics
- Hedging tinh tế: *"worth making", "I think", "of course"*
- Kết thúc mở: gợi ra câu hỏi, không đưa ra kết luận quá đơn giản
- Độ dài phù hợp: ~90 giây khi nói tự nhiên

**Những điểm khác biệt giữa Band 7 và Band 8:**
- Band 7 đưa ra lý do + ví dụ cụ thể
- Band 8 kết nối ý tưởng với các khái niệm học thuật, tâm lý học, xã hội học
- Band 8 có độ sâu phân tích tự nhiên, không bị cố gắng
- Band 8 sử dụng hedging phức tạp hơn và linh hoạt hơn

## 3. EXTENDED ANSWER FRAMEWORK — 5 BƯỚC

Sử dụng công thức này để trả lời mọi câu hỏi Part 3 trong 60–90 giây:

---

**Bước 1 — SIGNAL (3–5 giây):**
Mở đầu tự nhiên — tạo thời gian suy nghĩ đồng thời không im lặng:
- *"Well, that's quite a complex issue to unpack..."*
- *"That's a fascinating question, actually..."*
- *"Hmm, it's difficult to give a simple answer to that, but..."*
- *"That's something I find quite interesting to think about..."*

*Lưu ý: KHÔNG bắt đầu bằng "Yes, I think..." hoặc "No, I don't think..." — quá ngắn và không tự nhiên.*

---

**Bước 2 — POSITION (1 câu):**
Nêu rõ quan điểm của bạn ngay sau khi mở đầu:
- *"On balance, I think [your view]..."*
- *"I'd argue that..."*
- *"My feeling is that..."*

---

**Bước 3 — ELABORATE 1 (2–3 câu):**
Lý do thứ nhất + ví dụ hoặc bằng chứng cụ thể:
- *"The main reason I think this is... For example / Consider the case of..."*
- *"This is evidenced by..."*
- *"A clear illustration of this is..."*

---

**Bước 4 — ELABORATE 2 hoặc CONCEDE (2–3 câu):**
Hoặc đưa ra lý do thứ hai, hoặc thừa nhận ý kiến đối lập:
- *"At the same time, I can see why some people would argue that..."*
- *"However, there's a counterargument worth considering..."*
- *"That said, I'd also want to acknowledge that..."*

---

**Bước 5 — CONCLUDE (1 câu):**
Tóm tắt quan điểm ngắn gọn:
- *"So, on balance, I think..."*
- *"Ultimately, I believe..."*
- *"Taking everything into account, my overall view is that..."*

---

**Ví dụ áp dụng 5 bước:**

Q: "Should governments limit the use of social media?"

Signal: *"That's quite a contentious issue, actually..."*
Position: *"...and I'm somewhat sceptical of heavy-handed government regulation."*
Elaborate 1: *"The core problem is that once governments have the power to restrict online speech, that power can easily be misused. We've seen this in authoritarian contexts where 'harmful content' laws have been used to silence political opposition."*
Concede: *"That said, I recognise that completely unregulated platforms can cause genuine harm, particularly to young people."*
Conclude: *"So on balance, I'd favour targeted, transparent regulation of specific harms — such as child safety — rather than broad content control."*

## 4. LANGUAGE TOOLKIT — 60 PHRASES QUAN TRỌNG

---

### Nhóm 1: Expressing Uncertainty & Hedging (15 phrases)

*Dùng khi không chắc chắn hoặc muốn thể hiện ý kiến cẩn thận — đây là dấu hiệu của Band 7+*

1. "It's difficult to say with certainty, but my feeling is that..."
2. "I'm not entirely sure, but I would venture to suggest that..."
3. "This is quite a contentious issue, but I'd argue that..."
4. "It depends to a large extent on the context..."
5. "I'm somewhat sceptical of this view because..."
6. "I'd be inclined to say that... though I acknowledge that..."
7. "One could argue either way, but on balance..."
8. "The evidence seems to point towards..."
9. "Whether this is entirely accurate is debatable, but..."
10. "I may be wrong about this, but my instinct is that..."
11. "It's hard to generalise, of course, but broadly speaking..."
12. "I have mixed feelings about this, to be honest."
13. "That's not something I feel completely certain about, but..."
14. "The picture is more nuanced than it might first appear..."
15. "My overall sense is that... though I'm open to other interpretations."

---

### Nhóm 2: Speculating about the Future / Hypothetical (10 phrases)

*Dùng cho các câu hỏi "What might happen if...", "In the future...", "What do you predict..."*

1. "I imagine that in the coming decades, we might see..."
2. "Were this trend to continue, we might reasonably expect..."
3. "There's a real possibility that..."
4. "It seems entirely plausible that..."
5. "One scenario is that... though another is equally possible."
6. "It strikes me that in the long term..."
7. "I suspect that future generations will..."
8. "If nothing changes, I would predict that..."
9. "Hypothetically speaking, if [X] were to happen..."
10. "Should this become widespread, the consequences could be..."

---

### Nhóm 3: Evaluating Complexity (10 phrases)

*Dùng khi muốn thể hiện tư duy phân tích — dấu hiệu quan trọng của Band 7+*

1. "The picture is considerably more nuanced than it might initially appear..."
2. "It would be an oversimplification to suggest that..."
3. "It's worth distinguishing between [X] and [Y], because..."
4. "The key factor seems to be..."
5. "What makes this particularly complex is..."
6. "This is fundamentally a question of [values / priorities / trade-offs]..."
7. "The issue is genuinely multifaceted and depends on..."
8. "At its core, this debate is really about..."
9. "There are genuinely competing perspectives here..."
10. "The answer varies significantly depending on who you ask and why..."

---

### Nhóm 4: Developing an Argument (10 phrases)

*Dùng để mở rộng và phát triển luận điểm — giữ câu trả lời đủ dài*

1. "Building on that point, I'd also say that..."
2. "What's more, there's a broader dimension to consider..."
3. "Furthermore, and perhaps more importantly..."
4. "To illustrate this point, consider the case of..."
5. "A case in point is..."
6. "This is evidenced by the fact that..."
7. "Consider, for example, what happened in [country / context / study]..."
8. "Research seems to suggest that..."
9. "Anecdotally, I've noticed that..."
10. "This connects to the broader issue of..."

---

### Nhóm 5: Comparing and Contrasting (7 phrases)

*Dùng cho câu hỏi so sánh: "How has X changed?" / "Is X better than Y?"*

1. "In contrast to the situation in the past..."
2. "Whereas in previous generations... nowadays..."
3. "While some would argue that... others contend that..."
4. "Compared to [developing countries / younger generations]..."
5. "The key difference between [X] and [Y] is..."
6. "Unlike [X], which [Y]..."
7. "On the one hand... but on the other hand..."

---

### Nhóm 6: Concluding Part 3 Answers (8 phrases)

*Dùng để kết thúc câu trả lời — đừng để câu trả lời "chết đứng" không có kết*

1. "So, all things considered, I think..."
2. "On balance, I would say that..."
3. "To summarise my view briefly..."
4. "Ultimately, I believe that..."
5. "At the end of the day, the most important factor is..."
6. "Taking everything into account, I'd conclude that..."
7. "My overall position, then, is that..."
8. "So I'd have to conclude that... though I recognise this is an open question."

## 5. MODEL EXTENDED ANSWERS — 8 CHỦ ĐỀ

---

### Topic 1: Technology & Society

**Q:** "How do you think artificial intelligence will change employment in the future?"

*"That's a question I find genuinely thought-provoking. I think AI will have a profoundly disruptive effect on the labour market, though not uniformly across all sectors. The most significant impact will probably be felt in roles that involve routine, repetitive tasks — data processing, basic customer service, certain manufacturing functions — all of which are highly susceptible to automation. However, I'd be inclined to say that this narrative of 'AI replacing humans' is somewhat oversimplified. History suggests that technological transitions typically displace certain roles while generating entirely new ones that didn't previously exist. The real challenge, I think, is ensuring that the transition is managed equitably — that workers displaced by AI have access to retraining and that the economic gains from automation are distributed broadly, rather than accruing exclusively to technology owners. On balance, AI could be enormously liberating, or deeply destabilising — the outcome will depend heavily on policy choices."*

---

### Topic 2: Education

**Q:** "Do you think the purpose of education has changed compared to previous generations?"

*"That's quite a complex question, and I'd say the answer is both yes and no. Historically, formal education was primarily conceived as a means of transmitting knowledge and preparing citizens for a stable, predictable economy. The model of sitting in rows, memorising facts, and passing examinations was fit for an industrial era where most jobs required a specific, teachable set of skills. However, I think we're witnessing a fundamental rethinking of what education is for. In a world where information is instantly accessible through a smartphone, the ability to memorise facts matters far less than the ability to evaluate, synthesise, and critically apply information. What I find encouraging is the growing emphasis on creativity, collaboration, and adaptability — skills that are genuinely harder to automate. Whether educational systems are actually delivering this shift in practice is, admittedly, a different and considerably more complicated question."*

---

### Topic 3: Environment

**Q:** "Who bears more responsibility for addressing climate change — individuals, corporations, or governments?"

*"It's difficult to give a simple answer to this, but I'm instinctively drawn to the view that governments bear the greatest systemic responsibility, while recognising that all three actors have meaningful roles. My reasoning is this: individual behaviour changes, however laudable, are structurally insufficient. If I choose to cycle to work, that makes a negligible difference to global emissions when entire industries are essentially ungoverned. Similarly, corporations will rarely choose to decarbonise voluntarily if doing so undermines their competitive position. Governments, by contrast, have the power to set regulatory frameworks, price carbon, subsidise clean alternatives, and fundamentally reshape the incentive structures that individuals and corporations operate within. That said, I don't think this entirely absolves individuals of responsibility — consumer demand does drive corporate behaviour, and there's an important argument about democratic accountability: governments ultimately respond to what voters prioritise. So it's genuinely circular, but structurally, I'd place most responsibility with governments."*

---

### Topic 4: Society & Globalisation

**Q:** "In what ways is globalisation changing local cultures?"

*"I think globalisation's cultural effects are genuinely double-edged, and I'd resist the temptation to characterise them as simply positive or negative. On one hand, there's an undeniable homogenising tendency: the global spread of American popular culture, fast food, English as a lingua franca, and Western consumer aesthetics has undoubtedly eroded certain aspects of local distinctiveness. Walk into a shopping centre in Ho Chi Minh City, Seoul, or São Paulo, and the brands, the architecture, and the music are often remarkably similar. However — and this is what I find particularly interesting — globalisation simultaneously creates the conditions for cultural revival and assertion. When a minority culture is threatened, there often emerges a countervailing movement to preserve and celebrate it. The internet, paradoxically, has allowed indigenous languages and traditions to reach global audiences and find new advocates. So I'd say globalisation simultaneously homogenises and diversifies — the outcome depends enormously on policy choices and community agency."*

---

### Topic 5: Health

**Q:** "Why do you think mental health problems are increasing in many countries?"

*"That's something I think about quite a bit, actually. I'm inclined to believe that several structural forces are converging. Firstly, social media has created unprecedented pressure around self-image, social comparison, and the performance of happiness — particularly for younger generations who have grown up entirely within these digital environments. The evidence on this is not definitive, but it's suggestive. Beyond social media, there's a broader issue of social fragmentation: in many countries, traditional support networks — extended families, religious communities, stable employment, geographically rooted friendships — have weakened. When those structures dissolve, individuals are left to navigate uncertainty more or less alone, which is psychologically costly. I'd also note, to be fair, that part of the apparent increase may reflect greater awareness and reduced stigma, meaning people are more willing to seek diagnoses that were previously unacknowledged. But I don't think that entirely accounts for the trends we're seeing."*

---

### Topic 6: Media

**Q:** "Should social media companies be held responsible for the content users post?"

*"This is genuinely one of the most contested regulatory questions of our era, and I find myself with genuinely mixed views. The argument for holding platforms responsible is intuitive: if a company profits from content — through advertising, through engagement metrics — it seems reasonable to expect it to take responsibility for the harms that content generates. The legal immunity that platforms have historically enjoyed, particularly in the United States under Section 230, was designed for a different internet era when platforms were relatively passive conduits. However, I'm somewhat wary of the implications of full platform liability. If platforms face legal consequences for every piece of harmful user-generated content, the rational corporate response is aggressive, algorithmic censorship — which creates its own serious risks to free expression and diverse political discourse. So I'd argue for a more targeted approach: platforms should face liability for *systematic failures* to act against known harms — child safety, coordinated disinformation, incitement to violence — rather than being held responsible for the behaviour of every individual user."*

---

### Topic 7: Work & Economy

**Q:** "How important do you think it is for people to have a sense of purpose in their work?"

*"I think this is actually a profoundly important question that economics has historically undervalued. The dominant framework in labour economics treats work as a transaction — you exchange time for money — and assumes that satisfaction is simply a function of compensation. But decades of psychological research challenge this quite fundamentally. What studies consistently show is that a sense of meaning and contribution in work is a stronger predictor of both job performance and personal wellbeing than salary, once basic financial needs are met. This connects to what psychologists call intrinsic motivation — engagement that comes from the work itself, rather than external rewards. I think the implications of this are quite significant for how organisations are structured and managed. Companies that invest in giving employees a sense of genuine agency and purpose tend to see lower turnover, higher creativity, and better outcomes. Conversely, work that is experienced as meaningless — regardless of pay — is associated with higher rates of depression and disengagement."*

---

### Topic 8: Government & Society

**Q:** "Should wealthy nations be obligated to help developing nations address climate change?"

*"I think the ethical case for this is actually quite compelling, even if the political reality is more complicated. The fundamental argument rests on historical responsibility: the countries that are now most vulnerable to the effects of climate change — low-lying Pacific island nations, sub-Saharan African countries, Bangladesh — have contributed negligibly to the cumulative carbon emissions that created the problem. The industrialised nations that drove the bulk of historical emissions accumulated their wealth partly through activities that imposed costs on the rest of the world. That seems, to me, to generate a genuine obligation — not simply a matter of charity or generosity. That said, I recognise that 'obligation' is a strong word politically, and that domestic political constraints in wealthy nations make large-scale climate finance genuinely difficult. What I'd argue is that framing this as a legal or ethical obligation might actually be less effective than framing it as enlightened self-interest: climate instability in developing nations drives migration, conflict, and economic disruption that wealthy nations cannot insulate themselves from. On balance, I think the case for substantial climate finance is overwhelming on both ethical and strategic grounds."*

## TÓM TẮT NHANH

- **5 bước trả lời Part 3:** Signal (3–5 giây tạo thời gian suy nghĩ) → Position (1 câu nêu lập trường) → Elaborate 1 (lý do chính + ví dụ cụ thể) → Elaborate 2 hoặc Concede (lý do 2 hoặc thừa nhận ý đối lập) → Conclude (1 câu tóm tắt)
- **Độ dài mục tiêu:** 60–90 giây mỗi câu trả lời; dưới 30 giây = dấu hiệu rõ ràng của Band 5–6; trên 2 phút = dài quá và mất tự nhiên
- **Hedging là bắt buộc để đạt Band 7+:** "I'd argue that...", "I'm somewhat sceptical of...", "I'd be inclined to say...", "The picture is more nuanced than..." — thể hiện tư duy cẩn thận và phân tích
- **Câu hỏi giả định — dùng conditional và speculative language:** "Were this to happen...", "Should governments invest...", "If nothing changes, I would predict that...", "It seems entirely plausible that..." — không được trả lời "I don't know"
- **Ví dụ cụ thể mỗi câu trả lời:** Đặt tên quốc gia, tổ chức, chính sách, hoặc nghiên cứu cụ thể — "Countries such as...", "Research suggests...", "A case in point is..." — tránh "some people", "some countries"
- **Thừa nhận ý đối lập:** Dùng "Admittedly...", "That said...", "I recognise that..." để tạo chiều sâu — không cần đồng ý nhưng cần thừa nhận sự phức tạp của vấn đề
