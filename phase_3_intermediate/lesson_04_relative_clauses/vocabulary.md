### Exercise 1: Join the two sentences using an appropriate relative pronoun (who / which / whose / where / when / that) (15 items)

*Nối hai câu thành một câu bằng mệnh đề quan hệ phù hợp.*

1. The scientist published a groundbreaking paper. She works at MIT.
→ _______________________________________________

2. I grew up in a small coastal town. The fishing industry dominated life there.
→ _______________________________________________

3. The treaty was signed in 1783. It ended the American Revolutionary War.
→ _______________________________________________

4. The museum has a new director. Her background is in contemporary art.
→ _______________________________________________

5. We visited a village. Its residents had maintained their ancient customs for centuries.
→ _______________________________________________

6. The report was published last week. It raised serious concerns about air quality.
→ _______________________________________________

7. She met a diplomat at the reception. She had corresponded with him for years.
→ _______________________________________________

8. The 1960s was a turbulent decade. Enormous social changes took place then.
→ _______________________________________________

9. The company recently went bankrupt. Its shares had been worth millions just a year ago.
→ _______________________________________________

10. I finally visited the library. I had always wanted to study there.
→ _______________________________________________

11. The legislation was passed by a narrow margin. It has already transformed the healthcare system.
→ _______________________________________________

12. The building was constructed in the 1890s. It has been converted into luxury apartments now.
→ _______________________________________________

13. The professor gave a two-hour lecture. All the students admire him greatly.
→ _______________________________________________

14. The manuscript was discovered in a monastery in Tibet. It dates back over 700 years.
→ _______________________________________________

15. The city was almost entirely destroyed during the war. I was born there.
→ _______________________________________________

**→ Model Answers:**
1. The scientist **who works at MIT** published a groundbreaking paper. *(or: ...the scientist, **who works at MIT**, ...)*
2. I grew up in a small coastal town **where the fishing industry dominated life**.
3. The treaty **that/which was signed in 1783** ended the American Revolutionary War.
4. The museum has a new director **whose background is in contemporary art**.
5. We visited a village **whose residents had maintained their ancient customs** for centuries.
6. The report **(that/which) they published last week** raised serious concerns about air quality.
7. She met a diplomat **whom/who she had corresponded with for years** at the reception. *(formal: ...a diplomat **with whom she had corresponded for years**...)*
8. The 1960s was a turbulent decade **when enormous social changes took place**.
9. The company, **whose shares had been worth millions just a year ago**, recently went bankrupt.
10. I finally visited the library **where I had always wanted to study**.
11. The legislation, **which was passed by a narrow margin**, has already transformed the healthcare system.
12. The building, **which was constructed in the 1890s**, has been converted into luxury apartments.
13. The professor, **whom all the students admire greatly**, gave a two-hour lecture.
14. The manuscript, **which dates back over 700 years**, was discovered in a monastery in Tibet.
15. The city **where I was born** was almost entirely destroyed during the war.

### Exercise 2: Add commas OR remove unnecessary commas to fix each sentence (10 items)

*Câu nào đang thiếu dấu phẩy (cần thêm)? Câu nào có dấu phẩy nhưng không cần (phải bỏ)? Viết lại câu đúng.*

1. My father who is a retired architect designed our family home himself.
2. The Mekong River which flows through six countries eventually reaches the sea in southern Vietnam.
3. People, who study a second language, tend to show improved cognitive abilities.
4. The novel, that she wrote in three months, became an unexpected bestseller.
5. Students who fail to submit their essays on time will lose 10% of their grade.
6. Her eldest son, who lives in Canada, is a cardiologist.
7. The technology, that powers electric vehicles, has improved enormously in the last decade.
8. The town where the incident occurred has since been evacuated.
9. My best friend who I have known since primary school is getting married next year.
10. Shakespeare, who lived from 1564 to 1616, wrote 37 plays and 154 sonnets.

**→ Answers:**
1. My father**,** who is a retired architect**,** designed our family home himself. *(non-defining — only one father)*
2. Correct as written. *(non-defining — The Mekong River is already uniquely identified)*
3. People who study a second language tend to show improved cognitive abilities. *(Remove commas — defining: only those people)*
4. The novel **that** she wrote in three months became an unexpected bestseller. *(Remove comma + change "that" — defining, or: "The novel**,** which she wrote in three months**,** became...")*
5. Correct as written. *(defining — specifying which students)*
6. Correct as written. *(non-defining — one specific eldest son)*
7. The technology that powers electric vehicles has improved enormously. *(Remove commas + comma before "that" wrong — defining)*
8. Correct as written. *(defining — where clause specifies which town)*
9. My best friend**,** who I have known since primary school**,** is getting married next year. *(non-defining — only one best friend)*
10. Correct as written. *(non-defining — Shakespeare uniquely identified)*

### Exercise 3: Reduce the relative clause to a participial phrase where possible (10 items)

*Rút gọn mệnh đề quan hệ thành cụm phân từ (V-ing hoặc V3). Nếu không thể rút gọn, giải thích tại sao.*

1. The woman who is sitting at the front desk can help you with your query.
→ _______________________________________________

2. The report that was submitted by the committee last week contained several factual errors.
→ _______________________________________________

3. All students who are applying for the scholarship must submit three references.
→ _______________________________________________

4. The cathedral, which was built in the 12th century, is the oldest building in the city.
→ _______________________________________________

5. The delegates who attended the summit reached a historic agreement on climate change.
→ _______________________________________________

6. The policy, which was introduced in 2020, has already produced measurable results.
→ _______________________________________________

7. The children who live in that neighbourhood attend the local primary school.
→ _______________________________________________

8. The book that I borrowed from the library is about the history of the Roman Empire.
→ _______________________________________________

9. The scientists who are conducting the experiment expect to publish results by year-end.
→ _______________________________________________

10. The monument that stands in the centre of the square was erected in memory of the war dead.
→ _______________________________________________

**→ Answers:**
1. The woman **sitting** at the front desk can help you. *(active → V-ing)*
2. The report **submitted** by the committee last week contained several factual errors. *(passive → V3)*
3. All students **applying** for the scholarship must submit three references. *(active → V-ing)*
4. The cathedral, **built** in the 12th century, is the oldest building in the city. *(passive → V3)*
5. The delegates **attending** the summit reached a historic agreement. *(active → V-ing)*
6. The policy, **introduced** in 2020, has already produced measurable results. *(passive → V3)*
7. The children **living** in that neighbourhood attend the local primary school. *(active → V-ing)*
8. **Cannot reduce** — "that I borrowed" → pronoun is the OBJECT, not the subject; cannot form participial phrase naturally here. *(The relative pronoun is an object, not a subject.)*
9. The scientists **conducting** the experiment expect to publish results by year-end. *(active → V-ing)*
10. The monument **standing** in the centre of the square was erected in memory of the war dead. *(active → V-ing)*
