## SECTION A: FILL IN THE BLANK (12 câu — 12 điểm)

**Hướng dẫn:** Điền một từ phù hợp vào mỗi chỗ trống để tạo collocation đúng.

1. Governments must _______ decisive action to address the growing problem of youth unemployment.

2. The study _______ considerable damage to the company's reputation when its findings were made public.

3. Scientists have _______ extensive research into the long-term effects of air pollution on human health.

4. The new environmental legislation will come into _______ at the beginning of next year.

5. There is _______ concern among economists about the long-term consequences of rising national debt.

6. Rising energy costs _______ rise to widespread public discontent in many European countries.

7. The government needs to _______ priority to improving access to mental healthcare services.

8. Urban sprawl has a _______ impact on local biodiversity, reducing habitats for native species.

9. It is now _______ accepted that a balanced diet plays a critical role in preventing chronic disease.

10. The charity aims to _______ the gap between educational opportunities available to rich and poor students.

11. Automation _______ rise to significant concerns about future employment prospects for low-skilled workers.

12. The company _______ an effort to reduce its carbon footprint by investing in solar panels.

## PHẦN G: LISTENING-STYLE DICTATION / GAP-FILL (10 câu)

There is (G1) concern among leading scientists that climate change poses a (G2) threat to global food security in the decades ahead. Governments worldwide must (G3) decisive action to address this crisis before it becomes completely irreversible. Existing evidence suggests that rising temperatures will have a (G4) impact on agricultural yields, particularly in tropical and semi-arid regions. It is now (G5) accepted by the global scientific community that transitioning to renewable energy is absolutely essential. International organisations have recently (G6) priority to developing drought-resistant and heat-tolerant crop varieties. Research into sustainable farming practices has (G7) considerable progress over the past decade, driven by both public and private investment. However, there remains (G8) concern that funding for agricultural innovation is dangerously insufficient. Rising temperatures continue to (G9) rise to more frequent and severe weather events worldwide. Unless governments and private investors urgently (G10) to a comprehensive mutual agreement on shared climate targets, the global food system will remain dangerously exposed to the effects of climate change.

## PHẦN H: SPEAKING / DISCUSSION PROMPT

*Trả lời miệng hoặc viết câu trả lời, dùng cấu trúc/từ vựng của bài.*

1. "Governments should give priority to addressing climate change above all other policy concerns." To what extent do you agree?
2. Describe a situation in which you or someone you know had to take decisive action under pressure.
3. What compelling evidence exists that environmental problems are worsening globally? What measures should be taken?
4. Discuss the extent to which there is widespread concern in your country about environmental sustainability.

**Model answer (Band 7–8):** There is overwhelming evidence that climate change is posing a significant threat to both human wellbeing and global biodiversity. Governments must take decisive action to make a real difference before the damage becomes irreversible. It is now widely accepted that fossil fuel dependence gives rise to a range of severe consequences, from rising sea levels to prolonged droughts. While significant progress has been made in renewable energy development, there remains deep concern that the pace of change is insufficient. Unless all nations come to a mutual agreement and give priority to green policy, the consequences for future generations will be catastrophic.

# ANSWER KEY — ĐÁP ÁN

## Section A: Fill in the Blank

1. **take** decisive action
2. **did** considerable damage *(also acceptable: "caused")*
3. **done/conducted** extensive research *(formal: conducted; informal/phrasal: done)*
4. **effect** *(come into effect)*
5. **deep / widespread** concern *(both correct)*
6. **gave** rise to
7. **give** priority to
8. **profound / significant** impact
9. **widely** accepted
10. **bridge** the gap
11. **gives / has given** rise to
12. **made** an effort

## Section B: Multiple Choice

13. **C** — rapid growth *(heavy/strong/big growth are all unnatural — "rapid" is the standard collocation)*
14. **D** — both B (steps) and C (measures) are correct: "taken steps/measures to..."
15. **C** — overwhelming evidence *(big and heavy are wrong; "strong" evidence is acceptable but "overwhelming" is stronger and more academic)*
16. **B** — make a significant difference *(KHÔNG: do/take/create a difference)*
17. **B** — definitive conclusion *(strong conclusion is possible; "definitive" = dứt khoát, chính thức nhất)*
18. **B** — give rise to *(fixed collocation; cannot substitute make/take/bring)*
19. **D** — both B (harm) and C (damage) are correct *(both "do harm" and "do damage" are standard collocations)*
20. **A** — mutual agreement *(joint/common/general agreements are less standard for diplomatic contexts)*
21. **C** — take part in *(KHÔNG: make/do/join part in — "join" requires "join in" without "part")*
22. **A** — argues strongly *(heavily/deeply/broadly are incorrect collocations with "argues")*

## Section C: Sentence Transformation

23. The government decided to **take decisive action on** the housing crisis.
24. Social media has **given rise to** serious problems for mental health among teenagers.
25. This research **was not done/conducted properly**, which affected the results.
26. There is **deep/widespread concern about** the consequences of artificial intelligence.
27. It is **widely accepted that** human activity causes climate change.
28. The new law **will come into effect** from March 2027.
29. The company **made an effort to** lower its environmental impact.
30. Not all children **have equal access to** quality educational resources.

## Section D: Error Correction

31. **Error:** "do a real difference" → **Correction:** "make a real difference to public opinion"
32. **Error:** "done significant progress" → **Correction:** "made significant progress in understanding..."
33. **Error:** "doing serious damage to landfill use" — *The phrase itself is awkward. Better:* "reducing reliance on landfill" or "significantly reducing the amount of waste sent to landfill" *(The error is using "doing damage" — you reduce landfill use, not damage it)*
34. **Error:** "take advantage FROM" → **Correction:** "take advantage **OF** new digital technologies"
35. **Error:** "Strong rain" → **Correction:** "**Heavy** rain and extreme flooding..."
36. **Error:** "does a compelling argument" → **Correction:** "**makes** a compelling argument for..."
37. **Error:** "came to a deal" → **Correction:** "**reached** a deal" OR "came to **an agreement**"

## Section E: Translation — Model Answers

38. The government must **take decisive measures/action** to **address** corruption.

39. There is **compelling evidence** that air pollution has a **profound impact** on children's health.

40. The **rapid development** of artificial intelligence is **giving rise to** **deep concerns** about privacy.

41. Universities need to **give priority to** **providing equal opportunities** for students from all social backgrounds.

42. **Climate change** is **posing significant challenges** to agricultural systems worldwide.

## Section F: Sample Answer (Technology Topic)

> "Technology has undoubtedly had a **profound impact** on how young people communicate today. **Social media**, in particular, has become **increasingly common** as a primary means of interaction, **giving rise to** both new opportunities and serious concerns. While it is **widely accepted** that digital tools **contribute significantly** to breaking down geographical barriers, many experts are **deeply concerned** about their effects on face-to-face communication skills. The **rapid development** of messaging applications has **fundamentally changed** social norms around conversation, with many young people now **preferring** digital contact over physical interaction. Governments and educators must **take decisive action** to ensure that these changes ultimately benefit rather than harm society."
