## Exercise 1: Classify and use (20 items)
Put the verb in brackets in gerund or infinitive form:

1. She enjoys _______ (swim) in the sea.
2. He decided _______ (change) his career.
3. I can't stand _______ (wait) for people.
4. They suggested _______ (visit) the old town.
5. She refused _______ (answer) the question.
6. He admitted _______ (steal) the money.
7. Remember _______ (lock) the door before leaving!
8. I remember _______ (meet) her at a conference years ago.
9. She stopped _______ (smoke) last year.
10. She stopped _______ (buy) some water on the way.
11. Try _______ (add) a little pepper — it might taste better.
12. He tried _______ (lift) the box but it was too heavy.
13. We regret _______ (inform) you that your application failed.
14. I regret _______ (not study) harder when I was young.
15. This job means _______ (work) long hours.
16. He didn't mean _______ (hurt) anyone's feelings.
17. She went on _______ (talk) for another hour.
18. He finished school and went on _______ (study) medicine.
19. Would you mind _______ (open) the window?
20. She managed _______ (complete) the task before the deadline.

**Answers:** 1.swimming 2.to change 3.waiting 4.visiting 5.to answer 6.stealing 7.to lock 8.meeting 9.smoking 10.to buy 11.adding 12.to lift 13.to inform 14.not studying 15.working 16.to hurt 17.talking 18.to study 19.opening 20.to complete

## Exercise 2: Tricky pairs — choose and explain meaning
Choose the correct form AND explain the meaning difference:

1. She stopped _______ (talk / to talk) on her phone when the film began.
2. I must remember _______ (post / to post) that letter tomorrow.
3. Try _______ (take / to take) some aspirin — you might feel better.
4. He regrets _______ (not spend / not spending) more time with his family when they were young.

**Answers:**
1. talking — stopped talking = she was on the phone but stopped when the film began
2. to remember to post = she hasn't posted it yet, must not forget
3. taking — try taking = experiment with the aspirin to see if it helps
4. not spending — regret + gerund = feeling sorry about something in the past
