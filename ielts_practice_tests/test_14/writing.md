# IELTS Academic Writing — Test 14

**Time allowed: 60 minutes**

## WRITING TASK 1

*You should spend about 20 minutes on this task.*

The table below shows the percentage of adults in five countries who engaged in physical exercise **never**, **sometimes**, or **regularly** in 2023.

*Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.*

*Write at least 150 words.*

---

### Data Table

| Country | Never (%) | Sometimes (%) | Regularly (%) |
|---------|-----------|---------------|---------------|
| UK | 18 | 45 | 37 |
| USA | 22 | 38 | 40 |
| Japan | 28 | 52 | 20 |
| Brazil | 15 | 42 | 43 |
| Germany | 12 | 40 | 48 |

---

### Model Answer — Task 1

The table illustrates the proportion of adults in five countries—the UK, USA, Japan, Brazil, and Germany—who engaged in physical exercise never, sometimes, or regularly in 2023.

Overall, Germany recorded the highest proportion of regular exercisers, while Japan had both the lowest rate of regular exercise and the highest percentage of adults who never exercised. In all five countries, a substantial share of the adult population fell into the 'sometimes' category.

In terms of regular exercise, Germany led with 48%, closely followed by Brazil at 43% and the USA at 40%. The UK was slightly lower at 37%. By contrast, Japan recorded the weakest performance, with only 20% of adults exercising regularly—less than half the German figure.

The pattern for those who never exercised shows a clear divide. Japan had the highest rate at 28%, followed by the USA at 22% and the UK at 18%. Germany and Brazil had notably lower figures at 12% and 15% respectively, suggesting healthier exercise habits overall.

Regarding the 'sometimes' category, Japan stood out with 52%, indicating that while many Japanese adults exercise occasionally, sustained regular activity remains comparatively rare.

*(~178 words)*

## WRITING TASK 2

*You should spend about 40 minutes on this task.*

*Write about the following topic:*

> **Many people believe that individuals have a responsibility to protect the natural environment. Others argue that it is the government's duty. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.**

*Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own knowledge or experience.*

*Write at least 250 words.*

---

### Model Answer — Task 2

The question of whether individuals or governments bear primary responsibility for protecting the natural environment is one of the defining debates of our time. While both parties have important roles to play, I believe that governments must take the lead, as environmental challenges require coordinated, large-scale responses that exceed the capacity of individual action alone.

Those who emphasise individual responsibility argue that personal choices—consuming less, recycling, reducing energy use, and avoiding polluting activities—collectively constitute a powerful force for environmental change. From this perspective, individuals should be held accountable for their impact on shared ecological systems, and a culture of personal responsibility is essential if meaningful progress is to occur. There is genuine force to this view: consumer demand drives industrial production, and shifts in behaviour can signal important changes in social values.

However, the scale and systemic nature of contemporary environmental threats make government intervention indispensable. Climate change, biodiversity loss, and ocean pollution are driven primarily by industrial activity, agricultural practices, and energy systems that only governments have the authority and capacity to regulate effectively. Individual consumers cannot independently alter the incentive structures that govern corporate behaviour; binding legislation, carbon pricing, and international agreements are required to produce change at the necessary scale. Governments also have a responsibility to ensure that the costs of environmental protection are distributed fairly across society, rather than being borne disproportionately by those with the fewest resources.

In conclusion, while individuals undoubtedly have a moral obligation to minimise their environmental impact, the primary responsibility for safeguarding the natural world lies with governments. Only collective action coordinated through political institutions can address environmental challenges at the speed and scale the current crisis demands. Individual choices matter deeply, but they must be enabled and reinforced by robust public policy.

*(~280 words)*

---

### Examiner Criteria — Task 2

| Criterion | Assessment Focus |
|---|---|
| **Task Response** | Both views are presented fairly and fully; the writer's own opinion is clearly stated and consistently maintained throughout; the argument is developed with specific reasoning and examples. |
| **Coherence and Cohesion** | Four-paragraph structure with a clear introduction, two body paragraphs, and a conclusion; cohesive devices (*however*, *from this perspective*, *in conclusion*) used naturally and without mechanical repetition. |
| **Lexical Resource** | Academic vocabulary used accurately (*indispensable*, *incentive structures*, *disproportionately*, *systematic*); varied expression avoids repetition; no inappropriate informal language. |
| **Grammatical Range and Accuracy** | Complex and compound-complex sentences used throughout; passive voice, modal verbs, and subordinate clauses deployed appropriately; very few, if any, errors that impede communication. |
