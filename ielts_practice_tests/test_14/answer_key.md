### Passage 1: Human Evolution and the Emergence of Language (Q1–13)

**Q1–6: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | TRUE | Paragraph A states that bipedalism "freed the forelimbs from the burden of locomotion and enabled increasingly sophisticated tool use." |
| 2 | TRUE | Paragraph B states the brain grew "from approximately 400 cubic centimetres in early australopithecines." |
| 3 | TRUE | Paragraph C explicitly states Neanderthals "inhabited Europe and western Asia for over 300,000 years." |
| 4 | FALSE | Paragraph C states they "became extinct approximately 40,000 years ago, *following* the arrival of anatomically modern humans"—i.e., after, not before. |
| 5 | TRUE | Paragraph D describes the cognitive revolution as "marked by a sudden proliferation of complex symbolic artefacts—cave paintings, personal ornaments, musical instruments." |
| 6 | FALSE | Paragraph D states "The precise cause of this cognitive leap remains debated; some researchers propose a genetic mutation… while others attribute it to a critical threshold of population density." |

### Passage 2: The Italian Renaissance (Q14–26)

**Q14–18: Matching Headings**

| Q | Paragraph | Answer | Heading |
|---|---|---|---|
| 14 | B | **iii** | The economic foundations of cultural innovation |
| 15 | C | **i** | The role of wealthy families in funding the arts |
| 16 | D | **ii** | New philosophical ideas about human potential |
| 17 | E | **vii** | Artists who defined an era |
| 18 | F | **v** | The influence of Italian culture beyond its borders |

*Note: Headings iv (A transformation in the social status of artists), vi (The political use of artistic patronage), and viii (Ancient learning as a source of inspiration) are not used.*

**Q19–23: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|---|---|

### Passage 3: The Psychology of Urban Design (Q27–40)

**Q27–30: Multiple Choice**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| 27 | **B** | Paragraph B: green spaces are associated with "reduced levels of psychological distress, lower rates of depression and anxiety." |
| 28 | **C** | Paragraph C: "A landmark study conducted in the Netherlands." |
| 29 | **B** | Paragraph D: residents reported "feeling a stronger sense of community belonging than those in car-dependent areas." |
| 30 | **C** | Paragraph E: "The critical variable appears to be not density itself but the quality of design." |

---

**Q31–36: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| 31 | TRUE | Paragraph B: "Studies conducted across multiple countries have found that proximity to parks…is associated with reduced levels of psychological distress." |
| 32 | FALSE | Paragraph B specifies residents who lived within **300 metres** of a park, not 100 metres. |
| 33 | FALSE | Paragraph C links traffic noise to "elevated rates of **cardiovascular disease**, sleep disruption, and psychological stress"—both physical and psychological. |
| 34 | NOT GIVEN | Paragraph D discusses the Portland study only in terms of social cohesion; noise levels are not mentioned. |
| 35 | TRUE | Paragraph F: Singapore aims to ensure "no resident lives more than a ten-minute walk from a significant natural area." |
| 36 | TRUE | Paragraph G: "approximately 68 percent of the world's population will live in urban areas by 2050." 68% is more than two-thirds (≈66.7%). |

---

**Q37–40: Gap Fill**

| Q | Answer | Source in Passage |
|---|---|---|
| 37 | natural | Paragraph B: "direct engagement with **natural** environments…has been shown to reduce cortisol levels and restore attentional capacity" |
| 38 | noise | Paragraph C: "traffic **noise**…associated with elevated rates of cardiovascular disease, sleep disruption" |
| 39 | walkability | Paragraph D: "The concept of **walkability**—the degree to which a neighbourhood is designed for pedestrian movement" |
| 40 | evidence-based | Paragraph F: "The discipline of **evidence-based** urban design draws on findings from environmental psychology, public health, and neuroscience" |
