The chart below combines a bar graph showing the number of urban farms established in five cities between 2010 and 2020, and a line graph showing the average annual yield (in tonnes) per urban farm in the same cities over the same period.

**Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.**

*Write at least 150 words.*

---

### Model Answer — Task 1

The combined chart presents data on the number of urban farms established across five cities between 2010 and 2020, together with the average annual yield per farm in tonnes recorded in 2020.

Overall, Tokyo recorded the greatest number of urban farms and the highest yield per farm, while Bogotá had the fewest farms and the lowest yield. Notably, the relationship between farm numbers and productivity per farm was not consistent across all five cities.

In terms of the number of farms established, Tokyo led significantly with 410 farms, followed by Singapore at 320 and Detroit at 280. London and Bogotá recorded considerably fewer farms, at 195 and 140 respectively. Regarding average yield per farm, Tokyo again ranked highest at 55 tonnes, with Singapore in second place at 48 tonnes. London occupied a middle position at 31 tonnes, while Detroit and Bogotá produced the lowest yields at 22 and 18 tonnes respectively.

A particularly striking comparison is that Singapore, despite establishing fewer farms than Detroit (320 versus 280), achieved more than double the yield per farm — 48 tonnes compared with Detroit's 22 tonnes. This suggests that a greater number of farms does not necessarily correspond to higher productivity per farm, and that factors such as farming technology and available land quality may be more influential determinants of output.

## WRITING TASK 2

*You should spend about 40 minutes on this task.*

*Write about the following topic:*

Many people believe that democracy is the best system of government. However, others argue that it has significant flaws.

**To what extent do you agree that democracy is the most effective form of governance?**

*Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own knowledge or experience.*

*Write at least 250 words.*

---

### Model Answer — Task 2

Democracy, despite its well-documented imperfections, represents the most accountable and rights-protective form of government currently available. I largely agree that it is the most effective system of governance, though this position requires an honest acknowledgement of its limitations.

The central strength of democracy lies in its mechanisms of accountability. Elected officials remain answerable to the electorate through regular, competitive elections, creating a structural incentive to govern in the public interest. Compared to autocratic systems, where power is concentrated without institutional restraint, democracies have historically demonstrated greater respect for individual rights, freedom of expression, and the rule of law. Research in comparative politics consistently links democratic governance to lower rates of political violence, stronger protection of minorities, and higher levels of long-term human development.

However, democracy has genuine weaknesses that must not be minimised. The rise of populist leaders who exploit democratic processes to dismantle institutional safeguards represents a serious vulnerability — recent examples from Hungary and Venezuela illustrate how elected governments can erode the very structures that democracy depends upon. Furthermore, the proliferation of disinformation through digital media can distort public opinion, compromising the quality of collective decision-making. Democratic systems can also produce short-termism, as governments prioritise policies that secure electoral victory in the near term rather than addressing longer-term challenges such as climate change or pension reform.

Nevertheless, the alternatives — authoritarianism, oligarchy, and theocracy — have consistently demonstrated greater tendencies toward repression, corruption, and instability. While imperfect, democracy provides the most robust structural framework for peaceful power transfer, minority protection, and adaptive governance.

In conclusion, I believe democracy remains the most effective system of governance not because it is free from flaws, but because it provides the conditions most conducive to individual freedom, institutional accountability, and long-term societal resilience.
