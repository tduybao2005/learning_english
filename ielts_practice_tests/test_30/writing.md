## Task 1

**Đề bài:**

The maps below show the changes to a town centre between 2000 and 2025. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

**Mô tả bản đồ:**

*2000:* The town centre contained a central market square surrounded by a church (north), a post office (east), a car park (south), and a row of small shops (west). A bus station was located at the northern edge, adjacent to the church.

*2025:* The market square has been pedestrianised. The post office has been replaced by a modern community centre. The car park has been converted into a public garden with seating areas. The small shops have been demolished and replaced by a four-storey apartment block. The bus station has been relocated to the south of the town centre, and a new cycle lane network has been added along the main roads.

---

**Bài mẫu (Band 7–8, ~175 từ):**

The maps illustrate how a town centre underwent significant transformation between 2000 and 2025.

Overall, the most striking change is the shift towards pedestrian-friendly and green spaces, with several functional buildings either replaced or relocated.

In 2000, the town centre was dominated by practical infrastructure. A car park occupied the southern portion, a post office stood to the east, and a row of small shops lined the western edge. A bus station was situated at the northern boundary, beside the church, which has remained unchanged.

By 2025, considerable redevelopment had taken place. The car park was converted into a public garden, and the central market square was pedestrianised, suggesting a greater emphasis on leisure and walkability. The post office gave way to a community centre, while the western shops were demolished to make room for a four-storey apartment block, reflecting growing demand for residential accommodation. The bus station was relocated to the southern edge of the town, and a new cycle lane network was introduced throughout the area.

In summary, the town centre evolved from a primarily commercial and transport-oriented space into a more mixed-use, environmentally conscious environment.

## Task 2

**Đề bài:**

*"As cities grow larger, it becomes increasingly difficult to maintain a sense of community. What are the causes of this problem and what measures could be taken to address it?"*

Viết ít nhất **250 từ**.

---

**Bài mẫu (Band 7–8, ~280 từ):**

As urban populations continue to expand, many residents of large cities report feeling isolated and disconnected from their neighbours. This erosion of community is a complex problem with several identifiable causes, though practical solutions do exist.

One primary cause is the sheer scale and anonymity of large cities. In a village of a few hundred people, residents inevitably know one another. In a metropolis of millions, individuals may live in the same apartment building for years without learning their neighbours' names. The fast pace of urban life compounds this problem: long working hours and lengthy commutes leave little time or energy for social interaction. Additionally, social media and digital entertainment, while connecting people globally, can paradoxically reduce face-to-face contact at the local level.

Urban design also plays a significant role. Many modern residential developments lack communal spaces — parks, squares, or meeting rooms — where residents can naturally encounter one another. High-rise apartment blocks, in particular, can function as vertical dormitories where social life is essentially absent. Furthermore, the mobility of urban populations, with residents frequently moving in search of better employment or cheaper housing, prevents the long-term relationships that underpin genuine community.

Addressing these challenges requires action at multiple levels. Urban planners should prioritise the creation of public spaces that encourage spontaneous social interaction, such as pedestrianised squares, community gardens, and local markets. Local governments can support community organisations, sports clubs, and cultural events that give residents a shared identity and sense of belonging. Employers could also contribute by offering flexible working arrangements that allow people to spend more time in their local neighbourhoods.

In conclusion, while the growth of cities naturally fragments social bonds, thoughtful design and community investment can rebuild the connections that make urban life fulfilling.
