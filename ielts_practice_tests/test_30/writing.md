**Time allowed: 60 minutes**

## WRITING TASK 1

*You should spend about 20 minutes on this task.*

The chart below shows the percentage of people in five countries who reported feeling highly motivated at work in 2015 and 2023.

**Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.**

*Write at least 150 words.*

---

### Data Table

| Country | 2015 (%) | 2023 (%) | Change |
|---------|----------|----------|--------|
| Japan | 12 | 18 | +6 |
| USA | 31 | 34 | +3 |
| Germany | 22 | 27 | +5 |
| Brazil | 28 | 41 | +13 |
| India | 45 | 58 | +13 |

---

### Model Answer

The bar chart compares the percentage of workers reporting high motivation in five countries in 2015 and 2023. Overall, all five nations recorded increases over the eight-year period, with India consistently leading and Japan remaining the lowest throughout.

In 2015, India had by far the highest proportion of highly motivated workers at 45%, more than double the figure for the USA (31%) and nearly four times that of Japan (12%). Brazil (28%) and Germany (22%) occupied the middle positions between these two extremes.

By 2023, every country had seen growth, but the increases were notably uneven. India and Brazil recorded the most dramatic gains, both rising by 13 percentage points to reach 58% and 41% respectively, making them the standout performers. Germany and Japan saw more modest improvements of 5 and 6 points, climbing to 27% and 18%. The USA, while already relatively high in 2015, recorded the smallest increase of just 3 points, reaching 34%.

In summary, while workplace motivation rose universally, emerging economies — particularly India and Brazil — showed considerably stronger growth than their counterparts in Europe, North America, and Japan.

## WRITING TASK 2

*You should spend about 40 minutes on this task.*

*Write about the following topic:*

**Some people believe that artificial intelligence will have a largely positive impact on society, while others are concerned about its risks. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.**

*Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples or other evidence from your own knowledge.*

*Write at least 250 words.*

---

### Model Answer

Artificial intelligence is one of the most transformative technologies of our era, and opinion is sharply divided between those who celebrate its potential and those who warn of its dangers. I believe that while the benefits are real and substantial, they can only be fully realised if serious risks are proactively managed.

Proponents of AI point to its remarkable achievements in medicine, science, and daily life. AI-powered diagnostic systems can now detect diseases such as cancer with accuracy comparable to specialist physicians, potentially saving millions of lives in countries where medical expertise is scarce. In research, machine learning algorithms have accelerated drug discovery, materials science, and climate modelling in ways that would have been impossible using conventional methods. For consumers, AI underlies the navigation, translation, and personalisation tools that many people now depend upon.

Critics, however, raise legitimate concerns. AI systems trained on historical data frequently reproduce societal biases, as studies of facial recognition and algorithmic hiring tools have repeatedly demonstrated. Autonomous decision-making in areas such as criminal sentencing and credit scoring can produce outcomes that are difficult to contest or even understand. At a macroeconomic level, widespread automation threatens to displace large segments of the workforce faster than new employment can be created.

In my opinion, the risks do not negate the benefits but demand careful governance alongside innovation. Regulatory frameworks such as the EU's Artificial Intelligence Act represent a constructive attempt to categorise risks and impose appropriate oversight. With robust accountability mechanisms, investment in worker reskilling, and international cooperation on safety standards, AI's advantages can be extended broadly while its harms are contained.

*(~280 words)*
