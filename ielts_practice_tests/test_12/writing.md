## WRITING TASK 1

*You should spend about 20 minutes on this task.*

The bar chart below shows the average monthly temperatures (°C) in January and July for four cities: London, Sydney, Toronto, and Singapore.

**Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.**

*Write at least 150 words.*

---

| City | January (°C) | July (°C) |
|---|---|---|
| London | 4 | 18 |
| Sydney | 26 | 12 |
| Toronto | −7 | 22 |
| Singapore | 27 | 28 |

---

### Model Answer — Task 1

The bar chart illustrates the average monthly temperatures in January and July for four cities: London, Sydney, Toronto, and Singapore.

Overall, Singapore maintains consistently high temperatures throughout the year, while Toronto and London display substantial seasonal variation. Sydney, situated in the Southern Hemisphere, shows an inverse seasonal pattern compared to the Northern Hemisphere cities.

In January, Singapore and Sydney record the highest temperatures, at 27°C and 26°C respectively. Toronto, by contrast, experiences extreme winter cold at −7°C, while London registers a modest 4°C. In July, the pattern shifts considerably: Toronto rises sharply to 22°C, making it the warmest Northern Hemisphere city, while London reaches 18°C. Singapore remains relatively stable at 28°C, but Sydney's temperature drops to 12°C, reflecting its position in the Southern Hemisphere where July falls in winter.

The most dramatic seasonal range belongs to Toronto, with a difference of 29 degrees between January and July. Singapore, however, shows the least variation — just 1°C — across the two months, remaining warm throughout the year.

## WRITING TASK 2

*You should spend about 40 minutes on this task.*

*Write about the following topic:*

> **The increasing use of technology in classrooms has transformed education. Some believe this is a positive development, while others see it as harmful. Discuss both views and give your own opinion.**

*Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own knowledge or experience.*

*Write at least 250 words.*

---

### Model Answer — Task 2

The rapid integration of technology into educational settings has generated considerable debate among educators, policymakers, and parents alike. While proponents argue that digital tools enhance learning outcomes, critics warn of potentially damaging consequences. In my view, the benefits of classroom technology outweigh its drawbacks, provided it is implemented thoughtfully and equitably.

Those who welcome technology in education point to its capacity to personalise learning and increase student engagement. Interactive platforms can adapt to individual learning speeds, offering immediate feedback and allowing students to revisit challenging concepts at their own pace. Furthermore, digital resources give students access to a vast range of materials that would otherwise be unavailable in a traditional classroom setting, broadening their educational horizons considerably. Countries such as South Korea and Finland have reported improvements in student performance following the strategic incorporation of tablets and online learning platforms into their curricula.

However, opponents raise valid concerns. Excessive screen time has been linked to reduced attention spans and difficulties with sustained concentration, particularly among younger learners. There is also the risk that students who lack access to technology at home are placed at a systematic disadvantage, thereby widening existing educational inequalities. Additionally, some educators argue that over-reliance on digital tools may undermine the development of fundamental skills such as handwriting, critical reading of long-form texts, and face-to-face communication.

On balance, I believe that technology is a powerful educational asset when used judiciously. Schools should integrate digital resources to complement, rather than replace, traditional teaching methods. Clear guidelines on screen time, combined with equitable access to devices for all students, would help ensure that technology serves as an equaliser rather than a source of further division. The goal of education remains constant: to nurture curious, capable, and independent learners — and technology should be harnessed in service of that enduring aim.

*(~280 words)*
