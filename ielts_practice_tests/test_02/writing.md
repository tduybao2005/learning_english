## Task 1 (20 minutes — minimum 150 words)

**Prompt:** "The bar chart below shows renewable energy production (in GWh) by type in four countries in 2020."

**Data:**

| Country  | Solar | Wind | Hydro |
|----------|-------|------|-------|
| Germany  | 50    | 120  | 20    |
| Denmark  | 8     | 60   | 1     |
| Norway   | 3     | 18   | 140   |
| Vietnam  | 15    | 8    | 70    |

---

### Model Answer — Band 7.5 (~175 words)

The bar chart compares renewable energy production across three sources — solar, wind, and hydropower — in Germany, Denmark, Norway, and Vietnam in 2020.

Overall, the dominant energy source varied significantly by country: wind energy dominated in Germany and Denmark, while Norway relied overwhelmingly on hydropower. Vietnam's renewable output was more diversified, though hydropower led.

Germany produced the most wind energy of the four countries at 120 GWh, far exceeding Denmark's 60 GWh. Germany also led in solar generation at 50 GWh, compared to Vietnam's 15 GWh, Denmark's 8 GWh, and Norway's negligible 3 GWh. Hydropower presented a contrasting picture: Norway dominated at 140 GWh — more than double any other country's total renewable output — while Germany (20 GWh) and Denmark (just 1 GWh) produced very little.

Vietnam stood out for having meaningful production across all three categories, suggesting a more balanced renewable portfolio, though its figures were modest compared to Germany's wind and Norway's hydro output.

## Task 2 (40 minutes — minimum 250 words)

**Prompt:** "Governments should ban the production and sale of plastic bags in order to protect the environment. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

---

### Model Answer — Band 7.5 (~275 words)

The debate over whether governments should prohibit plastic bag production and sales has intensified as evidence of their environmental harm accumulates. I largely agree with such a ban, though I believe its success depends on how it is implemented.

The environmental case for banning plastic bags is compelling. Single-use plastic bags are responsible for substantial pollution in both land and marine environments. Unlike organic waste, they do not biodegrade but instead fragment into microplastics that contaminate soil, waterways, and the food chain. Given that millions of plastic bags are discarded every hour globally, and that viable alternatives such as reusable cloth bags and paper bags exist, the argument for banning them appears both rational and urgent.

However, an outright ban must be designed thoughtfully to be effective. Poorly regulated bans can displace consumption towards alternatives that are, counterintuitively, more environmentally damaging: cotton bags, for example, require far more water and energy to produce than a standard plastic bag and must be reused many hundreds of times before their environmental footprint is lower. Moreover, in some low-income communities, free plastic bags from supermarkets serve as affordable bin liners, and their removal without affordable substitutes can place a disproportionate burden on poorer households.

In conclusion, I support a government ban on single-use plastic bags as a necessary environmental measure, provided it is accompanied by accessible alternatives, public education campaigns, and support for communities most affected by the transition. A phased approach rather than an immediate prohibition is likely to achieve more durable behavioural change.

### Essay Structure Template (Opinion Essay)

**Paragraph 1 — Introduction:**
Paraphrase the topic + State your position clearly

**Paragraph 2 — Main argument supporting your position:**
Topic sentence → Explanation → Evidence / Example → Link back

**Paragraph 3 — Acknowledging complexity / Counter-argument:**
Topic sentence → Concession or qualification → Why your position still holds

**Paragraph 4 — Conclusion:**
Restate position (in different words) + Summarise key reasoning

---

### Useful Vocabulary for Task 2 (Environment Topics)

- **Problem language:** environmental degradation / contamination / carbon footprint / microplastic pollution
- **Policy language:** impose a ban / phase out / subsidise alternatives / regulate / incentivise
- **Concession language:** admittedly / it must be acknowledged that / while it is true that
- **Nuance language:** provided that / on the condition that / in the long run / in isolation
- **Conclusion language:** on balance / all things considered / the evidence suggests that
