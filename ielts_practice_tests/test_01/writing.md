## WRITING TASK 1

*You should spend about 20 minutes on this task.*

The line chart below shows the percentage of people in different age groups who used the internet in a particular country in 2000, 2010, and 2020.

**Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.**

*Write at least 150 words.*

---

### Data Table (Line Chart Represented as Table)

| Age Group | 2000 | 2010 | 2020 |
|-----------|------|------|------|
| 18–24     | 45%  | 88%  | 99%  |
| 25–34     | 38%  | 82%  | 98%  |
| 35–54     | 22%  | 70%  | 94%  |
| 55+       | 5%   | 35%  | 75%  |

---

### Model Answer — Task 1 (Band 7–8)

The line chart illustrates changes in internet usage rates across four age groups — 18–24, 25–34, 35–54, and 55 and above — over three points in time: 2000, 2010, and 2020.

Overall, internet usage increased substantially across all age groups over the twenty-year period. While younger age groups consistently recorded the highest usage rates, the most dramatic proportional growth was observed among older adults.

In 2000, usage was strongly correlated with youth: the 18–24 group led at 45%, followed by 25–34 year-olds at 38%. The 35–54 group stood at 22%, while the 55+ cohort recorded a markedly low rate of just 5%. By 2010, all groups had risen considerably, with the 18–24 and 25–34 groups reaching 88% and 82% respectively. The 35–54 group climbed to 70%, and even the oldest cohort tripled its usage to 35%.

By 2020, near-universal internet adoption had been achieved among younger adults, with the 18–24 and 25–34 groups reaching 99% and 98%. The 35–54 group followed closely at 94%. Most notably, usage among those aged 55 and above rose to 75% — a fifteen-fold increase from 2000 — representing the largest proportional gain across the period.

In conclusion, while generational differences in internet use remained visible throughout, the gap between the oldest and youngest groups narrowed dramatically over the two decades.

## WRITING TASK 2

*You should spend about 40 minutes on this task.*

**Write about the following topic:**

> *Technology has had a largely negative impact on society. To what extent do you agree or disagree?*

*Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own knowledge or experience.*

*Write at least 250 words.*

---

### Model Answer — Task 2 (Band 7–8)

The claim that technology has been predominantly harmful to society is one I find difficult to accept. While it is true that certain technological developments have created serious problems, a balanced assessment of the evidence suggests that technology has, on balance, been enormously beneficial to human welfare, health, and opportunity.

The strongest argument in favour of technology's positive impact lies in the realm of medicine and public health. Vaccines, surgical imaging systems, and pharmaceutical research tools have collectively extended average human life expectancy by more than thirty years since 1900. Modern diagnostic technology — from MRI scanners to genetic sequencing — allows diseases to be detected and treated at stages that would have been unthinkable to previous generations. It is difficult to characterise these developments as negative.

Communication technology, similarly, has expanded access to information and education on an unprecedented scale. The internet has made vast libraries of knowledge available to individuals in remote regions of the world who would previously have had no access to formal education. Platforms such as Khan Academy provide free instruction to millions of learners across the developing world. These tools have demonstrably reduced barriers to educational opportunity.

Nevertheless, it would be naive to ignore genuine harms. Social media platforms have been linked to increased rates of anxiety and depression, particularly among adolescents. Automation has disrupted established industries and displaced workers faster than new opportunities have emerged. Environmental damage caused by large-scale industrial technology remains a serious and largely unresolved challenge.

In conclusion, while acknowledging these real concerns, I disagree with the assertion that technology's impact has been largely negative. The challenge for contemporary societies is not to reject technology but to govern it more wisely — ensuring its benefits are equitably distributed and its harms are rigorously managed.

- **Grammar:** Range of complex structures; relative clauses and conditional forms used correctly.
