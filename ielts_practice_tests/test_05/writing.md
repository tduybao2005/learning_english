# IELTS ACADEMIC WRITING — TEST 05

## Task 1 (20 minutes — minimum 150 words)

The diagram below shows the process of treating wastewater before it is released or reused.

**Process steps (no image available — described in text):**
Stage 1: Wastewater collected from households and industry
Stage 2: Screening — large solids removed by metal screens
Stage 3: Primary sedimentation — heavy particles sink to form sludge
Stage 4: Biological treatment — bacteria decompose organic matter in aeration tanks
Stage 5: Secondary sedimentation — remaining solids settle out
Stage 6: Filtration — water passes through sand and gravel filters
Stage 7: Disinfection — chlorine or UV light kills remaining bacteria
Stage 8: Treated water stored in reservoir
Stage 9a: Distributed for agricultural/industrial use
OR Stage 9b: Further purified for drinking water supply

Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.

---

### Model Answer — Band 7.5 (~175 words):

The diagram illustrates a nine-stage process by which wastewater is treated and subsequently made available for various uses.

Initially, wastewater generated by households and industrial facilities is collected and undergoes screening, during which large physical solids are removed using metal screens. The water then enters a primary sedimentation tank, where heavier particles settle to the bottom, forming sludge.

Following this, the water undergoes biological treatment in aeration tanks, where bacteria decompose the remaining organic matter. A secondary sedimentation stage then removes further solids. The water subsequently passes through sand and gravel filters, which eliminate finer impurities.

At the disinfection stage, either chlorine or ultraviolet light is used to destroy any remaining harmful bacteria. The treated water is then stored in a reservoir, from which it can follow one of two pathways: it may be distributed directly for agricultural or industrial purposes, or alternatively, it may undergo further purification before being supplied as drinking water.

---

### Examiner Notes:

- **Task Achievement (Band 8):** All stages covered, passive voice used correctly for processes
- **Coherence & Cohesion (Band 7):** Good use of sequencing language (initially, following this, subsequently)
- **Lexical Resource (Band 7):** "sedimentation", "aeration", "purification" — appropriate technical vocabulary
- **Grammatical Range (Band 7):** Accurate passive constructions throughout; complex noun phrases used well

### Key Language for Process Diagrams:

**Sequencing expressions:**
- Initially / First of all / To begin with
- Following this / Subsequently / After that
- At the next stage / Once this is complete
- Finally / Ultimately / At the final stage

**Passive voice patterns (essential for processes):**
- Wastewater *is collected* from...
- Large solids *are removed* using...
- The water *is then transferred* to...
- Bacteria *are used* to decompose...
- The treated water *is stored* in...

**Common errors to avoid:**
- Do NOT use active voice when describing industrial or natural processes
- Do NOT list stages without connecting language
- Do NOT omit the final outcome/product of the process

## Task 2 (40 minutes — minimum 250 words)

**Essay Question:**

"Some people think that children living in cities are at a disadvantage compared to those growing up in rural areas. Others believe that cities offer more opportunities and advantages. Discuss both views and give your own opinion."

**Essay Type:** Discussion + Opinion
**Recommended structure:** Introduction → View 1 (rural advantages) → View 2 (urban advantages — your view) → Concession → Conclusion

---

### Model Answer — Band 7.5 (~280 words):

Whether urban or rural environments provide better childhoods is a question that generates considerable debate. While both settings offer distinct benefits, I believe that city environments ultimately provide more advantages for a child's long-term development.

Proponents of rural upbringings argue that children who grow up outside cities benefit from greater freedom and closer contact with nature. Spacious outdoor environments promote physical activity, independence, and a sense of wonder about the natural world. Furthermore, smaller communities tend to foster stronger social bonds and a sense of belonging, which can contribute positively to a child's emotional wellbeing. In rural settings, children are also typically shielded from the stresses and dangers that accompany dense urban living.

Nevertheless, I would argue that cities offer advantages that are more significant in today's world. Urban areas provide unparalleled access to educational institutions, cultural resources, and extracurricular activities. Children raised in cities are typically exposed to a greater diversity of people, ideas, and experiences, which can cultivate empathy, adaptability, and a broader worldview. Research suggests that access to quality schools, museums, and technology hubs in urban areas correlates with higher academic achievement. Moreover, cities offer superior healthcare facilities, which is particularly important during childhood.

Admittedly, cities also present challenges — pollution, limited green spaces, and higher cost of living — but these disadvantages can be mitigated through thoughtful urban planning and parental awareness.

In conclusion, while rural environments offer certain qualities that cities cannot easily replicate, the broader access to education, culture, and opportunities that urban life provides makes it, in my view, the more advantageous environment for children's development overall.

---

- **Task Achievement (Band 7):** Both views discussed fairly, clear personal opinion stated and maintained
- **Coherence & Cohesion (Band 7):** Well-organised paragraph structure; effective use of discourse markers

- **Grammatical Range (Band 7):** Mix of complex structures; accurate use of relative clauses and conditionals

### Common Writing Task 2 Mistakes (Band 5-6 → Band 7+):

| Weak Version | Stronger Version |
|---|---|
| "I think cities are better." | "I would argue that urban environments offer more significant advantages." |
| "Cities have many things." | "Urban areas provide unparalleled access to educational and cultural resources." |
| "Rural is good because of nature." | "Spacious outdoor environments promote physical activity and a sense of independence." |
| "But cities also have problems." | "Admittedly, cities present certain challenges, including pollution and high living costs." |
| "So I agree cities are better." | "In conclusion, while rural settings offer distinct merits, the broader opportunities of city life make it the more advantageous environment overall." |
