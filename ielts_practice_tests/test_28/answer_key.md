## PHẦN ĐỌC (READING) — Đáp án chi tiết

### Passage 1: Biodiversity Loss and the Sixth Mass Extinction (Q1–13)

**Q1–6: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | TRUE | Paragraph A states: "current rates of species loss are estimated at 100 to 1,000 times above this background level." |
| 2 | NOT GIVEN | The passage does not mention when the IUCN Red List was established. |
| 3 | TRUE | Paragraph D explains that sea otters "feed voraciously on sea urchins; when otter populations collapse, sea urchin numbers explode and the urchins devastate the kelp forests," confirming their keystone role in controlling sea urchin populations and protecting kelp forests. |
| 4 | FALSE | Paragraph B explicitly states that "habitat destruction remains the most pervasive threat," not climate change. |
| 5 | TRUE | Paragraph E states: "Estimates of the annual economic value of global ecosystem services exceed $125 trillion." |
| 6 | FALSE | Paragraph F states that Europe's Rewilding Network "operates across several European countries," not every country across the European continent. |

**Q7–10: Gap Fill**

| Q | Answer |
|---|--------|
| 7 | Red List |
| 8 | keystone |
| 9 | drivers |
| 10 | ecosystem services |

*Explanations:*
- *Q7: Paragraph C refers to "the Red List of Threatened Species" as the IUCN's classification system.*
- *Q8: Paragraph D introduces "the concept of keystone species — organisms whose presence in an ecosystem exerts a disproportionately large influence on ecological structure."*
- *Q9: Paragraph B states scientists have identified "five principal drivers of this unprecedented decline."*
- *Q10: Paragraph E defines "ecosystem services" as "the benefits that healthy natural systems provide at no direct cost to society."*

**Q11–13: Short Answer**

| Q | Answer |
|---|--------|
| 11 | HIPPO |
| 12 | wolves |
| 13 | rewilding |

*Explanations:*
- *Q11: Paragraph B states that the five drivers are "often summarised under the acronym HIPPO."*
- *Q12: Paragraph D states that "the reintroduction of wolves to Yellowstone National Park...triggered a trophic cascade" and "ultimately changing the course and hydrology of rivers."*
- *Q13: Paragraph F defines "rewilding" as "the large-scale restoration of natural ecosystems through the reintroduction of ecologically significant species."*

### Passage 2: Nuclear Energy: Risks and Potential (Q14–26)

**Q14–18: Matching Headings**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 14 | ii | Paragraph B discusses nuclear energy's capacity factor of 90%+ compared with solar and wind at around 25%, establishing its reliability advantage as a baseload source. |
| 15 | iv | Paragraph C describes the Three Mile Island, Chernobyl, and Fukushima accidents and their impact on public confidence and national energy policy. |
| 16 | i | Paragraph D discusses the half-lives of radioactive by-products (e.g. plutonium-239 at ~24,000 years) and the challenge of long-term storage. |
| 17 | vi | Paragraph E introduces Generation IV reactor designs and their intended improvements in safety, efficiency, and waste management. |
| 18 | v | Paragraph F describes ITER — a 35-nation collaborative research project aimed at demonstrating the feasibility of nuclear fusion. |

**Q19–23: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 19 | TRUE | Paragraph A confirms: "neutrons strike uranium fuel rods, triggering a controlled chain reaction...The heat produced converts water into high-pressure steam, which drives turbines connected to electrical generators." |
| 20 | FALSE | Paragraph B states nuclear stations achieve "capacity factors of 90 per cent or more," whereas solar and wind achieve "around 25 per cent." Nuclear is therefore higher, not lower. |
| 21 | FALSE | Paragraph C states the Chernobyl disaster resulted from "an unstable reactor design and serious operator errors during a safety test." It was the Fukushima accident (also Para C) that was "triggered by a massive tsunami that disabled the plant's cooling systems." |
| 22 | TRUE | Paragraph E states Generation IV designs are intended "to produce substantially smaller quantities of long-lived radioactive waste." |
| 23 | NOT GIVEN | Paragraph F describes ITER as a research facility aimed at demonstrating net energy gain from fusion, but makes no mention of any date for supplying electricity to power grids. |

**Q24–26: Multiple Choice**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 24 | B | Paragraph A states: "Control rods, made from neutron-absorbing materials such as boron or cadmium, are raised or lowered to regulate the reaction's intensity." Absorbing neutrons regulates the chain reaction. |
| 25 | B | Paragraph D explains that fission produces radioactive by-products with half-lives running to tens of thousands of years (e.g. "plutonium-239...has a half-life of approximately 24,000 years"), making indefinite isolation from the environment the core challenge. |
| 26 | C | Paragraph F explicitly states: "ITER...uses hydrogen isotopes known as deuterium and tritium as fuel." |

### Passage 3: The Origins and Evolution of Language (Q27–40)

**Q27–30: Multiple Choice**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 27 | B | Paragraph A states Chomsky proposed that "all humans are born with a set of innate grammatical principles that constrain the possible form of any natural language and explain why children acquire language with extraordinary speed and uniformity." |
| 28 | B | Paragraph D describes Nicaraguan Sign Language as emerging "spontaneously among deaf children...with no external linguistic model," demonstrating that language can develop without an external model to imitate. |
| 29 | C | Paragraph E states: "The principal causes are the economic and social pressures that lead communities to abandon heritage languages in favour of dominant national or international languages that offer superior educational and employment opportunities." |
| 30 | C | Paragraph G states that weaker versions of the Sapir-Whorf hypothesis "propose only that language influences rather than determines perception." |

**Q31–36: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 31 | FALSE | Paragraph A states Chomsky proposed that humans are born with "an innate biological endowment" — the opposite of primarily cultural transmission from caregivers. |
| 32 | TRUE | Paragraph D opens: "Sign languages provide some of the most compelling evidence for the biological basis of human language capacity." |
| 33 | FALSE | Paragraph F states that Welsh revitalisation "has reversed a long decline, and Welsh now has more than 800,000 speakers," which directly contradicts the claim of failure. |
| 34 | NOT GIVEN | The passage does not mention any connection between the Sapir-Whorf hypothesis and Chomsky's universal grammar theory, nor does it state why the hypothesis was first developed. |
| 35 | TRUE | Paragraph E states: "roughly half may be extinct by the end of the twenty-first century." |
| 36 | TRUE | Paragraph E states: "one language is estimated to die every 14 days." |

**Q37–40: Gap Fill**

| Q | Answer |
|---|--------|
| 37 | universal grammar |
| 38 | sign languages |
| 39 | half |
| 40 | revitalisation |

*Explanations:*
- *Q37: Paragraph A refers to Chomsky's "theory of universal grammar."*
- *Q38: Paragraph D states that "sign languages provide some of the most compelling evidence for the biological basis of human language capacity," including the example of Nicaraguan Sign Language, which emerged "with no external linguistic model."*
- *Q39: Paragraph E states: "roughly half may be extinct by the end of the twenty-first century."*
- *Q40: Paragraph F introduces "language revitalisation — deliberate programmes of documentation, education, and institutional promotion aimed at reversing decline."*
