## WRITING TASK 1

*You should spend about 20 minutes on this task.*

The table below shows data on four types of energy sources across five categories in 2022.

**Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.**

*Write at least 150 words.*

---

### Energy Sources Comparison Table (2022)

| Category | Coal | Natural Gas | Nuclear | Solar/Wind |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Global share of electricity generation | 36% | 23% | 10% | 12% |
| Average capacity factor | 40% | 50% | 92% | 25% |
| CO₂ emissions (g per kWh) | 820 | 490 | 12 | 11 |
| Average cost (USD per MWh) | $65 | $55 | $92 | $45 |
| Jobs created per GW installed | 1,800 | 1,200 | 3,500 | 5,400 |

---

### Model Answer — Task 1

The table compares four energy sources — coal, natural gas, nuclear, and solar/wind — across five categories in 2022.

Overall, coal continues to dominate global electricity generation despite having the highest carbon footprint, whereas solar and wind stand out as the cheapest and most job-intensive option. Nuclear, in contrast, leads on reliability and is almost equal to solar/wind on emissions.

In terms of generation share, coal accounts for the largest proportion at 36%, nearly triple that of nuclear (10%) and well ahead of solar/wind (12%). However, nuclear achieves by far the highest average capacity factor at 92% — more than double natural gas (50%) and nearly four times that of solar/wind (25%), which are constrained by weather dependence.

Regarding emissions, coal produces 820 g of CO₂ per kWh, roughly 75 times more than nuclear (12 g) or solar/wind (11 g). Natural gas sits in between at 490 g. Despite these environmental advantages, nuclear is the most expensive to generate at $92 per MWh, whereas solar/wind is the cheapest at $45. Finally, solar/wind creates the most employment at 5,400 jobs per GW installed — three times the coal figure and four and a half times that of natural gas.

## WRITING TASK 2

*You should spend about 40 minutes on this task.*

*Write about the following topic:*

> **Nuclear energy is a necessary solution to climate change and should be expanded globally, despite its risks. To what extent do you agree or disagree?**

*Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own knowledge or experience.*

*Write at least 250 words.*

---

### Model Answer — Task 2

The case for nuclear energy has grown more persuasive as the urgency of decarbonising electricity systems becomes clear. I partially agree that nuclear power deserves a significantly expanded role in addressing climate change, though I would stop short of calling it a necessary solution, since a diverse mix of technologies is likely to serve better than any single option.

The arguments in favour of nuclear expansion are compelling. Nuclear plants emit only around 12 grams of CO₂ per kilowatt-hour on a lifecycle basis — comparable to wind and solar — while achieving capacity factors of over 90 per cent, meaning they generate power almost continuously regardless of weather. This makes nuclear uniquely capable of providing the stable baseload electricity that renewables alone, given their intermittency, cannot reliably guarantee. Countries such as France, which derives approximately 70 per cent of its electricity from nuclear, demonstrate that large-scale low-carbon electricity systems are achievable with nuclear at their core.

That said, the risks are real and should not be minimised. The accidents at Chernobyl and Fukushima demonstrated that failures can have consequences far beyond national borders. Managing radioactive waste that remains hazardous for tens of thousands of years presents an unresolved technical and political challenge. Nuclear plants are also expensive and slow to build, which may make them less cost-effective than rapidly improving renewables in many markets.

In conclusion, I believe nuclear energy should be part of the global response to climate change, particularly in countries lacking the geographic or grid conditions to rely heavily on renewables. It is one essential tool in a diverse toolkit, rather than the necessary solution the prompt implies.

| **Coherence & Cohesion** | Four well-organised paragraphs (introduction with position, arguments for, arguments against, conclusion) with effective use of connectives ('though', 'That said', 'In conclusion') and logical progression throughout. |
