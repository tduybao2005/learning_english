### Passage 1: Plate Tectonics (Questions 1–13)

#### Questions 1–6: True / False / Not Given

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | **FALSE** | The passage states: "the scientific community largely rejected Wegener's hypothesis." The theory was not accepted — it was rejected. |
| **2** | **TRUE** | The passage states: "identical fossils of ancient organisms had been found on continents now separated by vast oceans," which Wegener used as supporting evidence. |
| **3** | **TRUE** | The passage states: "until the 1960s, when American geologist Harry Hess proposed the theory of seafloor spreading." |
| **4** | **FALSE** | The passage states: "Earth's magnetic field has reversed direction many times throughout geological history." It has not remained constant. |
| **5** | **NOT GIVEN** | The passage describes activity at both transform and convergent boundaries but makes no direct comparison of their relative levels of geological activity. |
| **6** | **TRUE** | The passage states: "the Himalayas, formed by the ongoing collision of the Indian and Eurasian plates" — two continental plates. |

#### Questions 7–10: Sentence Completion (NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS)

| Q | Answer | Source in passage |

| **9** | **subduction** | "the denser oceanic plate is forced beneath the continental plate in a process called subduction." |
| **10** | **San Andreas Fault** | "The San Andreas Fault in California is a well-known example of a transform boundary." |

### Passage 2: The Ottoman Empire (Questions 14–26)

#### Questions 14–18: Matching Headings

| Q | Paragraph | Answer | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|---|
| **14** | B | **ii** | Paragraph B describes the reign of Suleiman I as the "zenith of Ottoman power," the expansion of the empire, and his sweeping legal reforms — a golden age of imperial power and reform. |
| **15** | C | **iv** | Paragraph C describes the devshirme system (elite recruitment of Christian boys trained as soldiers and administrators) and the millet system (administrative structure for managing diverse communities). |
| **16** | D | **vii** | Paragraph D focuses entirely on Ottoman architecture: the Hagia Sophia conversion, the Süleymaniye Mosque, and the Topkapi Palace — the architectural expression of imperial identity. |
| **17** | E | **v** | Paragraph E covers Ottoman literary traditions (Divan poetry) and contributions to cartography (Piri Reis world map) — literary output and geographical knowledge. |
| **18** | F | **vi** | Paragraph F describes the combination of factors that led to the empire's decline from the seventeenth century onwards — a gradual weakening of imperial strength. |

#### Questions 19–23: True / False / Not Given

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| **19** | **TRUE** | The passage states: "Founded around 1299 by Osman I." |
| **20** | **FALSE** | The passage states Suleiman was known in the West as "the Magnificent" but "in the Ottoman world as Suleiman the Lawgiver." His own people called him the Lawgiver, not the Magnificent. |
| **21** | **FALSE** | The passage states the devshirme system "recruited young Christian boys from conquered territories, converted them to Islam." They came from Christian, not Muslim, communities. |
| **22** | **FALSE** | The passage states the Hagia Sophia was converted "from a Byzantine cathedral into a mosque in 1453." It was originally a cathedral, not built by the Ottomans. |
| **23** | **FALSE** | The passage explicitly states "A combination of factors — including military reverses…, the economic disruption… internal corruption, and a series of weak sultans — eroded the empire's strength." Decline had multiple causes, not solely military defeats. |

#### Questions 24–26: Multiple Choice

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| **24** | **B** | The passage states the millet system "granted recognised non-Muslim communities… a significant degree of autonomy in managing their own religious, legal, and educational affairs." This matches option B. |
| **25** | **C** | The passage states the Süleymaniye Mosque was "built under Suleiman's patronage and designed by the master architect Sinan." |
| **26** | **B** | The passage lists "the economic disruption caused by the rise of Atlantic trade routes that bypassed Ottoman-controlled territories" as one of the factors contributing to the seventeenth-century decline. |
