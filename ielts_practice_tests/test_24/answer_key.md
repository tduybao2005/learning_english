### Passage 1: Animal Migration (Q1–13)

**Q1–6: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | TRUE | Paragraph B states: "Studies using miniaturised tracking devices have recorded round trips of approximately 70,000 kilometres, making the Arctic tern the record-holder for the longest known migration of any animal." |
| 2 | FALSE | Paragraph B explicitly states that the wildebeest migration is "driven not by breeding imperatives but by the annual cycle of rainfall and the fresh grass it produces." The primary driver is food/rainfall, not breeding. |
| 3 | TRUE | Paragraph C states: "Monarch butterflies...rely primarily on the sun's position as a time-compensated solar compass." |
| 4 | FALSE | Paragraph C states that magnetoreception is a capacity of "many bird species" and that magnetite crystals occur only "in some cases" — not all migratory animals possess them. |
| 5 | TRUE | Paragraph E describes how "the peak abundance of insects or flowering plants...can shift out of alignment with arrival dates" due to climate change, directly confirming this statement. |
| 6 | NOT GIVEN | Paragraph F discusses the need for international collaboration but makes no reference to international treaties prohibiting habitat destruction within migratory corridors. |

**Q7–10: Gap Fill**

| Q | Answer | Source in Passage |
|---|--------|------------------|
| 7 | magnetic field | Para C: "detect the Earth's magnetic field" |
| 8 | magnetite | Para C: "tiny crystals of the mineral magnetite" |
| 9 | sun's position | Para C: "rely primarily on the sun's position as a time-compensated solar compass" |
| 10 | stopover sites | Para E: "the stopover sites that migrants depend on as critical refuelling and resting points" |

*Note: All answers are no more than two words and appear verbatim in the passage.*

**Q11–13: Short Answer**

| Q | Answer | Source in Passage |
|---|--------|------------------|
| 11 | phenological mismatch | Para E: "a phenomenon ecologists term phenological mismatch" |
| 12 | satellite telemetry | Para F: "Research programmes now combine satellite telemetry, stable isotope analysis, and citizen science networks" |
| 13 | magnetic map | Para D: "effectively reading a two-dimensional magnetic map" |

*Explanations:*
- *Q11: Paragraph E explains that phenological mismatch occurs when climate change causes the timing of food availability to fall out of alignment with migration schedules.*
- *Q12: Paragraph F lists satellite telemetry as the first technology combined with stable isotope analysis for route mapping. Accept "satellite telemetry" (two words).*
- *Q13: Paragraph D describes loggerhead sea turtles as "reading a two-dimensional magnetic map." Accept "magnetic map" (two words).*

### Passage 2: Economic Inequality and Its Consequences (Q14–26)

**Q14–18: Matching Headings**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 14 | i | Paragraph B traces the trajectory of rising Gini coefficients in OECD nations since the 1980s — "the rise of economic disparity in wealthy nations." |
| 15 | iii | Paragraph C identifies technological change, globalisation, tax cuts, and weakening unions as the "principal drivers" — the structural forces driving widening inequality. |
| 16 | iv | Paragraph D discusses consequences for life expectancy, infant mortality, mental health, educational attainment, and social mobility — health, wellbeing, and the opportunity gap. |
| 17 | v | Paragraph E links rising inequality to declining trust in democratic institutions, populism, and political polarisation — inequality's impact on democracy and political trust. |
| 18 | vi | Paragraph F examines policy instruments including minimum wage rises, progressive taxation, social transfers, and education investment — government strategies for narrowing the gap. |

*Distractor headings: vii (crime rates) and viii (cultural explanations) do not match any paragraph in the passage.*

**Q19–23: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 19 | TRUE | Paragraph A: "a coefficient of zero indicates that all income is shared perfectly equally." |
| 20 | NOT GIVEN | Paragraph B describes the US as "often cited as the starkest example" of inequality, but never states it has the highest Gini coefficient of any OECD nation. No ranking is given. |
| 21 | FALSE | Paragraph C states skill-biased technological change has "systematically rewarded high-skilled workers while displacing those in middle-income occupations." It decreased, not increased, demand for middle-income routine workers. |
| 22 | TRUE | Paragraph D: "the statistical correlation between a parent's income and their child's eventual earnings tends to be stronger" in highly unequal societies. |
| 23 | FALSE | Paragraph F states that "evidence from natural experiments suggests that moderate rises do not cause the significant employment reductions that critics predicted, though the debate remains active." The debate is ongoing — this has not been conclusively proved. |

**Q24–26: Multiple Choice**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 24 | B | Paragraph D: "the circumstances of birth play a disproportionately large role in determining life outcomes — a pattern sometimes encapsulated in the phrase 'the Great Gatsby curve.'" This describes the strong link between parental income and children's earnings. |
| 25 | D | Paragraph F: "Investment in early childhood education and access to higher education are widely regarded as the most effective long-term strategies." |
| 26 | C | Paragraph E mentions declining trust (A), populist movements (B), and polarisation (D), but does not mention voter turnout. C is therefore NOT mentioned. |

### Passage 3: Quantum Computing (Q27–40)

**Q27–30: Multiple Choice**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 27 | B | Paragraph B: "at which point it collapses into one definite value." A qubit collapses from superposition into a single state when measured. |
| 28 | C | Paragraph C states that quantum computers could "simulate the behaviour of complex molecules" in "pharmaceutical research and materials science," which "might dramatically accelerate drug discovery." |
| 29 | B | Paragraph D: "RSA, depend for their security on the practical impossibility of factoring very large numbers...Shor's algorithm...could, in principle, factor such numbers exponentially faster." |
| 30 | B | Paragraph F: "critics have noted that these demonstrations address narrow, contrived problems rather than the broad capability needed for practical cryptographic attacks." |

**Q31–36: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 31 | TRUE | Paragraph A: classical bits "can represent either the value zero or the value one" — they can only be in one state at a time. |
| 32 | FALSE | Paragraph B: entanglement affects partner qubits "regardless of the physical distance separating them." Proximity is irrelevant. |
| 33 | TRUE | Paragraph D: "In 1994, mathematician Peter Shor developed a quantum algorithm — now known as Shor's algorithm." |
| 34 | FALSE | Paragraph E: "The error rates of current quantum hardware are far too high for the kind of sustained, complex calculations that would be needed to break RSA encryption." |
| 35 | TRUE | Paragraph G: "In 2024, NIST published its first set of finalised post-quantum standards." |
| 36 | NOT GIVEN | Paragraph G states that "security professionals have coined the term 'Y2Q'" but gives no information about who coined it first or when/where it was first used. |

**Q37–40: Gap Fill**

| Q | Answer | Source in Passage |
|---|--------|------------------|
| 37 | qubits | Para B: "The basic unit of quantum information is the qubit" |
| 38 | superposition | Para B: "can exist in a state known as superposition" |
| 39 | decoherence | Para E: "The chief challenge is decoherence" |
| 40 | post-quantum | Para G: "post-quantum cryptographic algorithms" |

*Note: All answers are one or two words and appear verbatim in the passage. For Q40, accept "post-quantum" (hyphenated) or "post quantum" (two words).*

**Gợi ý cho bài mẫu Task 1 (Process Diagram — Monarch Butterfly Lifecycle):**
- Bắt đầu bằng câu giới thiệu tổng quan về chu kỳ (cyclical process)
- Dùng ngôn ngữ quy trình: *is initiated, subsequently, once this is complete, finally, following this*
- Dùng bị động tự nhiên: *eggs are laid, the larva emerges, the chrysalis is formed*
- So sánh thời lượng giữa các giai đoạn (larval stage longest of first three; migratory adult longest overall)
- Không cần kết luận trong Task 1

**Gợi ý cho bài mẫu Task 2 (Economic Inequality):**
- Cấu trúc 4 đoạn: Introduction → View 1 (inequality as motivation) → View 2 (inequality as harmful) → Conclusion with own opinion
