### Chủ đề 1: Hobbies & Leisure (Sở thích & Thời gian rảnh)

**Q1: What do you enjoy doing in your free time?**

*Sample answer:* I really enjoy reading — mostly non-fiction books about psychology and history. It helps me unwind after a long day at work and I always feel like I've learned something useful. At weekends, I also like going for long walks outdoors.

**Q2: Has your choice of hobbies changed since you were younger?**

*Sample answer:* Definitely. When I was a teenager I was into video games and team sports, but as I've got older I've gravitated towards quieter, more reflective activities. I think it reflects a shift in what I find fulfilling — I value learning and calm more than I used to.

**Q3: Do you think hobbies are important for mental health?**

*Sample answer:* Absolutely. Having an activity you genuinely enjoy gives you a sense of identity and purpose outside of work. Research actually backs this up — people with meaningful leisure activities report higher levels of well-being and lower stress. I certainly notice the difference when I haven't had time for my hobbies.

**Q4: Is there a hobby you've always wanted to try but haven't yet?**

*Sample answer:* Yes — I've always been curious about learning to play a musical instrument, specifically the piano. I love classical music and think playing it would be incredibly satisfying. I just haven't found the time and commitment to start properly yet.

### Chủ đề 2: Friendship (Tình bạn)

**Q5: How important are friendships to you?**

*Sample answer:* Friendships are extremely important to me. Some of my closest friendships have lasted over fifteen years, and those people really know who I am. Having people you can be honest with, and who support you during difficult times, makes a huge difference to your overall happiness and sense of security.

**Q6: Do you prefer having a few close friends or a large social circle?**

*Sample answer:* I prefer a small circle of genuinely close friends over a wide network of acquaintances. Deep friendships require time and investment, and I think spreading yourself too thin makes it hard to maintain real intimacy. Quality, in my view, far outweighs quantity when it comes to relationships.

**Q7: Has social media changed the way people maintain friendships?**

*Sample answer:* It has, in mixed ways. On one hand, it makes it much easier to stay connected with people across distances, which is genuinely valuable. On the other hand, I think it can create a false sense of closeness — liking someone's posts isn't really the same as having a meaningful conversation with them.

**Q8: What makes a good friend, in your opinion?**

*Sample answer:* For me, a good friend is someone who is honest with you even when it's uncomfortable, and who shows up consistently rather than only when things are easy. Reliability and genuine interest in your life matter far more to me than someone being entertaining or exciting to be around.

### Chủ đề 3: Stress & Wellbeing (Căng thẳng & Sức khoẻ tinh thần)

**Q9: What kinds of things cause you stress in daily life?**

*Sample answer:* Work deadlines and time pressure are my main sources of stress. When I feel like I have too much to do and not enough time, my focus suffers and I can become irritable. I've found that planning ahead and breaking tasks into smaller steps helps significantly.

**Q10: How do you usually manage or reduce stress?**

*Sample answer:* I have a few strategies that work for me. Exercise is probably the most effective — even a thirty-minute run clears my head considerably. I also practice brief mindfulness meditation in the mornings, which helps me start the day with a calmer mindset rather than jumping straight into a reactive mode.

**Q11: Do you think people today are more stressed than previous generations?**

*Sample answer:* I think the nature of stress has changed rather than necessarily its quantity. We face different pressures — information overload, financial insecurity, and constant connectivity — that previous generations didn't experience. Whether that's more or less stressful overall is debatable, but it's certainly a different kind of challenge.

**Q12: What do you think governments or employers could do to improve people's well-being?**

*Sample answer:* Employers could make a meaningful difference by normalising flexible working hours, offering mental health support programmes, and discouraging a culture of overwork. Governments could invest more in accessible mental health services and public spaces for recreation. Small structural changes can have a significant cumulative effect on population well-being.

## PART 2: Cue Card

### Đề bài

**Describe a person who has had a positive influence on your life.**

*You should say:*
- *who this person is*
- *how you know them*
- *what they did or said that influenced you*
- *and explain why their influence has been positive for you.*

*Bạn có 1 phút để chuẩn bị và nên nói trong 1–2 phút.*

---

### Bài mẫu (~200 từ)

The person who has had the most positive influence on my life is my secondary school English teacher, Mr. Nguyen. I was around fifteen when I first had him as a teacher, and I remember feeling quite disengaged with school at the time — not because I lacked ability, but because I hadn't yet found something that genuinely interested me.

Mr. Nguyen noticed that I liked writing and encouraged me to enter a regional essay competition. I remember him saying something that has stayed with me ever since: "You think clearly, but you don't yet trust your own thinking. That will come if you practise." It was the first time an adult outside my family had expressed genuine confidence in my intellectual ability, and it had a profound effect on my self-perception.

I entered the competition, placed third, and that small success gave me the confidence to pursue language and communication more seriously. It ultimately shaped my choice of university study and, eventually, my career.

What makes his influence so lasting is not that he pushed me toward a specific outcome, but that he helped me see something in myself that I hadn't recognised. That kind of encouragement — specific, honest, and well-timed — is genuinely transformative. I've tried to apply the same approach in my own interactions with others ever since.

## PART 3: Câu hỏi thảo luận

*Giám khảo sẽ hỏi các câu hỏi trừu tượng hơn liên quan đến chủ đề trong Part 2.*

---

**Q1: Why do you think role models are important for young people?**

*Sample answer:* Role models give young people a concrete image of what is possible for them. Abstract advice can be hard to act on, but seeing someone who has actually achieved something you admire makes the path feel more real and attainable. Role models also implicitly transmit values, attitudes, and ways of approaching challenges that are difficult to teach directly.

**Q2: Do you think celebrities make good role models?**

*Sample answer:* Some do and some don't — it really depends on the individual. Celebrities who are transparent about their struggles, who use their platform responsibly, and who demonstrate genuine resilience can be excellent role models. However, many celebrities project an artificial image of effortless success, which can create unrealistic expectations and even damage the self-esteem of young people who compare themselves to an illusion.

**Q3: How has social media changed the way people are influenced by others?**

*Sample answer:* Social media has dramatically expanded the range of people who can influence us, while also making influence more constant and ambient. We're no longer only shaped by people in our immediate community; we're exposed to the values, lifestyles, and opinions of millions of strangers every day. The risk is that this can create conformity rather than genuine inspiration — people may adopt behaviours or aspirations simply because they appear popular, not because they're genuinely meaningful.

**Q4: Do you think parents or peers have a greater influence on teenagers?**

*Sample answer:* The research generally suggests that both matter enormously, but in different ways. Parents tend to have a stronger influence on fundamental values, long-term aspirations, and identity, while peers have a more immediate influence on behaviour, attitudes, and self-image in the short term. During adolescence, the peer influence often feels more powerful simply because fitting in with the social group becomes a developmental priority.

**Q5: What role do you think schools should play in promoting mental health awareness?**

*Sample answer:* Schools are uniquely positioned to normalise conversations about mental health because they reach young people during a formative period and in a structured setting. Integrating well-being education into the curriculum — not just as a one-off lesson but as an ongoing, practical element of school life — can help young people develop emotional literacy, recognise warning signs in themselves and others, and reduce the stigma that still surrounds mental health conditions.

**Q6: Why do you think some people are reluctant to seek help for mental health problems?**

*Sample answer:* Stigma is the most significant barrier — the fear of being judged as weak, unstable, or different. This is compounded by a lack of awareness, so some people don't recognise that what they're experiencing is a mental health issue at all. There are also practical barriers such as cost, waiting times, and limited availability of services, which can discourage people even when they've overcome the psychological resistance to seeking help.

**Q7: Do you think modern life has a negative effect on mental health?**

*Sample answer:* There are certainly aspects of modern life that create genuine psychological strain — chronic time pressure, economic insecurity, social comparison amplified by social media, and a weakening of community ties. However, modern life also offers unprecedented access to mental health information, support networks, and professional resources. I think the net effect depends heavily on individual circumstances, though I'd agree that for many people, the pace and structure of contemporary life is not particularly conducive to sustained well-being.

**Q8: Is happiness a personal responsibility or something that society should support?**

*Sample answer:* It's both, and I think framing it as one or the other creates a false choice. Individuals clearly have agency over their habits, attitudes, and relationships, all of which research consistently links to well-being. But individuals also exist within social structures that can either facilitate or undermine that agency — poverty, discrimination, and lack of community support make it genuinely harder to flourish. A responsible society creates conditions in which individual effort can actually bear fruit.

**Q9: How do you think cultural background affects a person's understanding of happiness?**

*Sample answer:* Cultural background shapes what we're even taught to value, so it profoundly affects how we define and experience happiness. In more individualistic cultures, happiness tends to be associated with personal achievement and self-expression, whereas in collectivist cultures, it's more closely tied to social harmony and fulfilling obligations to others. These aren't just differences in expression; they reflect genuinely different conceptual frameworks for what a good life consists of.

**Q10: Do you think it is possible to measure happiness scientifically?**

*Sample answer:* Partially, yes. Researchers can measure certain dimensions of happiness with reasonable reliability — subjective well-being through self-report scales, physiological markers of stress and positive affect, and even neural correlates of positive emotion through brain imaging. However, happiness as a subjective lived experience will always contain elements that resist full quantification. What science can do is identify patterns and predictors of well-being with greater precision than intuition alone, even if it cannot capture the full richness of what it means to live well.
