# IELTS Practice Test 29 — Writing
**Chủ đề: Health & Medicine**

## Task 1

**Đề bài:**

*The diagram below shows the process by which a vaccine is developed and approved for public use. Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.*

*Write at least 150 words.*

---

**Hướng dẫn:** Đây là sơ đồ quy trình (process diagram) mô tả các bước phát triển và phê duyệt vắc-xin. Bài viết cần trình bày đầy đủ các giai đoạn theo trình tự, dùng ngôn ngữ thụ động và từ nối chỉ trình tự.

---

### Bài mẫu (Band 7–8, ~175 từ)

The diagram illustrates the sequential stages involved in developing and gaining approval for a new vaccine, from initial laboratory research through to public distribution.

The process begins with **exploratory research**, during which scientists identify a target disease and study the pathogen responsible. This is followed by the **pre-clinical stage**, where candidate vaccines are tested on cell cultures and animals to assess safety and immune response. If results are promising, the vaccine enters **clinical trials**, which are conducted in three phases. Phase I involves a small group of healthy volunteers to evaluate safety and dosage. Phase II expands testing to hundreds of participants to examine efficacy and side effects, while Phase III involves thousands of participants across diverse populations to confirm effectiveness on a larger scale.

Once clinical trials are completed successfully, the vaccine manufacturer submits a **regulatory dossier** to the national medicines authority for review. Upon approval, the vaccine undergoes **large-scale manufacturing** before being distributed to healthcare providers and administered to the public. Post-approval **surveillance** continues to monitor long-term safety.

Overall, the entire process is highly systematic, with each stage dependent on the successful completion of the previous one.

## Task 2

**Đề bài:**

*Governments should make it compulsory for all citizens to follow a healthy lifestyle. To what extent do you agree or disagree?*

*Write at least 250 words.*

---

**Hướng dẫn:** Đây là dạng bài "opinion essay." Bạn cần trình bày rõ ràng quan điểm của mình (đồng ý, không đồng ý, hoặc một phần đồng ý), phân tích các lý do, và kết thúc bằng kết luận nhất quán với lập trường đã nêu.

---

### Bài mẫu (Band 7–8, ~280 từ)

While the appeal of government-mandated healthy living is understandable given the enormous burden of lifestyle-related disease on public health systems, I largely disagree that compelling citizens to adopt specific health behaviours is either practical or ethically justifiable.

Proponents argue that unhealthy lifestyles impose costs on society as a whole. When individuals develop preventable conditions such as type 2 diabetes or heart disease, the financial burden falls on taxpayers through publicly funded healthcare. From this perspective, mandating healthier choices — restricting dietary options, requiring physical activity — could be framed as protecting the collective good.

However, personal autonomy must be respected in a democratic society. Adults have the right to make informed choices about their own bodies and lifestyles, even when those choices carry health risks. A government empowered to legislate diet and exercise habits today could justify increasingly invasive interventions tomorrow, setting a troubling precedent for state control over private life. Furthermore, defining precisely what constitutes a "healthy lifestyle" is deeply contested; cultural, socioeconomic, and medical variables make any universal standard deeply problematic.

More importantly, coercion is unlikely to be effective. Research in behavioural psychology demonstrates that lasting changes in health behaviour are driven by intrinsic motivation and supportive environments, not legal compulsion. Punishing citizens for unhealthy choices is more likely to generate resentment than genuine lifestyle change.

Rather than compulsion, governments should invest in education, subsidise healthy food, create safe spaces for physical activity, and restrict aggressive marketing of unhealthy products. These measures empower individuals to make healthier decisions without infringing on their fundamental freedoms.

In conclusion, mandatory healthy lifestyle laws are misguided both ethically and practically. Governments achieve better outcomes through positive incentives and enabling environments than through legal enforcement.
