## PHẦN ĐỌC (READING) — Đáp án chi tiết

### Passage 1: The Space Race (Q1–13)

**Q1–6: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | TRUE | Paragraph A states the competition "intensified through the 1950s as both nations developed long-range missiles," confirming rivalry existed before Sputnik in 1957. |
| 2 | TRUE | Paragraph B states Sputnik 1 weighed "83.6 kilograms," which is less than 100 kilograms. |
| 3 | FALSE | Paragraph C states Gagarin "completed a single orbit of Earth," not three orbits. |
| 4 | TRUE | Gagarin flew on 12 April 1961 (Paragraph C); Kennedy's address was delivered "in May 1961" (Paragraph C) — after Gagarin's flight. |
| 5 | FALSE | Paragraph D states the Apollo programme "employed more than 400,000" workers — under 500,000, so "more than half a million" is contradicted. |
| 6 | TRUE | Paragraph E states the event was "broadcast live to an estimated 600 million television viewers," which matches the statement exactly. |

### Passage 2: Gothic Architecture in Medieval Europe (Q14–26)

**Q14–18: Matching Headings**

| Q | Paragraph | Answer | Heading Text |
|---|---|---|---|
| 14 | B | i | The engineering role of the pointed arch |
| 15 | C | v | External supports as a structural solution |
| 16 | D | vii | A framework for vaulted ceilings |
| 17 | E | ii | Light as a spiritual and instructional medium |
| 18 | F | iv | Iconic examples across medieval Europe |

**Q19–23: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| 19 | FALSE | Paragraph A clearly states Gothic architecture originated "in the Île-de-France region" of France, not Italy. |
| 20 | TRUE | Paragraph C states flying buttresses meant "builders could construct walls of remarkable slenderness" — directly matching the statement. |
| 21 | FALSE | Paragraph E states windows served "not merely as a decorative element but as a theological medium" and "also performed an instructional role." They were not used solely for decoration. |
| 22 | FALSE | Paragraph F states Notre-Dame de Paris was "begun in 1163" and Chartres was "completed largely between 1194 and 1220," meaning Notre-Dame was begun earlier, not Chartres. |
| 23 | TRUE | Paragraph G states the Neo-Gothic revival was "particularly in Britain and Germany" — directly confirmed. |

**Q24–26: Multiple Choice**

| Q | Answer | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 24 | B | Paragraph B: the pointed arch "directs the load primarily downward, reducing the lateral thrust on the structure." This matches option B exactly. |
| 25 | C | Paragraph D: the ribbed vault "concentrated structural forces along discrete stone ribs, leaving the panels between them free to be filled with lighter material. This reduced the overall weight of the ceiling." Option C captures this accurately. |
| 26 | B | Paragraph G: architects "arguing that the style embodied both structural honesty and moral virtue" — a direct match to option B. |

### Passage 3: Climate Change and Agricultural Systems (Q27–40)

**Q27–30: Multiple Choice**

| Q | Answer | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| 27 | C | Paragraph A: "further warming is inevitable regardless of future emissions reductions." Option C states this accurately; options A and B contradict the passage; D conflates current observed warming with a projection. |
| 28 | B | Paragraph B: heat stress "reduces the efficiency of photosynthesis and accelerates the ripening process, curtailing the period during which plants accumulate dry matter." This matches option B directly. |
| 29 | C | Paragraph C: "soils in these northern regions are often thin, acidic, or nutrient-poor, and the infrastructure for large-scale farming is largely absent." Option C is a direct paraphrase. |
| 30 | C | Paragraph E: "yield improvements of up to 25 per cent compared with conventional cultivars under drought conditions." Option C matches this figure precisely. |

**Q31–36: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| 31 | TRUE | Paragraph A states the IPCC has concluded temperatures have risen "by approximately 1.1 degrees Celsius above pre-industrial levels" — confirmed. |
| 32 | FALSE | Paragraph B states that "In temperate regions, modest warming may initially increase yields for some crops" before reversing. Yields do not fall immediately and permanently; they may first rise. |
| 33 | TRUE | Paragraph D: "increase crop water demand through transpiration" — directly confirmed. |
| 34 | NOT GIVEN | Paragraph F says wealthier nations have "greater capacity to invest in adaptation" but does not compare actual investment levels of sub-Saharan African governments versus wealthier governments. |
| 35 | TRUE | Paragraph F: the FAO estimates that "achieving the goal of zero hunger by 2030 will require a 70 per cent increase in global food production." |
| 36 | FALSE | Paragraph G describes the Paris Climate Accord as seeking "to limit future warming" and states whether efforts "will prove adequate" is "an open question." The passage makes no claim that food insecurity has been eliminated. |

**Q37–40: Gap Fill**

| Q | Answer | Location in passage |
|---|---|---|
| 37 | staple | Paragraph B: "yields of staple crops such as wheat, rice, and maize" |
| 38 | photosynthesis | Paragraph B: "reduces the efficiency of photosynthesis" |
| 39 | drought-resistant | Paragraph E: "Drought-resistant crop varieties, developed through both conventional plant breeding and genetic modification" |
| 40 | precision irrigation | Paragraph E: "precision irrigation systems that minimise water waste" |
