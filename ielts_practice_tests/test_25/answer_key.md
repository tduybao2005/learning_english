## PHẦN ĐỌC (READING) — Đáp án chi tiết

### Passage 1: Antibiotic Resistance: A Growing Global Crisis (Q1–13)

**Q1–6: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | FALSE | Paragraph A states that "Fleming initially struggled to isolate and stabilise the active compound." It was Florey and Chain who transformed penicillin into a clinically viable medicine, not Fleming. |
| 2 | TRUE | Paragraph A explicitly states that "the subsequent work of Howard Florey and Ernst Boris Chain at Oxford during the Second World War transformed penicillin into a clinically viable medicine." |
| 3 | FALSE | Paragraph B states that fluoroquinolones "target DNA replication." It is penicillin and related beta-lactam drugs that "inhibit the synthesis of the bacterial cell wall." |
| 4 | TRUE | Paragraph C states that "bacteria can acquire resistance genes from other bacteria through a process called horizontal gene transfer" and that "a resistance trait that evolves in one bacterial species can spread rapidly to others." |
| 5 | FALSE | Paragraph D states that antibiotics in livestock farming are used "often as growth promoters rather than therapeutic agents" — meaning not primarily to treat sick animals. |
| 6 | TRUE | Paragraph E states: "A comprehensive 2016 review commissioned by the United Kingdom government estimated that, if current trends continue, drug-resistant infections could kill ten million people per year by 2050." |

**Q7–10: Gap Fill (NO MORE THAN THREE WORDS)**

| Q | Answer |
|---|--------|
| 7 | genetic mutations |
| 8 | horizontal gene transfer |
| 9 | plasmids |
| 10 | selective pressure |

*Explanations:*
- *Q7: Paragraph C: "Resistance can arise through spontaneous genetic mutations that alter the bacterial target of an antibiotic."*
- *Q8: Paragraph C: "bacteria can acquire resistance genes from other bacteria through a process called horizontal gene transfer."*
- *Q9: Paragraph C: "mobile genetic elements known as plasmids."*
- *Q10: Paragraph C: "The use of antibiotics creates a powerful selective pressure: susceptible bacteria are killed, but resistant strains survive and multiply."*

**Q11–13: Short Answer (NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS)**

| Q | Answer |
|---|--------|
| 11 | beta-lactam (drugs) |
| 12 | cell walls |
| 13 | phage therapy |

*Explanations:*
- *Q11: Paragraph B: "Penicillin and related beta-lactam drugs inhibit the synthesis of the bacterial cell wall."*
- *Q12: Paragraph B: "Because human cells lack cell walls and possess fundamentally different ribosomes from bacteria, these drugs can selectively kill bacterial invaders while leaving human tissues largely unharmed."*
- *Q13: Paragraph F: "A particularly promising alternative is phage therapy, which uses bacteriophages — viruses that specifically infect and kill bacteria."*

### Passage 2: The Silk Road: Commerce, Culture, and Connection (Q14–26)

**Q14–18: Matching Headings to Paragraphs**

| Q | Paragraph | Answer | Explanation |
|---|-----------|--------|-------------|
| 14 | B | **v** | Paragraph B describes the formal establishment of the Silk Road "under imperial authority" — Emperor Wu of the Han Dynasty dispatching Zhang Qian in ~130 BCE. |
| 15 | C | **iii** | Paragraph C catalogues the commodities and goods exchanged: silk, spices, porcelain, paper, gunpowder, glassware, and cultivated plants. |
| 16 | D | **i** | Paragraph D describes the spread of Buddhism and Islam along the route — the Silk Road as "a conduit for religious and philosophical ideas." |
| 17 | E | **vii** | Paragraph E describes the Black Death spreading via Silk Road trade networks — "the catastrophic public health consequences of interconnected trade." |
| 18 | F | **vi** | Paragraph F explains how Portuguese maritime exploration opened sea routes that ended the commercial dominance of the overland Silk Road. |

**Q19–23: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 19 | TRUE | Paragraph A: "The term 'Silk Road' (Seidenstraße) was coined by German geographer Ferdinand von Richthofen in 1877." |
| 20 | FALSE | Paragraph B states: "Though the military alliance Zhang Qian sought was never achieved." The question asks about success; the passage states clearly it was not achieved. |
| 21 | TRUE | Paragraph C: "produced by a technique so jealously guarded that the penalty for revealing its secrets to foreigners was reportedly death." |
| 22 | TRUE | Paragraph D states Buddhism spread "during the first centuries of the Common Era," while Islam reached the Tarim Basin oases "by the tenth century." Buddhism arrived in China earlier. |
| 23 | NOT GIVEN | Paragraph E states the Black Death killed "approximately one third of the European population" — not one third of the world's population. The statement about the world's population is neither confirmed nor contradicted. |

**Q24–26: Multiple Choice**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 24 | B | Paragraph B states Emperor Wu sent Zhang Qian to "forge alliances against the Xiongnu" and "to source the powerful 'heavenly horses' of Ferghana." Both motivations are captured in option B. |
| 25 | C | Paragraph F attributes the decline primarily to Portuguese explorers opening a sea route to India, which offered cheaper, safer, and higher-capacity alternatives to overland trade. |
| 26 | B | Paragraph G states that critics have questioned whether the BRI "primarily extends Chinese geopolitical influence and creates debt dependencies among participating states." |

### Passage 3: Digital Privacy and the Age of Surveillance (Q27–40)

**Q27–30: Multiple Choice**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 27 | C | Paragraph A mentions geographic location, browsing history, sleep patterns and heart rates, and facial geometry — but does NOT mention voice recordings. |
| 28 | C | Paragraph B: "platforms such as Google and Facebook sell predictions about user behaviour to advertisers." |
| 29 | C | Paragraph C: the GDPR "requires organisations to obtain explicit, informed consent before collecting most categories of personal data." |
| 30 | B | Paragraph E: "independent studies have documented significantly higher error rates for darker-skinned individuals and women than for lighter-skinned men." |

**Q31–36: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 31 | TRUE | Paragraph B: "Shoshana Zuboff, in her 2019 book *The Age of Surveillance Capitalism*." |
| 32 | FALSE | Paragraph C states the GDPR governs data processing "regardless of where the processing occurs" — it applies to data of EU citizens even when processed outside the EU, not only to organisations headquartered within the EU. |
| 33 | NOT GIVEN | Paragraph D identifies Snowden only as a "former National Security Agency contractor." No mention is made of prior CIA employment. |
| 34 | FALSE | Paragraph E explicitly states that "independent studies have documented significantly higher error rates for darker-skinned individuals and women" — the technology does not perform equally across all demographic groups. |
| 35 | TRUE | Paragraph F: "the 2017 Equifax breach compromised the financial and personal data of approximately 147 million Americans." |
| 36 | FALSE | Paragraph G states that individuals have "a qualified right to have certain links de-indexed" — not an absolute or unconditional right. The right must be balanced against public interest, press freedom, and the historical record. |

**Q37–40: Gap Fill (NO MORE THAN THREE WORDS)**

| Q | Answer |
|---|--------|
| 37 | terms of service |
| 38 | GDPR |
| 39 | turnover |
| 40 | data processing |

*Explanations:*
- *Q37: Paragraph B: "the data is extracted through complex terms of service that few read and fewer fully understand."*
- *Q38: Paragraph C: "the European Union's General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)."*
- *Q39: Paragraph C: "up to four per cent of global annual turnover or €20 million, whichever is greater."*
- *Q40: Paragraph G: "search engine results constituted a form of data processing subject to EU data protection law."*
