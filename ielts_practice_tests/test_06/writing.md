## Task 1 (20 minutes — minimum 150 words)

**Prompt:** "The table below shows employment distribution across four sectors in four countries in 2020."

**Data:**

| Sector        | UK  | Germany | Vietnam | Kenya |
|---------------|-----|---------|---------|-------|
| Agriculture   | 1%  | 2%      | 38%     | 61%   |
| Manufacturing | 10% | 24%     | 16%     | 4%    |
| Services      | 80% | 69%     | 42%     | 32%   |
| Other         | 9%  | 5%      | 4%      | 3%    |

---

### Model Answer — Band 7.5 (~175 words)

The table compares the distribution of employment across agriculture, manufacturing, services, and other sectors in the UK, Germany, Vietnam, and Kenya in 2020.

Overall, the data reveals a clear pattern linked to levels of economic development: highly developed economies such as the UK and Germany are dominated by service sector employment, while Kenya's workforce remains concentrated in agriculture. Vietnam occupies an intermediate position between these extremes.

The service sector accounted for the largest share of employment in the UK, at a striking 80%, followed by Germany at 69%. By contrast, Kenya's service sector employed only 32% of its workforce, with the majority — 61% — engaged in agriculture. Germany stood out for its significant manufacturing base at 24%, reflecting its reputation as an industrial economy, compared to just 10% in the UK and 16% in Vietnam. Vietnam was the only country where agriculture (38%) remained competitive with services (42%), suggesting it is in an active phase of economic transition. Agricultural employment in both the UK and Germany was negligible, at 1% and 2% respectively.

## Task 2 (40 minutes — minimum 250 words)

**Prompt:** "The government should invest more in public transport rather than in building new roads. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"

---

### Model Answer — Band 7.5 (~275 words)

The question of whether public funds are better spent on public transport or road infrastructure is one that city planners and governments worldwide continue to debate. I strongly agree that investment in public transport should take precedence, for both environmental and economic reasons.

The most compelling argument in favour of public transport investment is its potential to reduce carbon emissions. Private vehicles are among the largest sources of urban air pollution and greenhouse gas emissions. High-quality public transport — particularly electrified rail and bus rapid transit systems — can move far more passengers per unit of energy than private cars, dramatically reducing the per-capita environmental footprint of urban mobility. Cities such as Singapore and Tokyo demonstrate that excellent public transport systems can serve dense urban populations efficiently while keeping car ownership rates comparatively low.

Building more roads, by contrast, has consistently failed to solve congestion in the long term. Research in transportation planning has documented the phenomenon of "induced demand", whereby new road capacity generates additional traffic rather than relieving existing congestion, as drivers alter routes and behaviour to exploit the increased road space. Widening roads may provide temporary relief, but the underlying demand for car travel typically grows to fill available capacity within a decade.

In conclusion, investing in public transport is not only a more environmentally responsible choice but also a more economically rational one in the long run. Governments should prioritise expanding, modernising, and making affordable the public transport networks that allow cities to function without dependence on private vehicles. Road construction should be reserved for genuinely underserved areas where public transport is not viable.

### Essay Structure Template (Agree/Disagree Essay)

**Paragraph 1 — Introduction:**
- Paraphrase the topic (do NOT copy the prompt)
- State your position clearly: "I strongly agree / I largely disagree / I partially agree..."

**Paragraph 2 — Main argument supporting position (strongest reason):**
- Topic sentence → Explanation → Evidence/Example → Concluding link

**Paragraph 3 — Second argument OR acknowledge complexity:**
- If you strongly agree: add a second reason supporting your view
- If you partially agree: acknowledge the other side, then explain why your view still holds

**Paragraph 4 — Conclusion:**
- Restate position in different words + summarise key reasoning (2–3 sentences)
