### Passage 2: The Gutenberg Press (Q14–26)

**Q14–18: Matching Headings**

| Q | Answer | Heading |
|---|--------|---------|
| 14 | iv | The technical basis of Gutenberg's method |
| 15 | viii | The rapid dissemination of print technology across a continent |
| 16 | ii | The contribution of print to religious upheaval |
| 17 | v | The printed word as a tool for scientific progress |
| 18 | iii | How printing shaped the development of national languages |

**Q19–23: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 19 | TRUE | "copied by hand, predominantly by scribes working in monastic scriptoria — the dedicated writing rooms" |
| 20 | FALSE | The passage states approximately 180 copies were produced, not approximately 200 |
| 21 | NOT GIVEN | No information about Gutenberg's personal wealth appears anywhere in the passage |
| 22 | TRUE | "a document produced in one region might be scarcely intelligible to a literate reader from another part of the same country" |
| 23 | FALSE | The press "fundamentally altered the balance of power between Rome and its critics" — it undermined, not maintained, Church authority |

**Q24–26: Multiple Choice**

| Q | Answer | Reason |
|---|--------|--------|
| 24 | B | "The commercial potential of the new medium attracted entrepreneurs and investors" |
| 25 | B | "Standardised printed editions ensured that scholars in different countries were reading identical texts" |
| 26 | B | "Both technologies fundamentally disrupted established gatekeepers...And both generated anxieties about misinformation" |

### Passage 3: Social Media and Adolescent Mental Health (Q27–40)

**Q27–30: Multiple Choice**

| Q | Answer | Reason |
|---|--------|--------|
| 27 | C | "teenagers aged thirteen to seventeen spend an average of three to four hours per day on social media platforms" |
| 28 | B | "The relationship is particularly pronounced among adolescent girls" |
| 29 | C | "algorithms...amplify extreme or aspirational content, reinforcing social comparison dynamics" |
| 30 | C | "the Australian government passed legislation imposing a minimum age of sixteen for social media platform access" |

**Q31–36: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 31 | TRUE | "rates of adolescent anxiety and depression in many high-income countries began rising sharply around 2012" |
| 32 | FALSE | "whether social media causes mental health problems or merely correlates with them remains a subject of genuine scientific debate" — causation is not definitively proven |
| 33 | TRUE | "adolescents who experience cyberbullying are at significantly elevated risk of depression, self-harm, and suicidal ideation" |
| 34 | NOT GIVEN | Instagram is never specifically named in the passage |
| 35 | TRUE | "checking of social media notifications...has been shown to delay sleep onset...blue light...suppresses melatonin production" |
| 36 | NOT GIVEN | The passage says researchers "continue to debate" the issue; no mention of majority support for any particular position |
