## TASK 1

**Time recommended: 20 minutes**
**Minimum words: 150**

*You should spend about 20 minutes on this task.*

**The line graph below shows the average number of hours of sleep per night among adults aged 18–65 in three countries between 1990 and 2020.**

**Summarise the information by selecting and reporting the main features, and make comparisons where relevant.**

---

**DATA:**

| Year | Country A (hours) | Country B (hours) | Country C (hours) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1990 | 7.8 | 7.2 | 6.8 |
| 2000 | 7.4 | 7.0 | 6.5 |
| 2010 | 7.1 | 6.8 | 6.3 |
| 2020 | 6.9 | 6.7 | 6.2 |

*(Note: WHO recommends a minimum of 7 hours of sleep per night for adults)*

---

### Model Answer — Band 7.5 (~175 words)

The line graph illustrates trends in the average duration of sleep per night among adults in three countries over a thirty-year period from 1990 to 2020.

Overall, average sleep duration declined in all three countries throughout the period, though the rate and extent of decline varied. Country A consistently recorded the highest sleep averages, while Country C reported the lowest figures in every year surveyed.

In 1990, Country A's adults averaged 7.8 hours of sleep — the highest of the three — compared with 7.2 hours in Country B and 6.8 hours in Country C. By 2020, all three countries had experienced notable reductions: Country A fell to 6.9 hours, Country B to 6.7 hours, and Country C to just 6.2 hours. The most rapid decline occurred in Country A between 1990 and 2000, when average sleep time dropped by 0.4 hours — a steeper decrease than in subsequent decades. Country C's figures remained consistently below the WHO's recommended minimum of 7 hours throughout the entire thirty-year period, while Country A's average only fell below this threshold after 2010.

In summary, declining sleep duration was a universal trend across all three countries, with Country C's population consistently the most sleep-deprived across the entire period.

## TASK 2

**Time recommended: 40 minutes**
**Minimum words: 250**

*You should spend about 40 minutes on this task.*

**Write about the following topic:**

> *Some people believe that modern lifestyles are causing serious harm to people's mental health. Others argue that people are simply more willing to discuss mental health openly, which makes it appear that mental health problems are more common than they actually are.*
>
> *Discuss both views and give your own opinion.*

**Give reasons for your answer and include any relevant examples from your own knowledge or experience.**

---

### Model Answer — Band 7.5 (~280 words)

The apparent rise in mental health problems in recent decades has generated genuine debate: is modern life actually making people more psychologically unwell, or are we simply becoming more willing to acknowledge difficulties that have always existed? Having considered both perspectives, I believe both factors are real and operate simultaneously — but the evidence suggests that genuine increases in anxiety and depression, particularly among young people, cannot be explained by better reporting alone.

Those who argue that greater openness rather than a genuine increase accounts for the apparent rise in mental health conditions make a credible point. In previous generations, significant stigma surrounding mental illness discouraged people from seeking help or even acknowledging difficulties to themselves. Conditions such as depression and post-traumatic stress disorder were widely undiagnosed, and those affected suffered in silence. Improved public awareness campaigns, greater media representation, and the efforts of mental health advocates have made it more socially acceptable to seek help, generating a legitimate increase in reported cases without necessarily reflecting an underlying increase in actual prevalence.

Nevertheless, a growing body of evidence points to genuine and measurable deterioration in mental health, particularly among adolescents in high-income countries. The convergence of several distinctive features of modern life — the displacement of face-to-face socialising by screen-based interaction, chronic sleep deprivation driven by technology use and work demands, economic uncertainty and precariousness among young adults, the always-on connectivity of smartphones that blurs the boundary between work and rest, and the comparison-driven dynamics of social media — creates a set of pressures that previous generations did not face in the same form or intensity. Longitudinal studies tracking the same populations over time show real increases in reported symptoms of anxiety and depression that are difficult to attribute solely to changes in reporting behaviour.

In conclusion, both greater openness and genuine environmental pressures are contributing to current patterns of mental health. However, the scale and nature of changes documented in rigorous longitudinal research suggest that modern life is, for many people, making psychological wellbeing genuinely harder to maintain — a problem that requires policy responses, not merely better diagnosis.

### WRITING TASK 2 — DISCUSSION ESSAY STRUCTURE GUIDE

**Paragraph 1 — Introduction (~50 words)**
- Paraphrase the question
- State your position briefly
- Outline what the essay will discuss

**Paragraph 2 — View A (~80–100 words)**
- Introduce View A with a discourse marker ("Those who argue that...")
- Provide 1–2 supporting reasons
- Optional: example or evidence
- Acknowledge the credibility of this view if you disagree with it

**Paragraph 3 — View B + Your Opinion (~120–140 words)**
- Introduce counterargument with a discourse marker ("Nevertheless...", "However...")
- Present View B with reasons and evidence
- Make your position explicit ("I believe that..." or weave it naturally into the argument)
- Strongest evidence/examples here

**Paragraph 4 — Conclusion (~40–50 words)**
- Restate your position (in different words)
- Brief summary of why
- Optional: broader implication or call to action
