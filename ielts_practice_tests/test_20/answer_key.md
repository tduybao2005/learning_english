## PHẦN ĐỌC (READING) — Đáp án chi tiết

### Passage 1: Archaeological Methods and the Study of Prehistory (Q1–13)

**Q1–6: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 1 | FALSE | The passage states stratigraphy establishes *relative* chronologies — it determines which objects predate others "without necessarily knowing the actual age in calendar years." It does not give exact calendar ages. |
| 2 | TRUE | Paragraph B explicitly states that Sir William Flinders Petrie "applied it [stratigraphy] systematically to Egyptian sites." |
| 3 | TRUE | Paragraph C states "The development of radiocarbon dating in the late 1940s by American chemist Willard Libby." |
| 4 | FALSE | Paragraph C states that accelerator mass spectrometry "requires only a few milligrams of material" — meaning it requires *less* material, not more, than the original technique. |
| 5 | TRUE | Paragraph D states dendrochronology "provides a precise felling date accurate to a single calendar year." |
| 6 | FALSE | The passage states the tomb was excavated in 1922, not 1920. |

**Q7–10: Gap Fill**

| Q | Answer |
|---|--------|
| 7 | carbon-14 |
| 8 | half-life |
| 9 | accelerator mass spectrometry |
| 10 | growth ring |

*Note: Q9 is three words; the instruction says NO MORE THAN TWO WORDS, so accept "mass spectrometry" or the full phrase "accelerator mass spectrometry" depending on whether students interpret "accelerator mass spectrometry" as the named technique. In this passage, the intended answer is **accelerator mass spectrometry** (three words — examiners would adjust the word limit instruction to "NO MORE THAN THREE WORDS" in a real test; for practice purposes, accept the full term).*

| Q | Answer |

### Passage 2: The Global Transition to Renewable Energy (Q14–26)

**Q14–18: Matching Paragraphs**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 14 | F | Paragraph F discusses the need to redesign grid architecture, control systems, and market rules to integrate variable generation. |
| 15 | B | Paragraph B states that in 2023, renewables supplied around 30% of global electricity, and solar and wind accounted for approximately 13%. |
| 16 | D | Paragraph D explains intermittency — that solar and wind output is determined by weather conditions and cannot be controlled. |
| 17 | E | Paragraph E describes the approximately 97% cost decline of lithium-ion batteries between 1991 and 2023. |
| 18 | C | Paragraph C attributes cost reductions to "manufacturing scale, technological improvements in panel and turbine efficiency, and competitive procurement processes." |

**Q19–23: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 19 | TRUE | Paragraph B states: "In 2023, solar and wind together accounted for approximately 13 per cent of global electricity generation." |
| 20 | FALSE | Paragraph B says Denmark generates more than 50% from wind, not solar. Uruguay generates more than 50% from solar. |
| 21 | TRUE | Paragraph C states: "The cost of utility-scale solar photovoltaic power has fallen by more than 90 per cent since 2010." |
| 22 | FALSE | Paragraph E states that "current battery technology is generally suited to storage durations of a few hours" and that seasonal storage "remains a major unsolved challenge." |
| 23 | NOT GIVEN | The passage states 196 parties "committed" to the Paris Agreement but does not use the word "signed," and does not specify whether all 196 signed it. The word "adopted" is used, not "signed." Accept NOT GIVEN. |

**Q24–26: Multiple Choice**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 24 | B | Paragraph D defines intermittency as the challenge of matching supply with demand when "solar and wind output is determined by weather conditions that cannot be controlled." |
| 25 | B | Paragraph G states that at COP28, "nations pledged to triple global renewable energy capacity by 2030." |
| 26 | C | Paragraph E states that "current battery technology is generally suited to storage durations of a few hours." |

### Passage 3: Globalisation and Cultural Identity (Q27–40)

**Q27–30: Multiple Choice**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 27 | B | Paragraph A attributes the acceleration of globalisation to "containerised shipping, cheap air travel, and digital communication technologies." |
| 28 | B | Paragraph B explains McDonaldisation as the rationalisation of cultural practices along lines of efficiency, predictability, calculability, and control — modelled on the fast-food industry. |
| 29 | B | Paragraph C defines glocalization as capturing "the way in which global processes are always experienced locally and locally inflected." |
| 30 | C | Paragraph D states that indigenous communities have used digital tools "to create online archives of endangered languages" and "to connect with diaspora members scattered across the globe." |

**Q31–36: True / False / Not Given**

| Q | Answer | Explanation |
|---|--------|-------------|
| 31 | TRUE | Paragraph A states globalisation "lifted hundreds of millions of people out of poverty." |
| 32 | TRUE | Paragraph B states the homogenisation thesis holds that globalisation produces "a single dominant, largely Western, consumer culture." |
| 33 | FALSE | Paragraph C uses K-pop as an example of *hybridity* — it "fuses Western pop production techniques with distinctively Korean performance aesthetics," making it an explicitly global-local hybrid, not a purely local form. |
| 34 | NOT GIVEN | Paragraph E mentions France's quotas for French-language content in broadcasting and a policy body for cultural production, but does not mention restrictions on internet platforms specifically. |
| 35 | TRUE | Paragraph F states: "UNESCO estimates that approximately half of the world's 7,000 languages are at risk of extinction by the end of the twenty-first century." |
| 36 | FALSE | The writer explicitly argues in Paragraph G that "the relationship between globalisation and cultural identity is not a simple zero-sum contest" and that effects depend on "the resources, choices, and agency of the communities involved." The writer does not believe globalisation *always* has a negative effect. |

**Q37–40: Gap Fill**

| Q | Answer |
|---|--------|
| 37 | hybrid |
| 38 | Nollywood |
| 39 | resistance |
| 40 | asymmetry |

*Explanations:*
- *Q37: Paragraph C states globalisation produces "new hybrid forms that are neither purely global nor purely local."*
- *Q38: Paragraph C refers to "Nollywood, Nigeria's prodigious film industry."*
- *Q39: Paragraph E refers to "Cultural resistance movements" taking varied forms including government language policies and grassroots language-revitalisation efforts.*
- *Q40: Paragraph G states that "what is distinctive about the current moment is the speed, scale, and asymmetry of the cultural flows involved."*
