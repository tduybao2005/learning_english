## PHẦN 2: BÀI TẬP LUYỆN THÊM

---

### Exercise 1: Rewrite the Relative Clause as a Participle Clause

**Hướng dẫn:** Thu gọn mệnh đề quan hệ (relative clause) thành participle clause. Giữ nguyên nghĩa.

1. The data, which was collected over a period of five years, forms the basis of the study.
2. Policies that are designed to reduce carbon emissions must be implemented globally.
3. The proposal, which was submitted by the environmental lobby, was rejected by the committee.
4. The report, which has been widely circulated among policymakers, calls for urgent action.
5. Students who are enrolled in the programme receive mentoring from industry professionals.
6. The agreement, which was signed by forty nations, represented a historic moment in international diplomacy.
7. Infrastructure that has been neglected for decades is now in urgent need of repair.
8. The guidelines, which were established by the WHO, are now considered the global standard.
9. Researchers who are based at the institute have produced a series of landmark studies.
10. Applications that are received after the closing date cannot be considered under any circumstances.

---

### Exercise 2: Identify the Dangling Participle and Correct It

**Hướng dẫn:** Mỗi câu chứa một dangling participle. Xác định lỗi và sửa lại.

1. Reviewing the application, several inconsistencies were found.
2. Having analysed the data, the results were immediately published.
3. Walking through the city centre, numerous examples of urban decay were visible.
4. Designed to improve literacy rates, the teachers welcomed the new curriculum.
5. Not having been informed of the meeting, the agenda was approved without her input.
6. Launched in 2019, the researchers had high hopes for the initiative.
7. Considering the available evidence, this conclusion appears premature.
8. Having failed to secure adequate funding, the project was abandoned.

---

### Exercise 3: Choose the Correct Participial Form

**Hướng dẫn:** Chọn dạng phân từ đúng: V-ing / V3 / Having + V3 / Being + V3

1. ________ (Conduct / Conducted / Having conducted) three rounds of testing, the scientists were confident in their results.
2. The legislation, ________ (introduce / introducing / introduced) in 2021, has had a measurable impact on air quality.
3. ________ (Not inform / Not being informed / Not having been informed) of the changes, the staff were understandably concerned.
4. ________ (Exhaust / Exhausting / Exhausted) by years of conflict, the population welcomed the peace agreement.
5. ________ (Recognise / Recognising / Recognised) the urgency of the crisis, world leaders convened an emergency summit.
6. ________ (Submit / Submitting / Having submitted) her resignation, she left the organisation after fifteen years of service.
7. The findings, ________ (challenge / challenging / having challenged) conventional assumptions, were met with significant scepticism.
8. ________ (Fail / Failing / Having failed) to reach a consensus, the committee postponed its decision.
9. ________ (Analyse / Analysed / Analysing) from a sociological perspective, the phenomenon reveals deep structural inequalities.
10. ________ (Build / Built / Building) on the foundations of earlier research, this study advances our understanding considerably.

## ANSWER KEY (Phần từ vựng)

### Exercise 1: Answers

1. The data, **collected** over a period of five years, forms the basis of the study.
2. Policies **designed** to reduce carbon emissions must be implemented globally.
3. The proposal, **submitted** by the environmental lobby, was rejected by the committee.
4. The report, **widely circulated** among policymakers, calls for urgent action.
5. Students **enrolled** in the programme receive mentoring from industry professionals.
6. The agreement, **signed** by forty nations, represented a historic moment in international diplomacy.
7. Infrastructure **neglected** for decades is now in urgent need of repair.
8. The guidelines, **established** by the WHO, are now considered the global standard.
9. Researchers **based** at the institute have produced a series of landmark studies.
10. Applications **received** after the closing date cannot be considered under any circumstances.

### Exercise 2: Answers

1. **Error:** "several inconsistencies" cannot review. → **Corrected:** Reviewing the application, **the auditors** found several inconsistencies.
2. **Error:** "the results" cannot analyse data. → **Corrected:** Having analysed the data, **the researchers** published the results immediately.
3. **Error:** "numerous examples" cannot walk. → **Corrected:** Walking through the city centre, **we** noticed numerous examples of urban decay.
4. **Error:** "teachers" did not design the curriculum. → **Corrected:** Designed to improve literacy rates, the new curriculum **was welcomed by** teachers.
5. **Error:** "the agenda" cannot be informed. → **Corrected:** Not having been informed of the meeting, **she** found that the agenda had already been approved.
6. **Error:** "researchers" did not launch in 2019 — the initiative did. → **Corrected:** Launched in 2019, the initiative **gave** researchers high hopes.
7. **Acceptable** (impersonal "considering"): When "considering" is used impersonally, it is grammatically acceptable in informal/hedged academic English. However, for strict formal writing: → **Corrected:** Considering the available evidence, **one** might argue this conclusion appears premature.
8. **Correct** — The implied subject of "having failed to secure" is the same as "the project" — **WAIT:** "the project" was abandoned, but did the project fail to secure funding? In passive sense, yes. This is borderline. Clearer version: Having failed to secure adequate funding, **the team** abandoned the project.

### Exercise 3: Answers

1. **Having conducted** (perfect participle — action completed before main clause)
2. **introduced** (past participle — reduced relative clause, passive)
3. **Not having been informed** (perfect passive participle, negative)
4. **Exhausted** (past participle — describes a state)
5. **Recognising** (present participle — simultaneous action / reason)
6. **Having submitted** (perfect participle — action before main clause)
7. **challenging** (present participle — result/description following comma)
8. **Having failed** (perfect participle — reason/cause)
9. **Analysed** (past participle — reduced passive relative clause)
10. **Built** (past participle — reduced passive construction meaning "based on")
