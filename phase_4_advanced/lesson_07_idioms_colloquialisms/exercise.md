### Section C — Corrections:
1. "tip of the mountain" → sai idiom. Đúng: "the tip of the iceberg"
2. "sharp as a thumb" → sai idiom. Đúng: "as sharp as a tack"
3. "nick of space" → sai idiom. Đúng: "in the nick of time"
4. "burn all its bridges with environmental regulations" → idiom dùng sai nghĩa. "Burn bridges" có nghĩa là phá mối quan hệ với người/tổ chức. Câu này nên dùng: "abandon/dismantle environmental regulations"
5. "as cool as a cucumber" không phù hợp để mô tả người làm việc chăm chỉ. Nên dùng: "burns the midnight oil" hoặc "goes the extra mile"
6. "a step in the left direction" → sai. Đúng: "a step in the right direction"
7. "back to square two" → sai. Đúng: "back to square one"
8. "needless to say" dùng khi điều đó là hiển nhiên/expected. Kết quả bất ngờ (surprising) → không dùng được. Đúng: "Surprisingly, everyone expected her to fail — but she performed brilliantly."

### Section D — Appropriate or Not:
1. Speaking: ✓ | Writing: ✓ (borderline — cụm này gần với semi-formal, chấp nhận được) | Lý do: "without a shadow of a doubt" là cụm nhấn mạnh có thể dùng trong cả hai ngữ cảnh
2. Speaking: ✓ | Writing: ✗ | Lý do: "eat like horses" quá informal cho writing academic
3. Speaking: ✓ | Writing: ✗ (borderline) | Lý do: "sit on the fence" hơi informal cho writing; thay = "adopt an ambivalent stance"
4. Speaking: ✓ | Writing: ✗ | Lý do: "at the end of the day" là informal filler phrase — quá conversational cho Task 2
5. Speaking: ✓ | Writing: ✗ | Lý do: "tip of the iceberg" là informal idiom, không phù hợp với academic register
6. Speaking: ✓ | Writing: ✗ | Lý do: "between a rock and a hard place" là informal. Viết: "face an irreconcilable conflict between economic development and environmental preservation"

### Section E — Mẫu trả lời (Câu 1):
*"Frankly, in my view, most governments are still sitting on the fence when it comes to meaningful redistribution policies. They acknowledge the problem but rarely implement bold measures. Income inequality is, without a shadow of a doubt, the elephant in the room in most developed economies — everyone knows the gap is widening, yet substantive action remains elusive. What frustrates me most is that the solutions aren't new — progressive taxation and investment in public services have worked historically. The political will, however, seems to be lacking."*

### Section F — Mẫu bài nói Part 2:
*"I'd like to talk about the time I had to retake my university entrance exam — a challenge that, at the time, felt completely devastating.*

*I had burned the midnight oil for months preparing for the original exam, but when results came out, I had fallen short of the required score for my chosen faculty. I felt utterly defeated — as if I was back to square one after all that effort.*

*However, I made the decision to bite the bullet and spend six more months studying intensively. I changed my approach entirely — focused on weak areas, found a study group, and practised exam technique rather than just rote memorisation.*

*The second attempt resulted in a significantly higher score, and I was eventually admitted to my preferred faculty. Looking back, losing that first attempt was, without a shadow of a doubt, a blessing in disguise. It forced me to develop real study discipline and resilience that served me throughout my entire degree.*

*The experience taught me that setbacks are often redirections. The most valuable lessons I carry today were forged in that period of difficulty."*

**Idioms used:** burn the midnight oil / back to square one / bite the bullet / a blessing in disguise / without a shadow of a doubt (5 idioms — 3 minimum required, these flow naturally)
