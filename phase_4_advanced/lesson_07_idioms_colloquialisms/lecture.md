# BÀI 7: THÀNH NGỮ & TIẾNG LÓNG (IDIOMS & COLLOQUIALISMS)
## Phase 4 Advanced | IELTS 7.0–8.0

---

## ⚠️ QUY TẮC VÀNG — ĐỌC TRƯỚC KHI HỌC BẤT KỲ THÀNH NGỮ NÀO

### IDIOMS trong IELTS: Được phép ở ĐÂU và KHÔNG được phép ở ĐÂU

| Kỹ năng | Được dùng idioms? | Lý do |
|---|---|---|
| IELTS Speaking Part 1 | Có (dùng ít, tự nhiên) | Register informal được chấp nhận |
| IELTS Speaking Part 2 | Có (1–2 idioms phù hợp) | Cho thấy lexical range tự nhiên |
| IELTS Speaking Part 3 | Có (1 idiom, cẩn thận) | Nên ưu tiên academic vocab hơn |
| IELTS Writing Task 1 | **TUYỆT ĐỐI KHÔNG** | Academic register — formal only |
| IELTS Writing Task 2 | **TUYỆT ĐỐI KHÔNG** | Sẽ HẠ band score vì quá informal |

**Giải thích:** Examiner đánh giá Lexical Resource theo register. Writing Task 2 yêu cầu academic/semi-formal register. Idioms như "bite the bullet" hoặc "hit the jackpot" là informal colloquialisms không phù hợp với academic discourse. Dùng idioms trong writing sẽ khiến examiner đánh giá rằng bạn không hiểu sự khác biệt giữa các register — điều này trực tiếp hạ điểm Lexical Resource.

---

## TẠI SAO IDIOMS GIÚP ÍCH CHO IELTS SPEAKING?

Trong Speaking, Band Descriptors yêu cầu:
- **Band 7 Lexical Resource:** "uses vocabulary resource flexibly to discuss a variety of topics... uses some less common and idiomatic vocabulary"
- **Band 8 Lexical Resource:** "uses a wide vocabulary resource readily and flexibly to convey precise meaning... uses less common and idiomatic vocabulary skilfully"

Từ khóa: **"idiomatic vocabulary"** — đây là từ chính thức trong Band Descriptors của Cambridge. Dùng idioms đúng cách, tự nhiên, đúng ngữ cảnh = tăng điểm Lexical Resource.

---

## CÁCH DÙNG IDIOMS TỰ NHIÊN — KHÔNG ÉP BUỘC

### 3 sai lầm phổ biến khi dùng idioms trong Speaking:

**Sai lầm 1: Báo trước idiom (telegraphing)**
- ❌ "I want to use the idiom 'once in a blue moon'... I rarely go to the cinema — once in a blue moon."
- ✓ "I rarely go to the cinema — maybe once in a blue moon."

**Sai lầm 2: Dùng idiom sai ngữ cảnh**
- ❌ "I think pollution is very bad. It costs an arm and a leg." (không liên quan)
- ✓ "Electric vehicles are still quite expensive — they cost an arm and a leg for most families."

**Sai lầm 3: Nhồi nhét quá nhiều**
- ❌ "I hit the jackpot when I found a job. Now I'm burning the midnight oil and going the extra mile every day at a crossroads in my career..."
- ✓ Dùng tối đa 1–2 idioms trong một phần thi Speaking. Chất lượng hơn số lượng.

### Kỹ thuật tích hợp tự nhiên:
1. Dùng idiom ở cuối câu như một comment thêm vào
2. Sau idiom, giải thích ngay ý nghĩa bằng cách diễn đạt khác (paraphrase)
3. Chọn idiom diễn đạt đúng ý bạn muốn nói — đừng chọn idiom chỉ vì muốn "show off"

### NHÓM 1: TIME & FREQUENCY (Thời gian & Tần suất) — 10 idioms

**1. once in a blue moon** — rất hiếm khi xảy ra
- *"I visit my grandparents once in a blue moon — perhaps twice a year at most."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Describing habits, frequency of activities

**2. at the drop of a hat** — ngay lập tức, không cần suy nghĩ
- *"She's so spontaneous — she'd travel to another country at the drop of a hat."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Describing personality, decision-making styles

**3. in the nick of time** — đúng lúc, vừa kịp
- *"I submitted my university application in the nick of time — just five minutes before the deadline."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Narrating a personal experience (Part 2)

**4. against the clock** — đua với thời gian, bị áp lực thời gian
- *"Medical staff in emergency rooms are always working against the clock."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Discussing work pressure, professions, education

**5. burn the midnight oil** — thức đêm để làm việc hoặc học
- *"Before final exams, students inevitably end up burning the midnight oil."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Education, studying habits, work-life balance

**6. beat around the bush** — vòng vo không đi vào vấn đề
- *"Stop beating around the bush — just tell me your honest opinion."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Communication styles, directness vs. indirectness

**7. on the spur of the moment** — bốc đồng, quyết định không có kế hoạch
- *"We decided to go on a road trip on the spur of the moment — no planning whatsoever."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Travel, lifestyle, spontaneity vs. planning

**8. kill time** — giết thời gian, làm gì đó để chờ
- *"I usually listen to podcasts to kill time during my commute."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Daily routines, transport, hobbies

**9. behind the times** — lỗi thời, không theo kịp xu hướng
- *"Some traditional schools are behind the times when it comes to integrating technology."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Education, technology, social change

**10. sooner or later** — sớm muộn gì cũng sẽ xảy ra
- *"Sooner or later, renewable energy will replace fossil fuels entirely."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Future predictions, trends, technology

### NHÓM 2: MONEY & SUCCESS (Tiền bạc & Thành công) — 10 idioms

**11. hit the jackpot** — trúng số hoặc đạt được thành công lớn bất ngờ
- *"She really hit the jackpot with that investment — it tripled in value within a year."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Business, luck vs. hard work, success

**12. make ends meet** — kiếm đủ sống, vừa đủ chi tiêu
- *"Many young people in expensive cities struggle to make ends meet on a minimum wage."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Cost of living, urban life, economy

**13. go broke** — phá sản, hết tiền
- *"Several small businesses went broke during the economic downturn."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Economy, business, financial crisis

**14. on a shoestring** — với ngân sách rất ít
- *"Backpacking lets you travel the world on a shoestring."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Travel, student life, budget

**15. go through the roof** — tăng vọt (giá, chi phí, nhiệt độ)
- *"House prices in major cities have gone through the roof in the past decade."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Property, economy, cost of living

**16. in the black** — có lãi (tài chính)
- *"The company only moved into the black after its third year of operations."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Business, economics

**17. in the red** — thua lỗ, thâm hụt
- *"The national health service is deeply in the red due to underfunding."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Government, healthcare, economics

**18. turn the corner** — vượt qua giai đoạn khó khăn nhất
- *"The economy seems to have turned the corner — unemployment is finally falling."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Economy, personal challenges, healthcare

**19. strike it rich** — đột ngột trở nên giàu có
- *"A few early cryptocurrency investors managed to strike it rich, but many others lost everything."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Technology, risk, investment

**20. at a price** — đạt được điều gì đó nhưng phải trả giá đắt
- *"Success in highly competitive industries often comes at a price — personal relationships and health often suffer."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Work-life balance, ambition, sacrifice

### NHÓM 3: COMMUNICATION & OPINION (Giao tiếp & Quan điểm) — 10 idioms

**21. speak one's mind** — nói thẳng, bày tỏ ý kiến thật
- *"In some cultures, people are encouraged to speak their mind, while in others, indirect communication is preferred."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Cultural differences, communication, Part 3 discussions

**22. sit on the fence** — không chọn bên, không đưa ra ý kiến rõ ràng
- *"Politicians often sit on the fence on controversial issues to avoid alienating voters."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Politics, opinion, decision-making

**23. read between the lines** — hiểu ý ngầm, không nói thẳng
- *"You have to read between the lines in formal diplomatic statements — the real message is often implied."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Communication, media, politics

**24. take with a pinch of salt** — không tin hoàn toàn, nghi ngờ một phần
- *"You should take social media health advice with a pinch of salt — it's rarely verified by experts."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Media literacy, health information, critical thinking

**25. give the benefit of the doubt** — tin tưởng ai dù không có bằng chứng chắc chắn
- *"I'm willing to give the benefit of the doubt to companies that claim to be environmentally friendly, provided they're transparent."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Trust, media, business ethics

**26. the elephant in the room** — vấn đề rõ ràng mà mọi người biết nhưng không ai muốn đề cập
- *"Climate change is the elephant in the room at most energy industry conferences."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Environment, social issues, politics

**27. put your foot in your mouth** — lỡ lời, vô tình nói điều xúc phạm
- *"He really put his foot in his mouth by joking about the redundancies in front of the manager."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Communication, workplace, social situations

**28. get to the point** — đi thẳng vào vấn đề
- *"In professional settings, people appreciate it when presenters get to the point quickly."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Communication styles, business, culture

**29. see eye to eye** — đồng ý, có cùng quan điểm
- *"My parents and I rarely see eye to eye on career choices — they prefer stability, I crave creativity."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Generational differences, family, relationships

**30. break the ice** — phá vỡ sự ngại ngùng, mở đầu cuộc trò chuyện
- *"Humour is often the most effective way to break the ice in a new social or professional setting."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Social skills, communication, Part 1 topics

### NHÓM 4: PROBLEMS & CHALLENGES (Vấn đề & Thách thức) — 10 idioms

**31. bite off more than you can chew** — nhận/cắn miếng quá to, không kham được
- *"Students who take on too many extracurricular activities risk biting off more than they can chew."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Education, time management, ambition

**32. in hot water** — gặp rắc rối nghiêm trọng
- *"The company found itself in hot water after the data breach was exposed."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Business, technology, law

**33. a blessing in disguise** — điều tưởng như xấu nhưng thực ra lại tốt
- *"Losing that job turned out to be a blessing in disguise — I started my own business and it flourished."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Personal growth, resilience, optimism

**34. add fuel to the fire** — làm cho tình hình tệ hơn
- *"The government's delayed response only added fuel to the fire of public anger."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Social issues, politics, conflict

**35. open a can of worms** — gây ra nhiều vấn đề phức tạp hơn mong đợi
- *"Allowing anonymous online speech opens a can of worms regarding accountability and harm."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Technology, law, social media

**36. tip of the iceberg** — chỉ là một phần nhỏ của vấn đề lớn hơn
- *"The reported cases of cyberbullying are just the tip of the iceberg — most go unreported."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Social issues, data, scale of problems

**37. between a rock and a hard place** — tiến thoái lưỡng nan, không có lựa chọn tốt
- *"Developing nations are often caught between a rock and a hard place — economic growth vs. environmental protection."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Development, politics, dilemmas

**38. burn bridges** — tự phá huỷ mối quan hệ, không thể quay lại
- *"It's rarely wise to burn bridges with former employers — industries are smaller than people think."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Workplace, relationships, career advice

**39. throw in the towel** — bỏ cuộc
- *"Entrepreneurship requires persistence — the most successful people are those who refused to throw in the towel."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Success, resilience, business

**40. every cloud has a silver lining** — trong cái rủi có cái may
- *"Even the pandemic had a silver lining — it accelerated remote working and work-life flexibility."*
- **IELTS Speaking context:** Optimism, challenges, unexpected benefits

### NHÓM 5: LEARNING & WORK (Học tập & Công việc) — 10 idioms

**41. learn the ropes** — học cách làm việc trong môi trường mới
- *"It takes time to learn the ropes in any new job, regardless of your qualifications."*

**42. get the hang of it** — dần nắm được kỹ thuật, trở nên thành thạo
- *"Coding seemed impossible at first, but I eventually got the hang of it."*

**43. back to square one** — phải bắt đầu lại từ đầu
- *"When the prototype failed, the engineering team was back to square one."*

**44. think outside the box** — suy nghĩ sáng tạo, không theo khuôn mẫu
- *"Modern education should encourage students to think outside the box, not just memorise facts."*

**45. hit the nail on the head** — nói/làm đúng trọng tâm
- *"The report hits the nail on the head — the main problem is poor urban planning."*

**46. cut corners** — làm tắt, bỏ qua bước quan trọng để tiết kiệm thời gian/tiền
- *"Builders who cut corners on safety regulations put lives at risk."*

**47. go the extra mile** — làm hơn mức được yêu cầu
- *"Employees who consistently go the extra mile are far more likely to be promoted."*

**48. bite the bullet** — chấp nhận điều khó khăn và vượt qua nó
- *"She decided to bite the bullet and retake the exam, despite the pressure."*

**49. cover all the bases** — chuẩn bị cho mọi tình huống có thể xảy ra
- *"A good business plan covers all the bases — marketing, finance, and contingencies."*

**50. the ball is in your court** — đến lượt người kia quyết định/hành động
- *"I've made my offer — now the ball is in your court."*

### NHÓM 6: CHANGE & IMPROVEMENT (Thay đổi & Cải thiện) — 10 idioms

**51. turn over a new leaf** — quyết tâm thay đổi bản thân, làm lại từ đầu
- *"After failing his first year, he turned over a new leaf and graduated with distinction."*

**52. at a crossroads** — ở trước ngã rẽ quan trọng trong cuộc đời
- *"At 30, she felt she was at a crossroads — stay in a secure job or pursue her passion."*

**53. come full circle** — quay lại điểm xuất phát sau một vòng trải nghiệm
- *"The fashion industry has come full circle — styles from the 70s are fashionable again."*

**54. shake things up** — thay đổi mọi thứ mạnh mẽ
- *"The new CEO shook things up considerably, restructuring entire departments."*

**55. out with the old, in with the new** — bỏ cái cũ, chào đón cái mới
- *"In the technology sector, it's out with the old, in with the new — products become obsolete within years."*

**56. take a turn for the better/worse** — chuyển biến tốt hơn hoặc xấu hơn
- *"Her health took a turn for the better after the surgery."*

**57. go downhill** — suy giảm, ngày càng tệ hơn
- *"The quality of journalism has gone downhill since social media became the primary news source."*

**58. on the up and up** — ngày càng tốt hơn, thành công hơn
- *"The startup was on the up and up — revenues doubled for three consecutive quarters."*

**59. move the goalposts** — thay đổi điều kiện/tiêu chuẩn giữa chừng, làm khó người khác
- *"It's frustrating when employers move the goalposts during salary negotiations."*

**60. a step in the right direction** — bước đi đúng hướng, tiến bộ dù chưa hoàn hảo
- *"The new recycling policy is a step in the right direction, though far more comprehensive measures are needed."*

### NHÓM 7: PEOPLE & CHARACTER (Con người & Tính cách) — 10 idioms

**61. a chip on your shoulder** — mang theo sự bực bội hoặc mặc cảm từ quá khứ
- *"He has a chip on his shoulder about not going to university — he constantly feels he has to prove himself."*

**62. set in your ways** — cứng nhắc, khó thay đổi thói quen
- *"Older generations can be more set in their ways, making them resistant to new technologies."*

**63. stand on your own two feet** — tự lập, không phụ thuộc vào người khác
- *"Living abroad forces young people to stand on their own two feet."*

**64. stab in the back** — phản bội ai đó, đặc biệt là người tin tưởng mình
- *"Sharing confidential information with a competitor is a real stab in the back."*

**65. bend over backwards** — cố gắng hết sức để giúp đỡ ai
- *"The customer service team bent over backwards to resolve the complaint."*

**66. wear your heart on your sleeve** — biểu lộ cảm xúc rõ ràng, không che giấu
- *"He wears his heart on his sleeve — everyone can tell when he's unhappy."*

**67. get under someone's skin** — làm ai đó khó chịu, bực bội (có thể tốt hoặc xấu)
- *"Persistent advertising tends to get under people's skin in a negative way."*

**68. look up to someone** — ngưỡng mộ, tôn trọng ai đó
- *"Many young people look up to entrepreneurs like Elon Musk as role models, though opinions are divided."*

**69. be up to no good** — đang làm điều gì đó xấu hoặc đáng ngờ
- *"Surveillance cameras deter those who might otherwise be up to no good."*

**70. keep someone at arm's length** — giữ khoảng cách, không cho ai đến gần
- *"After the betrayal, she kept her former friends at arm's length."*

### NHÓM 8: COMPARISONS (So sánh) — 10 idioms

**71. like two peas in a pod** — giống nhau như đúc (thường về tính cách hoặc ngoại hình)
- *"The twins are like two peas in a pod — not just in appearance, but in their mannerisms too."*

**72. as cool as a cucumber** — bình tĩnh như không, không bị xáo trộn
- *"Despite the crisis, the CEO remained as cool as a cucumber in the press conference."*

**73. as sharp as a tack** — cực kỳ thông minh, nhanh trí
- *"At 85, she was still as sharp as a tack — her memory and analysis were impressive."*

**74. fit as a fiddle** — rất khỏe mạnh
- *"Regular exercise and a balanced diet kept him fit as a fiddle well into his seventies."*

**75. old as the hills** — rất cổ xưa, đã có từ lâu
- *"The tradition of storytelling is as old as the hills — it predates written language."*

**76. as busy as a bee** — bận rộn liên tục
- *"During harvest season, farmers are as busy as bees from dawn to dusk."*

**77. as clear as mud** — không rõ ràng chút nào (mỉa mai) — khi nói câu này, bạn đang chê là mơ hồ
- *"The government's policy guidelines were, frankly, as clear as mud."*

**78. as good as gold** — ngoan ngoãn, đáng tin cậy, hành xử tốt
- *"The children were as good as gold throughout the ceremony."*

**79. sleep like a log** — ngủ rất say, không bị quấy rầy
- *"After the hiking trip, I slept like a log for ten hours straight."*

**80. eat like a horse** — ăn rất nhiều
- *"After training sessions, the athletes eat like horses — their caloric needs are enormous."*

### NHÓM 9: CERTAINTY & EMPHASIS (Chắc chắn & Nhấn mạnh) — 10 idioms

**81. without a shadow of a doubt** — không còn nghi ngờ gì nữa
- *"Climate change is, without a shadow of a doubt, the defining challenge of our generation."*

**82. beyond question** — không thể bàn cãi, hiển nhiên
- *"The health benefits of regular exercise are beyond question."*

**83. hands down** — dễ dàng hơn hẳn, vượt trội rõ ràng
- *"She was hands down the most qualified candidate for the position."*

**84. at the end of the day** — xét cho cùng, cuối cùng thì
- *"At the end of the day, both education and experience are essential for career success."*

**85. when push comes to shove** — khi đến lúc thực sự cần thiết, khi không còn lựa chọn
- *"When push comes to shove, most people prioritise family over work."*

**86. needless to say** — không cần phải nói, điều đó hiển nhiên
- *"Needless to say, studying abroad improves language skills significantly."*

**87. goes without saying** — ai cũng hiểu, không cần giải thích
- *"It goes without saying that regular revision is essential before any major exam."*

**88. by all accounts** — theo như mọi người đều nói / theo những gì được biết
- *"By all accounts, the new prime minister is an effective communicator."*

**89. make no mistake** — đừng có nhầm, hãy chú ý kỹ
- *"Make no mistake — the consequences of ignoring climate data will be severe."*

**90. in no uncertain terms** — nói rõ ràng, không mơ hồ, thẳng thắn
- *"The professor made it clear in no uncertain terms that plagiarism would not be tolerated."*

### NHÓM 10: BODY (Cơ thể) — 10 idioms

**91. have a bone to pick** — có điều muốn phàn nàn hoặc nói chuyện nghiêm túc
- *"I have a bone to pick with the airline about how they handled the delay."*

**92. cost an arm and a leg** — cực kỳ đắt tiền
- *"Private healthcare in some countries costs an arm and a leg."*

**93. get cold feet** — mất can đảm vào phút chót, đột ngột không dám nữa
- *"He got cold feet and withdrew his job application at the last minute."*

**94. have butterflies** — cảm giác hồi hộp, lo lắng (thường trước sự kiện quan trọng)
- *"Even experienced speakers admit to having butterflies before a major presentation."*

**95. pull someone's leg** — chọc ghẹo, nói đùa để trêu ai
- *"You got a scholarship to Harvard? Are you pulling my leg?"*

**96. keep a straight face** — không để lộ cảm xúc, cố không cười
- *"It was nearly impossible to keep a straight face during his dramatic retelling of events."*

**97. rule of thumb** — quy tắc thực tế, kinh nghiệm chung (không hoàn hảo nhưng hữu ích)
- *"As a rule of thumb, you should spend no more than 30% of your income on housing."*

**98. give someone the cold shoulder** — phớt lờ, cố ý không chú ý đến ai
- *"After the argument, my colleague gave me the cold shoulder for a week."*

**99. turn a blind eye** — giả vờ không thấy, cố tình bỏ qua
- *"Governments that turn a blind eye to corporate pollution are complicit in environmental damage."*

**100. have a thick skin** — không bị tổn thương dễ dàng, chịu đựng được chỉ trích
- *"Working in customer service requires you to have a thick skin."*

## CHIẾN LƯỢC DÙNG IDIOMS TRONG IELTS SPEAKING

### Part 1 (Short answers — 1–2 sentences)
Dùng idioms nhẹ nhàng, hỗ trợ trả lời:
- Q: "How often do you exercise?"
- A: "Not as often as I should, honestly — maybe once in a blue moon. I know I need to be more disciplined."

### Part 2 (2-minute monologue)
Tích hợp 1–2 idioms vào câu chuyện:
- "...and that's when I realised I'd bitten off more than I could chew. Taking three part-time jobs simultaneously while studying was simply unsustainable."

### Part 3 (Discussion — abstract ideas)
Dùng idioms với confidence nhưng sau đó phát triển ý bằng academic language:
- "Urban sprawl is, without a shadow of a doubt, one of the most pressing planning challenges. Cities that fail to address it are essentially moving the goalposts on sustainability targets."
