## PHẦN 1: NGỮ PHÁP NÂNG CAO (30 điểm)

### Bài 1: Đảo ngữ (Inversion) — 6 điểm (1 điểm/câu)

Transform each sentence using the inverted structure indicated.

**1.** She rarely missed a class during her three years at university.
→ **Rarely did she miss a class during her three years at university.**
*(Rarely + auxiliary did + subject + bare infinitive)*

**2.** I had barely arrived at the station when the last train departed.
→ **Barely had I arrived at the station when the last train departed.**
*(Barely + had + subject + past participle)*

**3.** He had never seen such a remarkable scientific achievement in his career.
→ **Never had he seen such a remarkable scientific achievement in his career.**
*(Never + had + subject + past participle)*

**4.** We can only understand the implications of this data if we examine it within its broader context.
→ **Only if we examine it within its broader context can we understand the implications of this data.**
*(Only if + clause + inverted main clause)*

**5.** She not only won the prestigious award but also received an unexpected promotion.
→ **Not only did she win the prestigious award, but she also received an unexpected promotion.**
*(Not only + did + subject + verb, but + subject + also)*

**6.** The president had no sooner left the podium than the journalists began their questions.
→ **No sooner had the president left the podium than the journalists began their questions.**
*(No sooner + had + subject + past participle + than)*

---

### Bài 2: Câu chẻ (Cleft Sentences) — 6 điểm (1 điểm/câu)

Rewrite each sentence as a cleft sentence using the structure indicated.

**1.** The lack of funding caused the project to fail. [It-cleft]
→ **It was the lack of funding that caused the project to fail.**

**2.** John submitted the report first. [It-cleft, focus on subject]
→ **It was John who submitted the report first.**

**3.** The new policy shocked everyone in the department. [What-cleft]
→ **What shocked everyone in the department was the new policy.**

**4.** The research team's determination impressed the review panel. [What-cleft]
→ **What impressed the review panel was the research team's determination.**

**5.** The committee rejected the original proposal, not the revised version. [It-cleft, focus on object]
→ **It was the original proposal (not the revised version) that the committee rejected.**

**6.** She discovered the error by checking the raw data manually. [It-cleft, focus on method]
→ **It was by checking the raw data manually that she discovered the error.**

---

### Bài 3: Mệnh đề phân từ (Participle Clauses) — 6 điểm (1 điểm/câu)

Reduce each sentence using a participle clause.

**1.** Because she had finished her work early, she left the office before the others.
→ **Having finished her work early, she left the office before the others.**
*(Perfect participle = action completed before the main action)*

**2.** While he was walking home through the park, he noticed a strange car parked by the gate.
→ **Walking home through the park, he noticed a strange car parked by the gate.**
*(Present participle for simultaneous action)*

**3.** Because the report was written in ambiguous language, it was misinterpreted by several reviewers.
→ **Written in ambiguous language, the report was misinterpreted by several reviewers.**
*(Passive participle for passive main subject)*

**4.** After the scientists had completed the analysis, they published their findings in a peer-reviewed journal.
→ **Having completed the analysis, the scientists published their findings in a peer-reviewed journal.**

**5.** The government, which was alarmed by the rate of deforestation, introduced new environmental legislation.
→ **Alarmed by the rate of deforestation, the government introduced new environmental legislation.**

**6.** Since he did not have sufficient evidence, the researcher could not substantiate his initial hypothesis.
→ **Not having sufficient evidence, the researcher could not substantiate his initial hypothesis.**
*(Negative perfect participle)*

---

### Bài 4: Giả định cách (Subjunctive Mood) — 6 điểm (1 điểm/câu)

Complete each sentence with the correct subjunctive form.

**1.** It is essential that he _____ the conference in order to present his findings.
→ **attend** *(Mandative subjunctive: base form after "It is essential that")*

**2.** The board insisted that the annual report _____ by the end of the financial quarter.
→ **be submitted** *(Passive mandative subjunctive)*

**3.** The university requires that all international students _____ proof of English proficiency before enrolment.
→ **provide**

**4.** The committee recommended that the policy _____ and revised in light of the new evidence.
→ **be reconsidered**

**5.** It is vital that every member of the team _____ aware of the new safety protocols.
→ **be** *(Subjunctive of "to be" — not "is")*

**6.** The judge ordered that the defendant _____ in custody until the verdict was reached.
→ **remain** *(or: be held)*

---

### Bài 5: Thể bị động nâng cao — 6 điểm (1 điểm/câu)

Rewrite using impersonal passive or causative have/get where indicated.

**1.** People say that he is the most qualified candidate for the position. [Impersonal passive — two forms]
→ **It is said that he is the most qualified candidate for the position.**
→ **He is said to be the most qualified candidate for the position.**

**2.** They are going to renovate the entire city centre over the next five years. [Standard passive]
→ **The entire city centre is going to be renovated over the next five years.**

**3.** A professional designer created a new logo for their company last month. [Causative have]
→ **They had a new logo created (by a professional designer) last month.**

**4.** Experts believe that the ancient tomb dates back to the third century BC. [Impersonal passive]
→ **It is believed that the ancient tomb dates back to the third century BC.**
→ **The ancient tomb is believed to date back to the third century BC.**

**5.** Someone stole his wallet on the underground this morning. [Causative get — informal]
→ **He got his wallet stolen on the underground this morning.**

**6.** A mechanic is servicing her car at the garage right now. [Causative have — present continuous]
→ **She is having her car serviced at the garage right now.**

## PHẦN 2: TỪ VỰNG NÂNG CAO (20 điểm)

### Bài 6: Sắc thái từ ngữ (Connotation & Nuance) — 10 điểm (1 điểm/câu)

**1.** Positive context (praising an employee) → **tenacious** *(not stubborn — stubborn has negative connotation)*
**2.** Complaint context → **stingy** *(not frugal — frugal is positive/neutral)*
**3.** Academic neutral context → **traditional** *(not backward/primitive — these carry ethnocentric bias)*
**4.** Formal praise → **self-assured** *(not arrogant/conceited — both negative)*
**5.** Critical editorial → **meddlesome** *(not methodical — methodical is positive)*
**6.** Positive formal report → **cost-effective** *(not cheap — cheap implies poor quality)*
**7.** Admiring description → **single-minded** *(not obsessive — obsessive is clinical/negative)*
**8.** Formal self-description → **inquisitive** *(not nosy/prying — both negative)*
**9.** Literary/horror description → **decrepit** and **foreboding** *(not old/scary — too basic for C1)*
**10.** Formal rewrite → **apprehended** *(not caught — apprehended is appropriate for formal crime reporting)*

### Bài 7: AWL (Academic Word List) — 10 điểm (1 điểm/câu)

Complete each gap with the correct AWL word from the box.

*[Typical exam box: demonstrate / establish / significant / approach / indicate / assume / policy / concept / require / consist]*

**1.** The study _____ a clear link between dietary habits and cognitive function. → **demonstrated**
**2.** It would be wrong to _____ that all students learn at the same rate. → **assume**
**3.** The new government _____ on renewable energy investment has attracted international praise. → **policy**
**4.** There was a _____ increase in applications following the announcement. → **significant**
**5.** The results _____ that further research into this area is urgently needed. → **indicate**
**6.** The framework _____ of three interconnected components. → **consists**
**7.** Researchers have attempted to _____ a causal link rather than a mere correlation. → **establish**
**8.** The data _____ analysis using both quantitative and qualitative methods. → **requires**
**9.** The _____ of cognitive bias has attracted growing attention in behavioural economics. → **concept**
**10.** The most effective _____ to reducing inequality involves both policy reform and cultural change. → **approach**

## PHẦN 3: VIẾT LUẬN IELTS (30 điểm)

**Đề bài:**
*"Some people argue that technological innovation always leads to social progress. Others believe technology creates as many problems as it solves. Discuss both views and give your opinion."*

---

### Bài luận mẫu — Band 7–8 (~270 từ):

Technology's role in shaping society is one of the defining debates of the modern era. While many contend that innovation is inherently progressive, others argue that it introduces new problems alongside the ones it solves. In my view, technology is neither uniformly beneficial nor harmful; its impact depends critically on how it is governed and distributed.

Those who regard technological innovation as a force for social progress point to compelling historical evidence. The development of vaccines and antibiotics dramatically reduced mortality from infectious disease, while the internet has democratised access to information in ways that were unimaginable a generation ago. Medical imaging technology, clean energy solutions, and digital financial services have collectively improved living standards for hundreds of millions of people. From this perspective, opposing innovation risks condemning future generations to preventable suffering.

However, the counterargument carries considerable weight. The same digital connectivity that enables education and free expression also facilitates mass surveillance, cybercrime, and the rapid dissemination of misinformation. The automation of manufacturing has displaced vast numbers of workers, concentrating wealth among technology owners while impoverishing many communities. Social media, designed to connect people, has simultaneously been linked to rising rates of anxiety and political polarisation. These are not incidental side effects; they are structural consequences of technologies developed with insufficient social foresight.

On balance, I believe technology creates the conditions for both progress and harm, and that the determining factor is not the innovation itself but the regulatory frameworks and ethical priorities that govern its deployment. Uncritical optimism about technology is as dangerous as reactionary resistance to it.
