### Section 1: INVERSION (10 điểm — 5 items × 2 điểm)

**Hướng dẫn:** Viết lại câu sử dụng cấu trúc đảo ngữ (inversion) với cụm từ đã cho. Giữ nguyên nghĩa gốc của câu.

---

**1.** *"I had never seen such a breathtaking natural landscape before."*

→ **Never** _______________

---

**2.** *"She had only just arrived at the conference when the opening ceremony began."*

→ **Only just** _______________

---

**3.** *"He not only improved his writing skills significantly but also developed greater confidence in public speaking."*

→ **Not only** _______________

---

**4.** *"We will under no circumstances accept a proposal that compromises the safety of local residents."*

→ **Under no circumstances** _______________

---

**5.** *"She rarely makes grammatical errors in her academic writing despite English being her second language."*

→ **Rarely** _______________

---

### Section 2: CLEFT SENTENCES (10 điểm — 5 items × 2 điểm)

**Hướng dẫn:** Viết lại câu sử dụng cấu trúc cleft sentence theo dạng gợi ý trong ngoặc vuông. Không thay đổi nghĩa của câu.

---

**1.** *"Underfunding caused the healthcare project to fail in its first year."*

→ [It was...] _______________

---

**2.** *"She admires his unwavering commitment to social justice most of all."*

→ [What she admires...] _______________

---

**3.** *"They blamed the senior manager for the financial losses incurred during the restructuring."*

→ [It was the senior manager...] _______________

---

**4.** *"His meticulous attention to detail makes him an invaluable member of the research team."*

→ [What makes him...] _______________

---

**5.** *"The lack of transparent communication between departments led to the fundamental misunderstanding."*

→ [It was the lack of...] _______________

---

### Section 3: PARTICIPLE CLAUSES (10 điểm — 5 items × 2 điểm)

**Hướng dẫn:** Rút gọn câu bằng cách sử dụng participle clause (mệnh đề phân từ). Không thay đổi nghĩa của câu.

---

**1.** *"After she had completed the field research, she began writing the final report."*

→ _______________

---

**2.** *"Because he was exhausted from the lengthy conference proceedings, he made several significant errors in the summary."*

→ _______________

---

**3.** *"While they were walking through the research site, they noticed something unusual about the water samples."*

→ _______________

---

**4.** *"When the long-term experiment had been completed, the results were carefully analysed by the team."*

→ _______________

---

**5.** *"As she had not received a satisfactory reply to her initial inquiry, she decided to send a formal follow-up email."*

→ _______________

### Section 4: SUBJUNCTIVE MOOD (10 điểm — 5 items × 2 điểm)

**Hướng dẫn:** Viết lại câu sử dụng cấu trúc subjunctive (cầu khẩn pháp) với cụm từ gợi ý. Chú ý: cầu khẩn pháp yêu cầu base form của động từ sau *that*.

---

**1.** *"You must submit the application form by the deadline on Monday."*

→ **It is essential that** _______________

---

**2.** *"Everyone should receive equal and fair treatment under the law."*

→ **It is imperative that** _______________

---

**3.** *"He should step down from his position immediately."*

→ **The board of directors recommends that** _______________

---

**4.** *"The data must be independently verified before the report can be published."*

→ **It is necessary that** _______________

---

**5.** *"A comprehensive new policy on data privacy should be introduced without further delay."*

### Section 6: ADVANCED PASSIVE — Impersonal Passive (10 điểm — 5 items × 2 điểm)

**Hướng dẫn:** Viết lại câu sử dụng **impersonal passive** theo dạng gợi ý:
- **Type A:** *It + passive verb + that-clause* (e.g. *It is believed that...*)
- **Type B:** *Subject + passive verb + to-infinitive* (e.g. *The trend is believed to...*)

---

**1.** *"Experts believe that the current economic trend will continue for at least another decade."*

→ **(Type A: It...)** _______________

→ **(Type B: The trend...)** _______________

---

**2.** *"Researchers have found that regular meditation significantly reduces measurable stress levels."*

→ **(Type A: It...)** _______________

---

**3.** *"People generally consider her to be the most qualified candidate for the senior position."*

→ **(Type B: She...)** _______________

---

**4.** *"They reported at the time that the company had been systematically mismanaging its financial reserves."*

→ **(Type A: It...)** _______________

---

**5.** *"Most scientists now think that the observable universe is approximately 13.8 billion years old."*

→ **(Type B: The universe...)** _______________

### Section 7: AWL WORDS — Correct Form (10 điểm — 10 items × 1 điểm)

**Hướng dẫn:** Sử dụng dạng từ đúng của AWL word trong ngoặc để điền vào chỗ trống.

---

**2.** This approach has been _______ (establish) as the most cost-effective method of delivering community health services.

**3.** The long-term consequences of accelerating climate change are _______ (consider) to be severe by the majority of climate scientists.

**4.** Several _______ (distinct) advantages have been identified in the proposed reform of the current taxation framework.

**5.** It is vital that all _______ (relevance) factors be systematically taken into account during the policy evaluation process.

**6.** The primary aim of the research is to _______ (identification) the root causes of persistent intergenerational poverty.

**7.** These findings are broadly _______ (consistency) with the results reported in previous peer-reviewed studies on the topic.

**8.** The implementation of digital platforms is expected to _______ (facilitation) greater and more equitable access to educational resources.

**9.** A _______ (significance) increase in youth unemployment was observed across all surveyed regions during the study period.

**10.** The proposed legislation seeks to _______ (address) several key structural issues within the current immigration framework.

## PHẦN C — ĐỌC HIỂU (15 điểm)

---

### Đoạn đọc (~350 từ):

**THE KNOWLEDGE ECONOMY: PROMISE AND CHALLENGE**

The emergence of the knowledge economy represents a fundamental transformation in the drivers of economic growth and prosperity. Unlike the industrial economy of the twentieth century, which **derived** its primary value from the physical production of goods through manufacturing and extraction, the knowledge economy is **predicated** upon the generation, **dissemination**, and application of information, ideas, and innovation. Nations that lead in research, technology, and skilled human capital are increasingly positioned to capture the greatest share of global economic value.

The transition to a knowledge-based economic model has profound implications for national educational systems. Countries that have successfully **navigated** this shift — notably Singapore, South Korea, and several Nordic states — have done so by making sustained and strategically directed investment in education, research infrastructure, and the capacity to translate academic knowledge into commercial and social innovation. In such **contexts**, universities function not merely as institutions of learning but as engines of economic development and drivers of technological progress.

For developing nations, however, the knowledge economy presents a more **ambivalent** picture. While digital connectivity and the global spread of technology have nominally lowered barriers to knowledge access, structural inequalities in educational quality, research funding, and intellectual property frameworks mean that many lower-income countries remain **net consumers** rather than producers of globally valued knowledge. This dynamic risks deepening existing economic disparities between the developed and developing world rather than bridging them.

Addressing this challenge requires targeted policy intervention at both the national and international levels. At the national level, developing nations must **prioritise** investment in high-quality tertiary education and research capacity, rather than allocating resources exclusively to primary and secondary schooling. At the international level, wealthy nations and multilateral institutions bear a responsibility to facilitate equitable knowledge transfer through more generous intellectual property concessions, research partnerships, and the funding of open-access educational resources. Without such concerted action, the knowledge economy is likely to consolidate existing hierarchies of global power rather than create new pathways for inclusive development.
