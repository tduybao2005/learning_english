**Model paragraph demonstrating nuanced vocabulary (Band 7–8):**

*"Those who advocate for urban childhoods contend that cities offer unparalleled access to diverse educational opportunities and cultural experiences that smaller communities simply cannot replicate. This exposure cultivates adaptable, globally-minded individuals who are better equipped for the demands of a competitive modern economy. However, critics of this view argue that the relentless pace of urban life can be deeply detrimental to children's emotional wellbeing, fostering anxiety rather than the sense of security that a close-knit rural community is uniquely positioned to provide. The incessant stimulation of city environments — noise, congestion, and social fragmentation — may undermine the very foundations of healthy psychological development."*

**Annotation of nuanced choices in the model:**
- *advocate for* (formal; more precise than "support")
- *contend* (academic; more precise than "say" or "think")
- *unparalleled* (strong positive connotation)
- *replicate* (formal register)
- *cultivates* (positive connotation — growth/development metaphor)
- *detrimental* (formal, strong negative connotation)
- *fostering anxiety* (negative connotation — contrast with "cultivates")
- *close-knit* (positive connotation for rural community)
- *incessant* (strong negative connotation — intensity)
- *undermine* (formal, negative connotation)

## ANSWER KEY — Sections A–D

### Section A Answers:

1. **b) tenacious** — positive connotation; appropriate for formal praise. *Stubborn* and *pigheaded* both carry negative connotations of unreasonable inflexibility.

2. **c) stingy** — negative connotation; appropriate for a complaint. *Economical* and *frugal* both imply sensible money management and would seem complimentary, not critical.

3. **b) inquisitive** — positive connotation; appropriate for self-promotion. *Nosy* and *prying* both imply unwanted intrusion into others' affairs.

4. **a) verbose** — negative connotation; appropriate for criticism. *Articulate* and *eloquent* are highly positive and would undermine the critical tone.

5. **b) self-assured** — positive/neutral; appropriate for a positive report. *Arrogant* and *conceited* both imply unpleasant overconfidence.

6. **c) meddlesome** — negative connotation; appropriate for a critical editorial. *Methodical* is positive/neutral; *cautious* is neutral to positive.

7. **b) single-minded** — positive in context of admiration. *Obsessive* and *fixated* both carry clinical or negative connotations of unhealthy preoccupation.

8. **c) delusional** — most precisely captures a concern about inability to distinguish fantasy from reality. *Imaginative* and *inventive* are positive attributes.

9. **b) traditional** — neutral, academic-appropriate term. *Backward* and *primitive* are considered ethnocentric and inappropriate in academic writing.

10. **b) cost-effective** — positive formal register; appropriate for positive reporting. *Cheap* has a negative connotation (inferior quality); *budget-cutting* is more neutral but less positive.

---

### Section B Answers:

1. **b) demonstrated** — academic standard; *showed* is acceptable but less formal; *proved* is too absolute for a correlation study.
2. **b) discussed** — formal; *talked about* is informal; *chatted about* is very informal.
3. **c) results in** — academic, precise causal language; *causes* is slightly more direct/informal; *leads to* is also acceptable.
4. **b) bought** — natural informal register; *purchased* is overly formal for conversation; *acquired* is unusual in informal speech.
5. **c) good** — most natural in informal speech; *productive* and *fruitful* are both more formal.
6. **b) help** — most natural informal register; *assist* and *aid* are more formal.
7. **b) illustrates** — IELTS standard verb for describing charts/graphs; *shows* is acceptable but slightly less academic.
8. **b) suggests** — appropriate academic hedging; *means* is too direct; *implies* carries a slightly different meaning (indirect inference).
9. **b) fluctuated** — precise academic vocabulary for irregular movement; *changed* is vague; *went up and down* is too informal.
10. **b) argue** — formal, appropriate for IELTS; *say* is too informal; *talk* is grammatically incorrect in this structure.

---

### Section C Answers:

**Word Set 1 (Surprise):** surprised → astonished → flabbergasted → stupefied
- Context A: **surprised** (mild, expected-slightly-exceeded)
- Context B: **astonished** (moderate, unexpected promotion)
- Context C: **flabbergasted** (extreme, lottery win)

**Word Set 2 (Dislike):** dislike → disapprove of → detest → loathe → abhor

**Word Set 3 (Fear) Classification:**
- worried: mild | anxious: mild-moderate | alarmed: moderate-strong | terrified: strong | petrified: extreme
- Context A: **alarmed** (academic, factual tone; conveys serious concern without melodrama)
- Context B: **terrified** (strong, appropriate for a child's emotional response)
- Context C: **alarmed** (journalistic; conveys official concern, prompting action)

---

### Section D Answers:

1. "The government's plan was **cost-ineffective** and would fail to achieve its stated objectives." *(or: 'fiscally irresponsible', 'fundamentally flawed')*

2. "The police **apprehended** the suspect near the scene of the incident."

3. "The researcher's central claims were **unfounded / unsubstantiated** and lacked the requisite supporting evidence."

4. "She was **resolute / tenacious / unwavering** in her pursuit of justice, refusing to concede despite intense opposition."

5. "Your service was **wholly unsatisfactory / deeply inadequate**, and I would request a full refund at your earliest convenience."

6. "The building was **decrepit and foreboding**, and no one had set foot inside for decades."

7. "Pollution is a **pressing challenge / pervasive issue** that **poses significant risks to / affects adversely** nations across the globe."

8. "I **would like to enquire about / am writing to request information regarding** the current status of my application."
