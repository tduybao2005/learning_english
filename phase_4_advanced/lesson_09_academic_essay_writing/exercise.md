## SECTION E: FULL ESSAY WRITING TASK (50 điểm)

### Đề bài:

> **"The development of artificial intelligence will create more problems than it solves. To what extent do you agree or disagree?"**

**Yêu cầu:**
- Viết 250–280 từ
- Cấu trúc rõ ràng: Introduction + 2 Body Paragraphs + Conclusion
- Dùng ít nhất 3 academic phrases từ vocabulary list
- Dùng PEEL structure cho body paragraphs
- Formal register (không dùng contractions, từ lóng)
- Có hedging phù hợp

## PHẦN G: LISTENING-STYLE DICTATION / GAP-FILL (10 câu)

*Đọc đoạn văn và điền từ còn thiếu dựa vào ngữ cảnh (không cần audio).*

Writing an effective academic essay demands a clear understanding of both structure and argumentation. The (G1) ______ serves as the essay's foundation, establishing context and signalling the writer's central position on the issue. A strong (G2) ______ statement does more than simply announce a topic; it advances a precise, arguable claim that the body paragraphs will subsequently develop with evidence and analysis. Each body paragraph should open with a clear (G3) ______ sentence that introduces the paragraph's central argument, followed by concrete (G4) ______ in the form of data, research, or specific examples. The writer must then provide a detailed (G5) ______ of how this evidence supports the broader thesis, avoiding the common error of allowing evidence to speak for itself without analytical commentary. The paragraph concludes with a (G6) ______ sentence that reinforces the argument and transitions toward the next point. A well-crafted (G7) ______ does not merely summarise the essay's content; it restates the thesis in fresh language and offers a broader (G8) ______ that reflects the significance of the argument in a wider context. Throughout the essay, the writer should employ appropriate (G9) ______ language — such as "it may be argued" or "evidence suggests" — to avoid overstating claims. Finally, maintaining a consistently (G10) ______ register, free from contractions and colloquialisms, is essential for achieving the highest band scores in IELTS Academic Writing.

## PHẦN H: SPEAKING / DISCUSSION PROMPT

*Trả lời miệng hoặc viết câu trả lời, dùng cấu trúc/từ vựng của bài.*

1. What do you consider to be the most common structural weaknesses in IELTS Writing Task 2 essays at the Band 5–6 level?
2. Why is it important for a thesis statement to be arguable rather than simply a statement of fact?
3. How does applying the PEEL structure consistently help learners develop more disciplined and analytical academic writing?
4. In what ways does appropriate hedging language contribute to the academic credibility and persuasiveness of an essay?

**Model answer (Band 7–8):** *The most fundamental distinction between Band 6 and Band 7 academic essays lies not in the complexity of ideas but in the precision and discipline of their organisation. A compelling thesis statement establishes an arguable, specific position that guides every subsequent paragraph; without it, even well-researched body paragraphs tend to feel disconnected and purposeless. Moreover, applying the PEEL structure consistently ensures that evidence is never presented in isolation but is always accompanied by the analytical commentary that transforms raw information into persuasive argument. In essence, academic essay writing rewards not those who think the most, but those who structure their thinking most rigorously.*

### SECTION C — Mẫu đáp án cho Item 1:

**Weak paragraph (gốc):**
> *"Public transport is good for the environment. Fewer cars means less pollution."*

**PEEL paragraph mẫu (Band 7):**

> **[P]** Expanding and improving public transport infrastructure represents one of the most effective strategies available to municipal governments seeking to reduce urban carbon emissions.
> **[E]** According to a 2021 report by the International Energy Agency, cities with well-developed metro and bus networks generate, on average, 45% less transport-related carbon dioxide per capita than those heavily reliant on private vehicle use.
> **[E — Explain]** This reduction occurs because a single bus or train can displace dozens or even hundreds of individual car journeys, dramatically concentrating passenger movement and reducing the total energy expended per person transported.
> **[L]** The environmental case for public transport investment is therefore compelling, and governments that prioritise road construction over transit development do so at a measurable ecological cost.

---

### SECTION D — Mẫu đáp án cho Introduction 1:

**Vấn đề:**
1. Hook yếu — "Nowadays, many people think" là cụm cực kỳ cũ và thiếu học thuật
2. Background quá đơn giản — chỉ mô tả sự bất đồng chứ không contextualize vấn đề
3. Không có thesis rõ lập trường — "will discuss both sides" không cho biết quan điểm của người viết

**Mở bài viết lại (Band 7):**
> *The use of animals in biomedical research has long provoked fierce ethical debate, pitting scientific necessity against fundamental questions of animal welfare. As advances in computational modelling and in vitro testing increasingly offer viable alternatives to animal experimentation, pressure on governments to enact more stringent regulations has intensified. This essay argues that while animal testing has historically contributed to significant medical breakthroughs, the scientific community's growing capacity for non-animal research methodologies makes a continued, unregulated reliance on live animal subjects ethically unjustifiable.*

### SECTION E — Bài luận mẫu (Band 7 — ~270 từ):

> **Introduction:**
> Artificial intelligence has emerged as the defining technological development of the early twenty-first century, with applications spanning medicine, finance, education, and industrial manufacturing. While optimists herald AI as a transformative force for human progress, growing concerns persist regarding its potential to displace workers, perpetuate bias, and erode privacy. This essay argues that, on balance, the risks associated with artificial intelligence are likely to outweigh its benefits unless accompanied by robust governance frameworks.

> **Body Paragraph 1:**
> The most pressing concern is the systemic displacement of employment across multiple sectors. Research by the McKinsey Global Institute (2020) estimated that up to 375 million workers globally may need to transition to new occupational categories by 2030 as a result of automation. This disruption is disproportionately likely to affect lower-skilled and lower-income workers, who lack the resources to reskill, thereby exacerbating existing socioeconomic inequalities. The economic dislocation caused by AI-driven automation thus has the potential to generate significant social instability if left unaddressed by policy.

> **Body Paragraph 2:**
> Proponents of AI contend that the technology will simultaneously create new industries and roles, as occurred during previous industrial revolutions. Admittedly, this historical precedent carries some weight; however, the speed and breadth of AI's reach suggest that job creation may not keep pace with displacement, nor will new roles necessarily be accessible to those affected. Furthermore, the ethical concerns surrounding algorithmic bias in criminal justice and hiring decisions represent systemic risks that compound the employment argument.

> **Conclusion:**
> In light of this evidence, it can be concluded that artificial intelligence, while possessing genuine transformative potential, is more likely to create problems than solutions in the absence of comprehensive regulatory oversight. Governments must act urgently to establish ethical frameworks that govern AI development and ensure that its benefits are equitably distributed.

---

**Annotation — Điểm mạnh của bài mẫu:**
- ✅ Hook học thuật và cụ thể
- ✅ Thesis rõ lập trường với điều kiện ("unless accompanied by...")
- ✅ PEEL đầy đủ trong cả 2 body paragraphs
- ✅ Evidence cụ thể (McKinsey, 2020)
- ✅ Hedging phù hợp (*likely to, may need to, suggests*)
- ✅ Nhượng bộ + phản bác trong Body 2
- ✅ Kết bài có broader implication và call to action

**Lỗi thường gặp cần tránh:**
- ❌ Không có evidence — "AI is dangerous" mà không có số liệu
- ❌ Không hedge — "AI will destroy all jobs" thay vì "may displace"
- ❌ Kết bài có ý mới — chỉ restate và summarize
- ❌ Dùng "I think" quá nhiều — thay bằng "It is argued that / Evidence suggests"
